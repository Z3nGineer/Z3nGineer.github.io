Echoing Strike Warlock

Warlock · Season 13: Reign of the Warlock · #1 Ranked Build · Magic Damage
#1 Build S-Tier New Class Magic Damage FCR Scaling

Overview

The Warlock is the first new class added to Diablo 2: Resurrected, arriving in Season 13 — Reign of the Warlock. An arcane-melee hybrid that conjures astral weapon projections and curses, the Warlock completely reshapes the D2R meta. Its signature skill, Echoing Strike, creates ghostly copies of your weapon swing that boomerang outward through enemies and return for a second hit, dealing pure magic damage.

This build sits at #1 for one massive reason: almost nothing in the game is immune to magic damage. While Hammerdins deal magic damage too, the Warlock does it with better clear speed, built-in mobility via Blade Warp, and a curse that strips the few remaining resistances. You never need Infinity. You never need to skip packs. You just kill everything.

Why Magic Damage is King

In Hell difficulty, most monsters have immunity to at least one element — Fire, Cold, Lightning, or Poison. But magic damage immunity is extremely rare. Only a tiny handful of monsters in the entire game resist magic, and the Warlock's Hex: Purge curse can break even those. This means zero skipping, zero immunity headaches, and full clear potential in every area of the game.

Warlock Class Identity

  • Archetype Arcane-Melee Hybrid — scales with FCR, not IAS. Plays like a caster in melee range.
  • Primary Echoing Strike — astral projections boomerang through packs, piercing and returning
  • Mobility Blade Warp — pseudo-teleport: warp to target location, damaging enemies in the path
  • Curse Hex: Purge — strips enemy resistances, breaks the rare magic immunes
  • Buff Mirrored Blades — multiplies projections, massive damage scaling synergy
  • Damage Magic — almost zero immunes in the entire game. Superior to elemental builds.
Ladder Starter Friendly

Unlike Lightning Sorc (needs Infinity) or Hammerdin (needs Enigma for true endgame), the Echoing Strike Warlock functions on budget gear from day one. The new Authority runeword (leveling weapon) and Spirit shield carry you through Hell. This is the best ladder starter in Season 13.

Max Block Variant

Some players opt for max block (75%) using Stormshield or the new Void runeword. This requires ~130 Dexterity depending on level, which costs ~100 Vitality points. The tradeoff is debatable — you gain physical damage reduction but lose ~300 HP. For hardcore, max block is standard. For softcore, the Vitality dump is generally better.

The Immunity Advantage

While Blizzard Sorcs skip Cold Immunes and Hammerdins struggle with tight corridors, the Echoing Strike Warlock clears every single area in Hell at full speed. This makes it the most versatile MF build and the most efficient ladder farmer in Season 13.

Magic Skill Damage is Multiplicative

The "% Magic Skill Damage" modifier on Void, Ritual, and Coven is a separate multiplier that applies after +skills, synergies, and other bonuses. With Void (15%) + Ritual (10%) + Coven (10%), you get a total 1.35x damage multiplier on top of everything else. This is why the Warlock's ceiling is so much higher than Hammerdin despite dealing the same damage type.

FCR Breakpoints

Echoing Strike scales with Faster Cast Rate, not Increased Attack Speed. The Warlock has unique breakpoints similar to the Sorceress. Hitting the right breakpoint is critical for clear speed.

Target: 105% FCR

The 105% breakpoint (9 frames) is the sweet spot for endgame. With Hoto (40) + Void (40) + Arachnid (20) + Magefist (20) = 120% FCR, you comfortably exceed 105% with room to swap gear. This gives you 2.77 casts per second, which translates to 2.77 Echoing Strikes launching projections into packs every second. The difference between 10 and 9 frames is enormous for clear speed.

Insight over Infinity

Most caster builds eventually upgrade their merc weapon to Infinity for the Conviction aura. The Warlock does not need Infinity. Magic damage doesn't benefit from -enemy resistance from Conviction (it only reduces Fire/Cold/Lightning resist). Keep Insight for the mana sustain — it's better for this build specifically.

Playstyle — Combat Rotation

The Echoing Strike Warlock plays like a mid-range caster with teleport-like mobility. Here's the optimal combat flow.

  1. Blade Warp into pack density
    Use Blade Warp to teleport into the center of a pack. The warp itself deals damage along the path. Position yourself so Echoing Strike projections will travel through the maximum number of enemies.
  2. Cast Hex: Purge on dangerous targets
    Pre-curse bosses, champions, and unique monsters. The -100% magic resistance at max level makes even resistant targets melt. For normal packs, skip this step — they die too fast to bother cursing.
  3. Spam Echoing Strike
    Hold down Echoing Strike and aim through pack centers. Each cast sends astral projections outward that pierce through enemies, then boomerang back through them for a second hit. With Mirrored Blades maxed, you launch 4+ projections per cast. At the 105% FCR breakpoint, you're launching ~11 projections per second.
  4. Reposition with Blade Warp
    If enemies close in or you need to dodge a dangerous attack, Blade Warp out. With Enigma, you can also use Teleport for faster repositioning in open areas. Blade Warp is better in tight corridors because it damages along the path.
  5. Use Hex: Wither for crowd control
    Against extra-fast or extra-strong packs, cast Hex: Wither to slow them. Combined with your merc's Holy Freeze, enemies become nearly stationary. This is your "oh no" button for dangerous packs in hardcore.
  6. Dark Pact for emergency mana
    If you run dry on mana (rare with Insight merc), Dark Pact converts life to mana. Use sparingly — it's a panic button, not a sustain tool. Pop a rejuv immediately after if low on life.
Clear Speed Context

An optimally geared Echoing Strike Warlock clears Chaos Sanctuary in under 2 minutes solo, comparable to a fully geared Hammerdin. The key difference is that the Warlock never has to path around magic-immune monsters (there are essentially none), making runs more consistent. Cow Level runs are even faster due to the open layout and dense packs.

Farm Rotation Suggestion

High efficiency loop: Countess → Pit → Travincal → Chaos Sanctuary → Baal → Cows. This loop hits every major drop table in the game, covers all rune tiers, and takes roughly 12-15 minutes per cycle with endgame gear. No other build can run this full rotation without skipping areas.

Season 13 Tips

The Warlock is NOT a Melee Class

Despite swinging a weapon, the Warlock functions as a caster. Echoing Strike's damage comes from the astral projections, not the physical hit. The projections scale with +skills, FCR, and synergies — not weapon damage, IAS, or physical stats. Stack caster gear, not melee gear. Spirit/Hoto outperforms any physical weapon by miles.

Hex: Purge vs. Lower Resist

Hex: Purge reduces magic resistance specifically, which is different from the Necromancer's Lower Resist curse. They can stack in party play. If you're in a group with a Necromancer, coordinate curses for maximum resistance reduction on bosses. Your Purge handles magic immunes; their Lower Resist handles elemental immunes for the party's other damage dealers.

Blade Warp Has No Cooldown

Unlike Teleport (which has a natural delay between casts for non-Sorceress classes), Blade Warp is instant cast with no cooldown. You can spam it for repositioning just like a Sorceress with Teleport. However, it has a shorter range (~2/3 of Teleport distance). For long-distance travel across open maps, Enigma's Teleport is superior. Use Blade Warp for combat repositioning and Teleport for map traversal.

Warlock Cannot Use All Weapon Types

The Warlock class can only equip Scepters, Maces, Swords, and Daggers. Axes, Polearms, Spears, Bows, and Claws are NOT equippable. This doesn't matter much since Hoto (Flail = Mace) and Spirit (Crystal Sword) are both usable, but be aware when shopping for weapons. Don't accidentally make a runeword in a weapon type the Warlock can't use.

Spectral Shield Stacks with Other DR

The Warlock's Spectral Shield provides magic damage absorb that stacks with physical damage reduction from gear (Shako's 10% DR, Void's 10% DR, etc.). At level 10, Spectral Shield absorbs ~15% of incoming magic damage. Combined with Shako + Void, you have 35% DR against physical and 15% against magic. This makes the Warlock surprisingly tanky for a "caster" build.

Charm Priority

Warlock-specific Hellfire Torches exist in Season 13. A Warlock Torch with +3 skills / +20 resist / +20 attributes is extremely valuable early in the season. Anni + Torch first, then Astral Blades skillers with +life. Small charms should be 20 life / 5 resist or 20 life / 5% FHR if you need the FHR breakpoint.

Hardcore Considerations

For hardcore, swap to the max block variant with Stormshield (or Void if you can hit 75% block with Dex investment). Add a Call to Arms on weapon switch for Battle Orders (+life). Consider Holy Freeze merc (standard) and keep Hex: Wither on your right-click for emergency crowd control. The Warlock is one of the safest HC classes thanks to Blade Warp's instant repositioning and Spectral Shield's damage absorb.

Party Play Value

The Warlock is a top-tier party member. Hex: Purge benefits the entire party by stripping magic resistance from enemies. In a Baal run group, the Warlock's curse makes the Hammerdin's Blessed Hammer deal significantly more damage to high-resist targets. You're not just a DPS — you're a force multiplier for the party's other magic damage dealers.

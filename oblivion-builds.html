Top 10 Builds
Oblivion Remastered

From game-breaking exploits to perfectly optimized warriors, these are the strongest character builds for every playstyle in the remastered Cyrodiil.

Key Mechanics

Oblivion Remastered changes several core systems. Understand these before building.

Virtue Point Leveling

You earn 12 Virtue Points per level. You can place a maximum of 5 points into any single attribute and must spread across exactly 3 attributes. This replaces the old +5 modifier grind entirely.

Retroactive Health

Endurance now retroactively grants health. You no longer need to rush Endurance early. Raise it whenever you want and your HP will recalculate.

Willpower = Magicka Regen

Willpower governs magicka regeneration with quadratic scaling. High Willpower builds regenerate magicka dramatically faster than low ones. Essential for any caster.

Agility Governs Daggers

Daggers and shortswords now scale with Agility, not Strength. This makes Agility a combat stat for stealth builds and changes how you allocate Virtue Points.

Fatigue Reworked

Fatigue no longer affects melee damage. It is consumed by sprinting and still affects stagger, but the old "always keep fatigue full" rule is gone.

Out-of-Combat Regen

Health regenerates passively when out of combat. This reduces potion dependency but doesn't help during fights. Restoration is still king for sustained combat healing.

Magic XP Scales with Cost

Spell experience is based on mana cost, not cast count. Expensive spells train skills faster. This means efficient leveling requires casting your highest-tier spells.

Arrows Always Recoverable

Arrows can always be recovered from targets and surfaces. This makes Marksman builds far more economical and eliminates the need to hoard arrows.

Heavy Armor + Conjuration

Wearing Heavy Armor reduces Conjuration spell duration. Summoner builds should use Light Armor or robes to get full uptime on their summons.

The Lord Birthsign Bug

The Lord's Shield ability causes accelerated armor deterioration. Your armor breaks much faster than normal. Avoid this birthsign unless you keep Armorer high.

Block Expert = Shield Bash

Reaching Expert level in Block unlocks Shield Bash, a powerful stagger move. Warrior builds should prioritize Block to Expert for this combat tool.

Sprint Uses Fatigue

Sprinting drains Fatigue. Athletics and higher Endurance help offset this. Plan around it for builds that need to close distance quickly in heavy armor.

#1
The Immortal Absorber
Atronach Exploit Build -- Magic Specialization
S-Tier Exploit Magic Endgame God
Race & Setup
  • RaceHigh Elf or Breton
  • BirthsignThe Atronach
  • SpecMagic
  • FavoredIntelligence, Willpower
Major Skills
Alchemy Alteration Conjuration Destruction Heavy Armor Mysticism Restoration
Playstyle

The single most powerful build in the game. The Atronach birthsign gives you 50% Spell Absorption and Stunted Magicka (no natural regen). The trick: cast Telekinesis on any object, then spam low-cost spells into it. Each absorbed spell refunds magicka, creating an infinite magicka loop. You never run dry.

At endgame, you stack enchanted gear to hit 100% Spell Absorption (immune to all magic) and 100% Reflect Damage (enemies kill themselves hitting you). Nothing in the game can threaten you. Physical attacks bounce back, magic gets absorbed as free mana. You are literally immortal.

Why It's Powerful

Unlimited magicka means unlimited Destruction spells, unlimited healing, unlimited summons. The 100% Spell Absorption makes you immune to every magical effect in the game, including dragon fire breath and Daedric prince attacks. Combined with 100% Reflect Damage, enemies at max difficulty die from their own melee swings while your health never drops.

Endgame Gear
ArmorMadness Armor -- enchanted with Spell Absorption sigil stones
ShieldEscutcheon of Chorrol -- Reflect Damage + Reflect Spell
AmuletAmulet of Axes -- Reflect Damage
RingRing of Iron Fist -- Reflect Damage (hit 100% total)
Leveling Strategy
  1. Levels 1-5: Spam cheap Mysticism spells (Minor Life Detection) to level Mysticism. Buy Telekinesis ASAP. Use Alchemy for early income and stat boosts.
  2. Levels 6-12: Establish the Telekinesis + Spell Absorption loop for infinite magicka. Prioritize Intelligence and Willpower every level. Begin Conjuration training for summons.
  3. Levels 13-20: Push Destruction to Expert for area spells. Start collecting Spell Absorption sigil stones from Oblivion gates. Enchant each Madness armor piece.
  4. Levels 21+: Acquire Escutcheon of Chorrol, Amulet of Axes, Ring of Iron Fist. Reach 100% Reflect Damage and 100% Spell Absorption. You are now unkillable.
Remastered Tips
Remastered Note

With retroactive Endurance health, you don't need to rush Endurance early. Focus entirely on Intelligence and Willpower for the first 10 levels. The Telekinesis loop still works in Remastered. Willpower's quadratic magicka regen scaling doesn't matter for this build since you use Spell Absorption for magicka, but it's a nice backup if absorption misses.

#2
The Ghost
100% Chameleon -- Stealth Specialization
S-Tier Exploit Stealth Permanently Invisible
Race & Setup
  • RaceBreton
  • BirthsignThe Mage
  • SpecStealth
  • FavoredIntelligence, Agility
Major Skills
Alchemy Blade Conjuration Destruction Illusion Light Armor Sneak
Playstyle

Enchant 5 pieces of gear with 20% Chameleon each to reach 100%. At 100% Chameleon, you are permanently undetectable -- you can walk up to any NPC, stand in front of them, and they will never see you. Unlike Invisibility, Chameleon does not break when you attack, open containers, or interact with objects.

Every melee hit is a sneak attack. Daggers get the 8x sneak attack multiplier, turning every swing into massive damage. You can clear entire dungeons without ever being detected, pickpocket anyone, and steal anything you want.

Why It's Powerful

Total immunity to detection means zero risk in any encounter. 8x sneak attack damage with daggers one-shots most enemies even at max difficulty. You can complete every quest, every guild, every Daedric quest without ever entering real combat. Conjuration lets you summon creatures to tank while you backstab from stealth.

Endgame Gear
All Slots5 pieces with 20% Chameleon -- enchant via Grand Soul Gems + Sigil Stones
WeaponBlade of Woe / Mehrunes' Razor -- high base damage for sneak multiplier
Leveling Strategy
  1. Levels 1-5: Level Sneak naturally through the tutorial and early quests. Join the Dark Brotherhood and Thieves Guild for access to stealth gear. Collect Grand Soul Gems.
  2. Levels 6-12: Seek out Chameleon enchantments. Sigil stones from Oblivion gates offer 20% Chameleon -- you need 5. Close Oblivion gates and enchant gear pieces one by one.
  3. Levels 13-15: Once you hit 100% Chameleon, the game is effectively broken. Focus on Blade and Alchemy for poison crafting. Every fight is trivial from here.
  4. Levels 16+: Push Destruction for ranged AoE when you want to speed-clear. Level Conjuration for summoned distractions. Complete quest lines at your leisure -- nothing can stop you.
Remastered Tips
Remastered Note

Agility now governs dagger damage in Remastered, making it a must-pick attribute for this build. Prioritize Agility alongside Intelligence every level. The 8x sneak attack multiplier with daggers scales with Agility, so your sneak attacks grow significantly stronger. Chameleon enchanting works identically to the original game.

#3
Conjuration Overlord
Summoner -- Magic Specialization
A-Tier Magic Summoner Difficulty-Proof
Race & Setup
  • RaceBreton
  • BirthsignThe Atronach
  • SpecMagic
  • FavoredIntelligence, Endurance
Major Skills
Alchemy Conjuration Destruction Illusion Marksman Restoration Security
Playstyle

The secret of this build: summoned creatures don't scale with difficulty. On maximum difficulty, where enemies have 5x health and deal 6x damage, your summons still fight at their base stats. This means a summoned Xivilai or Storm Atronach tears through enemies while you sit back safely.

You summon tanky creatures, buff them with Illusion (Courage/Rally), debuff enemies with Destruction weakness spells, and finish with ranged attacks from Marksman. Restoration keeps you alive, Alchemy funds everything, and Security opens every chest. A complete, self-sufficient build.

Why It's Powerful

Summons bypass Oblivion's brutal difficulty scaling. While your weapons deal 1/6 damage on max difficulty, summons deal full damage. You are essentially playing with AI companions that do the heavy lifting. Late-game Master Conjuration summons are nearly unkillable and deal tremendous damage.

Endgame Gear
RobeSpelldrinker Amulet -- Absorb + Fortify Magicka
RingMundane Ring -- Fortify Intelligence
BowHatreds Soul Bow -- ranged support while summons tank
ArmorLight Armor / Robes -- avoid Heavy Armor (reduces Conjuration duration)
Leveling Strategy
  1. Levels 1-5: Spam Summon Skeleton/Zombie constantly. Every fight should start with a summon. Use the Telekinesis + Absorption loop for infinite magicka (Atronach birthsign).
  2. Levels 6-15: Push Conjuration to Expert for mid-tier Atronachs. Start Marksman training with a good bow. Level Illusion for Courage/Rally to buff your summons.
  3. Levels 16-25: Master Conjuration unlocks Xivilai and Storm Atronach -- the most powerful summons. These will solo most encounters while you support from range.
  4. Levels 25+: With Master Conjuration, Alchemy, and Destruction, you can handle any content. Focus on collecting gear and completing Daedric quests.
Remastered Tips
Warning

Heavy Armor reduces Conjuration duration in Remastered. This is critical. Wear Light Armor or robes to get full summon uptime. If you must wear Heavy Armor, enchant it with Fortify Conjuration to compensate. This penalty did not exist in the original game.

#4
Armored Archmage
Battlemage -- Magic Specialization
A-Tier Magic Melee Hybrid
Race & Setup
  • RaceBreton
  • BirthsignThe Apprentice
  • SpecMagic
  • FavoredStrength, Intelligence
Major Skills
Alchemy Alteration Blade Conjuration Destruction Heavy Armor Mysticism
Playstyle

The cleverest racial synergy in the game. Breton's innate 50% Magic Resistance perfectly cancels The Apprentice's 100% Weakness to Magic, leaving you with a net 50% Weakness -- far more manageable than it sounds. In exchange, you get +100 bonus Magicka with essentially no meaningful downside once you stack a few more resistance sources.

You fight in full Heavy Armor with a blade in one hand and spells in the other. Open with Destruction to soften groups, swap to Blade for melee, cast Alteration shields for defense, and use Conjuration for backup. A versatile build that excels at everything.

Why It's Powerful

+100 Magicka at character creation is enormous. Most mage builds don't reach that bonus until late game. Combined with Heavy Armor tanking and Blade damage, you have no weakness. You can handle melee enemies, magic users, groups, and bosses with equal ease. The Apprentice birthsign's "downside" is almost entirely negated by being Breton.

Endgame Gear
ArmorDaedric / Madness Heavy -- highest physical defense
WeaponGoldbrand / Umbra -- top-tier enchanted swords
RingRing of Khajiiti -- Fortify Speed + Chameleon
AmuletMundane Ring -- Spell Absorption to offset remaining weakness
Leveling Strategy
  1. Levels 1-8: Balance Blade and Destruction training. Use Alteration shields (Shield spell) before every fight. Prioritize Intelligence and Strength equally -- you need both.
  2. Levels 9-16: Seek Resist Magic enchantments to eliminate the remaining Apprentice weakness. Once at 100% Magic Resistance, you're untouchable by spells. Level Conjuration for summon support.
  3. Levels 17-25: Push Destruction to Expert/Master for powerful AoE. Heavy Armor should be high enough to tank multiple enemies. You're a walking fortress that casts.
  4. Levels 25+: Complete gear set. With maxed Blade, Destruction, and Heavy Armor, you're the most versatile build in the game -- there is no encounter you can't handle.
Remastered Tips
Remastered Note

Willpower's quadratic magicka regen makes this build even better in Remastered. You don't need Stunted Magicka or Absorption loops -- just pump Willpower and watch your magicka fly back. The 12 Virtue Points system means you can reliably hit Strength 5 / Intelligence 5 / Willpower 2 each level for optimal growth. Note that Heavy Armor reduces Conjuration duration, so consider swapping Conjuration for Restoration if you lean into melee.

#5
Shadow Striker
Stealth Archer / Assassin -- Stealth Specialization
A-Tier Stealth Assassin Poison Master
Race & Setup
  • RaceWood Elf
  • BirthsignThe Thief
  • SpecStealth
  • FavoredAgility, Endurance
Major Skills
Acrobatics Alchemy Blade Light Armor Marksman Illusion Sneak
Playstyle

The classic stealth archer taken to its logical extreme. Open every fight from stealth with a poisoned arrow for 3x ranged sneak attack damage plus poison. If enemies survive, use Illusion (Invisibility/Calm) to re-enter stealth and hit again. For melee range, switch to daggers for the 8x dagger sneak attack multiplier.

Alchemy is the force multiplier. Craft Damage Health + Weakness to Poison dual-effect poisons that stack. Apply Weakness to Poison first, then Damage Health -- the second poison hits for amplified damage. At high Alchemy, a single poison can deal 700+ damage.

Why It's Powerful

Sneak attacks + Alchemy poisons scale independently of difficulty. Even at max difficulty where your base weapon damage is nerfed, poison damage is unaffected. A 700-damage poison kills nearly anything in one hit. Combined with Invisibility re-stealth, you can kill entire dungeons without ever being seen or taking a single hit.

Endgame Gear
BowBow of Infernal Frost -- high damage + elemental enchant
DaggerBlade of Woe -- Dark Brotherhood reward, excellent sneak weapon
ArmorGlass / Dark Brotherhood -- light armor with stealth bonuses
RingRing of Khajiiti -- Fortify Speed + Chameleon 35%
Leveling Strategy
  1. Levels 1-5: Sneak everywhere, even in towns. Level Alchemy immediately -- collect every ingredient you see. Wood Elf racial skill bonuses give you a head start on Marksman and Sneak.
  2. Levels 6-12: Join the Dark Brotherhood for Blade of Woe. Push Marksman to Expert for Power Shot (knockback on draw). Start crafting poisons -- Weakness to Poison + Damage Health is your bread and butter.
  3. Levels 13-20: Level Illusion for Invisibility. This lets you re-stealth mid-combat for repeated sneak attacks. Push Alchemy to Expert+ for 700-damage poisons.
  4. Levels 20+: Master Marksman + Master Alchemy = everything dies in one shot. Acrobatics gives mobility. You can clear any content effortlessly.
Remastered Tips
Remastered Note

Arrows are always recoverable in Remastered, making Marksman much more economical. Agility governs dagger damage now, so your dagger sneak attacks scale with the same stat as your stealth skills. Pump Agility every level. Fatigue no longer affects damage, so sprinting into position before a sneak attack has no damage penalty. This build got strictly better in Remastered.

#6
Destruction Archmage
Pure Mage -- Magic Specialization
A-Tier Magic Destruction Spellcrafter
Race & Setup
  • RaceBreton
  • BirthsignThe Mage
  • SpecMagic
  • FavoredIntelligence, Willpower
Major Skills
Alchemy Alteration Conjuration Destruction Illusion Mysticism Restoration
Playstyle

The purest mage experience. All 7 major skills are magic schools. Your weapon is Spellcrafting -- the ability to create custom spells at the Arcane University. The real power comes from Weakness stacking: cast Weakness to Fire 100% on an enemy, then hit them with a Fire spell. The fire spell does double damage. Stack it again -- triple. This scales infinitely.

You have an answer for everything. Conjuration for tanks, Illusion for crowd control, Alteration for shields, Restoration for healing, Mysticism for utility, and Destruction for damage. Alchemy provides potions and poisons for any situation.

Why It's Powerful

Spellcrafting with Weakness stacking has no damage ceiling. A custom spell combining Weakness to Fire + Fire Damage in one cast does exponentially more damage with each successive hit. At high Destruction, you can one-shot anything in the game. Breton's Magic Resistance + The Mage's +50 Magicka gives a solid, safe foundation without exploit dependency.

Endgame Gear
RobeArch-Mage's Robe -- Mages Guild reward, best mage robe
StaffStaff of Indarys -- backup damage when low on magicka
JewelryFortify Intelligence / Magicka -- enchanted rings and amulets
HandsSpelldrinker Gauntlets -- Absorb Magicka on touch
Leveling Strategy
  1. Levels 1-5: Join the Mages Guild immediately. Complete recommendation quests. Spam cheap spells from every school to level broadly. Prioritize Intelligence every level.
  2. Levels 6-12: Reach the Arcane University. Start Spellcrafting. Create combined Weakness + Damage spells. Push Destruction to Expert for area damage spells.
  3. Levels 13-20: Master Destruction for the most powerful spell effects. Create Weakness to Fire 100% + Fire Damage 80 custom spells. Two casts kill almost anything.
  4. Levels 20+: Collect Fortify Intelligence gear for maximum magicka pool. With all 7 schools at Expert+, you are prepared for every possible encounter.
Remastered Tips
Remastered Note

Willpower's quadratic magicka regen is a game-changer for pure mages. Pump Willpower alongside Intelligence every level -- the regen at high Willpower is dramatic. Magic XP scales with mana cost, so casting expensive spells levels your skills faster. This means your custom high-cost spells double as training tools. Prioritize Intelligence 5 / Willpower 5 / Endurance 2 for your first 10 levels.

#7
Iron Fortress
Tank Warrior -- Combat Specialization
A-Tier Melee Tank Reflect Damage
Race & Setup
  • RaceOrc
  • BirthsignThe Warrior
  • SpecCombat
  • FavoredStrength, Endurance
Major Skills
Alteration Armorer Athletics Blade Block Heavy Armor Restoration
Playstyle

The simplest and most durable build. Stack the highest armor rating in the game with Orc racial bonuses, Heavy Armor, and Alteration Shield spells. Enemies barely scratch you. At Expert Block, you unlock Shield Bash -- a stagger move that interrupts enemy attacks and creates openings for power attacks.

The endgame goal is 100% Reflect Damage via enchanted gear. Enemies literally kill themselves by hitting you. Combined with your massive health pool and armor rating, you walk into any fight and win by standing still. Restoration keeps you topped off, Armorer keeps your gear at max condition.

Why It's Powerful

100% Reflect Damage means melee enemies die from their own attacks. Your armor + Shield spells make you nearly immune to physical damage. Restoration handles any chip damage. The Orc racial Berserk power (2x damage, halved Agility) is a panic button that lets you burst down tough enemies. Simple, reliable, and incredibly strong in every situation.

Endgame Gear
ArmorDaedric Heavy Armor -- highest base armor rating
ShieldEscutcheon of Chorrol -- Reflect Damage + Reflect Spell
WeaponGoldbrand -- 22 base + fire enchant
JewelryReflect Damage enchanted -- stack to reach 100% total
Leveling Strategy
  1. Levels 1-5: Focus on Heavy Armor and Blade. Block every attack you can -- you need to reach Expert Block for Shield Bash. Repair your armor constantly to level Armorer.
  2. Levels 6-12: Get Block to Expert ASAP. Shield Bash transforms your combat from passive to aggressive. Start Alteration training for Shield spells -- these stack with your armor.
  3. Levels 13-20: Begin collecting Reflect Damage enchantments. Escutcheon of Chorrol is a quest reward -- prioritize getting it. Level Restoration for efficient self-healing in combat.
  4. Levels 20+: Reach 100% Reflect Damage. With Daedric armor + Shield spells + Reflect Damage, you are the most durable character possible. Walk into Oblivion gates and clear them without breaking a sweat.
Remastered Tips
Remastered Note

Retroactive Endurance health is great news -- you can focus on Strength early for damage without sacrificing HP. Block Expert = Shield Bash is confirmed in Remastered and is even more impactful with the new stagger system. Sprint uses Fatigue, so Athletics helps offset that. Fatigue no longer affects damage, so don't worry about keeping it full before power attacks. Armorer is more important than ever since you'll be tanking every hit.

#8
Arcane Sorcerer
Heavy Armor Mage -- Magic Specialization
A-Tier Magic Heavy Armor +250 Magicka
Race & Setup
  • RaceHigh Elf
  • BirthsignThe Atronach
  • SpecMagic
  • FavoredIntelligence, Endurance
Major Skills
Alchemy Alteration Conjuration Destruction Heavy Armor Mysticism Restoration
Playstyle

The maximum magicka build. High Elf gives +100 Magicka, The Atronach gives +150 Magicka and 50% Spell Absorption. That's +250 bonus Magicka at level 1. The tradeoff is Stunted Magicka (no natural regen) and High Elf's elemental weaknesses, but with Spell Absorption absorbing hostile magic as mana, these downsides become advantages.

You wear Heavy Armor and fight at touch range with Destruction touch spells. Touch spells cost less magicka than ranged versions and deal the same damage. With your massive magicka pool, you can chain dozens of touch-range blasts. Heavy Armor keeps you alive in melee range. Alchemy potions restore magicka when Spell Absorption isn't enough.

Why It's Powerful

+250 starting Magicka is absurd. At high Intelligence, your total magicka pool can exceed 800. Touch-range Destruction spells are extremely magicka-efficient, and with your pool, you can cast 30+ before needing a refill. Spell Absorption means enemy mages heal your magicka bar. Heavy Armor means melee enemies can't kill you. You're durable, efficient, and devastating.

Endgame Gear
ArmorDaedric Heavy -- Spell Absorption enchantments to reach 100%
JewelrySpelldrinker Amulet -- Absorb + Fortify Magicka
RingFortify Intelligence Ring -- maximize magicka pool
ShieldSpell Absorption shield -- push toward 100% absorption
Leveling Strategy
  1. Levels 1-5: Use Alchemy potions for magicka restoration early -- Stunted Magicka means no regen. Learn the Telekinesis + Absorption loop ASAP. Cast touch-range Destruction in the tutorial to start leveling.
  2. Levels 6-12: Push Destruction to Expert for area touch spells. Level Heavy Armor by tanking hits. Prioritize Intelligence and Endurance every level for magicka and survivability.
  3. Levels 13-20: Start collecting Spell Absorption enchantments. Each piece brings you closer to 100%. Alteration shields stack with Heavy Armor for extreme durability.
  4. Levels 20+: At 100% Spell Absorption + full Daedric, you are immune to magic and highly resistant to physical. Your 800+ magicka pool lets you obliterate anything with chain-cast touch spells.
Remastered Tips
Warning

Heavy Armor reduces Conjuration duration. If you rely on summons, either use Fortify Conjuration enchants or accept shorter summon uptime. On the bright side, Willpower's quadratic regen doesn't matter for Atronach builds (Stunted Magicka), so skip Willpower and invest those Virtue Points in Endurance or Strength instead. Magic XP scaling with cost means your expensive touch spells level Destruction faster.

#9
Nightblade
Magic Assassin -- Stealth Specialization
B-Tier Stealth Magic Crowd Control
Race & Setup
  • RaceBreton
  • BirthsignThe Shadow
  • SpecStealth
  • FavoredIntelligence, Agility
Major Skills
Acrobatics Blade Destruction Illusion Light Armor Mysticism Sneak
Playstyle

The magical assassin. While the Shadow Striker relies on poisons and raw sneak attacks, the Nightblade uses Illusion for crowd control. Paralyze, Calm, Frenzy, and Invisibility let you manipulate every encounter. Detect Life through walls means you always know where enemies are before they know about you.

The combat loop: Detect Life to scout, Invisibility to approach, sneak attack with a dagger, Paralyze any survivor, finish with Destruction. Calm lets you re-stealth in the middle of a group fight. Frenzy turns enemies against each other. You are a puppetmaster who controls the battlefield with magic and kills with steel.

Why It's Powerful

Illusion is the most underrated school. Paralyze is a guaranteed disable with no save. Calm instantly ends combat, letting you re-stealth for another sneak attack. Invisibility is better than Chameleon for a single strike. The Shadow birthsign gives a free once-per-day Invisibility power to bootstrap early gameplay. Breton's Magic Resistance protects against enemy casters.

Endgame Gear
ArmorGlass Armor -- best light armor rating
WeaponSufferthorn / Blade of Woe -- dagger with Damage Health enchant
JewelryFortify Intelligence + Chameleon -- partial Chameleon for detection reduction
RingRing of Khajiiti -- Speed + Chameleon 35%
Leveling Strategy
  1. Levels 1-5: Use The Shadow's daily Invisibility power liberally. Sneak through the tutorial. Start leveling Illusion with Starlight/minor Illusion spells. Buy every Illusion spell you can afford.
  2. Levels 6-12: Push Illusion to Apprentice/Journeyman for Calm and Invisibility spells. Learn Detect Life from Mysticism -- this is your scouting tool. Stealth kill everything.
  3. Levels 13-20: Illusion Expert unlocks Paralyze -- the single strongest crowd control spell. You can now freeze any enemy in place and execute them. Destruction provides ranged burst for targets you can't reach.
  4. Levels 20+: Master Illusion lets you Calm/Frenzy anything in the game. Combined with sneak attacks and Paralyze, no encounter is dangerous. Acrobatics lets you escape any bad position.
Remastered Tips
Remastered Note

Agility governs daggers in Remastered -- perfect for this build since you already want high Agility for stealth. Your dagger sneak attacks scale with the same stat as your Sneak skill. Magic XP scaling with mana cost means your expensive Illusion spells (Paralyze, Invisibility) train the skill faster. Pump Agility 5 / Intelligence 5 / Willpower 2 for optimal growth.

#10
Holy Crusader
Paladin -- Combat Specialization
B-Tier Melee Restoration Paladin
Race & Setup
  • RaceBreton
  • BirthsignThe Lady
  • SpecCombat
  • FavoredStrength, Willpower
Major Skills
Alteration Armorer Blade Block Blunt Heavy Armor Restoration
Playstyle

A warrior-priest who smites the unholy. The core mechanic: Turn Undead from Restoration trivializes every undead dungeon in the game. Vampires, zombies, liches, ghosts -- they all flee in terror while you cut them down. Since roughly 40% of Oblivion's dungeons are undead-themed, this is enormously powerful.

Blunt weapons at Expert apply Silence on power attacks, shutting down enemy mages. Combined with Shield Bash from Expert Block, you can stagger-lock any melee enemy and silence any caster. Restoration keeps you healed, Alteration provides Shield spells, and The Lady birthsign gives +10 Willpower and +10 Endurance for a solid stat foundation.

Why It's Powerful

Turn Undead is effectively an "I win" button for 40% of the game's content. Blunt Expert Silence shuts down mages -- the only real threat to armored warriors. Block Expert Shield Bash handles melee enemies. You have a specific counter for every enemy type. The Lady's stat bonuses accelerate early leveling, and Breton's Magic Resistance covers your main vulnerability.

Endgame Gear
ArmorCrusader's Relics / Daedric -- Knights of the Nine reward set or Daedric
WeaponMace of Molag Bal -- Daedric artifact, powerful blunt weapon
ShieldEscutcheon of Chorrol -- Reflect Damage + Reflect Spell
BackupBlade for undead -- silver or enchanted blade for ghost enemies
Leveling Strategy
  1. Levels 1-5: Train Blade and Blunt equally. Block every attack to push toward Expert. Buy Turn Undead from any Chapel or Restoration trainer. Start clearing undead dungeons for easy loot and XP.
  2. Levels 6-12: Reach Block Expert for Shield Bash. Push Blunt toward Expert for the Silence effect. Prioritize Strength and Willpower -- you need both for melee damage and Restoration magicka.
  3. Levels 13-20: With both Block and Blunt at Expert, you can handle any enemy type. Turn Undead makes Ayleid ruins and vampire caves trivial. Level Armorer to keep your heavy gear in top condition.
  4. Levels 20+: Complete Knights of the Nine for the Crusader's Relics. Full Daedric is also an option. Focus Alteration for high-tier Shield spells. You're a self-sustaining melee powerhouse.
Remastered Tips
Remastered Note

Willpower's quadratic magicka regen is essential for this build since you need Restoration magicka for combat healing and Turn Undead. The Lady's +10 Willpower gives you a head start. Block Expert Shield Bash is even stronger in Remastered. Retroactive Endurance means you can prioritize Strength and Willpower early without losing HP. Having both Blade and Blunt as major skills gives weapon flexibility -- use whichever matches the encounter.

Swords Bard Archer

Half-Elf · College of Swords Bard 10 / Fighter 2 · Dazzling Bowmaster
Ranged DPS Full Caster Support Patch 8 #7 Ranked

Overview

The Swords Bard Archer is the most versatile character in Baldur's Gate 3. You are a full caster with 5th-level spell slots, a martial combatant with Extra Attack and Blade Flourishes that work at range, a face character with maxed Charisma for every dialogue check, and a support pillar handing out Bardic Inspiration and healing. No other build in the game does all four at this level.

College of Swords gives you medium armor, martial weapon proficiency, and Blade Flourishes — special maneuvers that add your Bardic Inspiration die to attacks. The critical insight: Blade Flourishes work with ranged weapons. You fire arrows enhanced with magical flourishes while staying safely out of melee. Fighter 2 gives Action Surge for nova turns: four attacks plus a leveled spell in one round.

Why Bard 10 / Fighter 2?

Bard 10 is the sweet spot. You get 5th-level spells, Magical Secrets (steal the best spells from any class list), Extra Attack, and an upgraded d10 Bardic Inspiration die. Fighter 2 gives Action Surge — a second full action once per short rest. This is the single strongest feature you can get in two levels. Combined, you have more options on any given turn than any other build in the game.

Race — High Half-Elf (Recommended)

  • Racial Civil Militia — Shield + martial weapon proficiency (backup option)
  • Racial Darkvision — 12m. No disadvantage in dark dungeons.
  • Racial Fey Ancestry — Advantage on saves vs Charm. Can't be put to sleep.
  • Cantrip Fire Bolt — Free wizard cantrip from High Half-Elf. Solid ranged filler.
Alternative: Wood Elf

Wood Elf gives +1.5m movement (10.5m base), Stealth proficiency, and Darkvision. Great for kiting and repositioning. You lose the free cantrip but gain the fastest base speed in the game. Pick Wood Elf if you want maximum mobility, Half-Elf if you want the extra cantrip.

DEX vs CHA Priority

Both start at 16. DEX powers your attacks and AC. CHA powers your spell DC, Inspiration dice count, and every social check. At level 4 you'll take +2 CHA to hit 18 (your face role is critical from Act 1). At level 8 you take Sharpshooter instead of +2 DEX — the -5/+10 damage matters more than a +1 to hit. DEX stays at 16 and that's fine because Sharpshooter makes up the difference.

Action Surge + Spells

Action Surge gives a full action. You can use it to cast a leveled spell (Hypnotic Pattern, Hold Monster) AND still make your normal attacks. Or fire 4 Sharpshooter arrows in one turn. This flexibility is why the build is #7 ranked — you always have the perfect answer.

College of Swords: Blade Flourishes

Blade Flourishes are the signature feature of College of Swords. When you take the Attack action, you can expend one Bardic Inspiration die to add it to the weapon damage roll plus trigger a special effect. In BG3, these work with ranged weapons — making them core to this archer build.

FlourishEffectBest Use
Defensive Flourish Add Inspiration die to damage AND to your AC until next turn. Default choice. +d10 AC while staying at range makes you nearly untouchable. Use every combat.
Slashing Flourish Add Inspiration die to damage against the target AND deal the same bonus damage to a second enemy within 1.5m of the target. When enemies are clustered. The splash damage is free — great against groups.
Mobile Flourish Add Inspiration die to damage and push the target back up to 6m. You can also move up to the target without provoking opportunity attacks. Push enemies off cliffs, into hazards, or away from squishy allies. The free movement helps reposition.
Flourish Economy

After Bard 5 (Font of Inspiration), your Inspiration dice recharge on short rest. You get CHA mod (4-5) dice per rest. Use Defensive Flourish on your first attack each combat for the AC bonus, then save remaining dice for allies who need Inspiration checks or for Slashing Flourish in multi-target fights. You'll never run dry.

Ranged Flourish Interaction

Mobile Flourish with a bow fires a magically-enhanced arrow that pushes the target back. You don't need to be in melee. Slashing Flourish with a bow sends a flourish of magical energy that hits a second target near the first. These are not melee-only abilities despite the name — BG3 specifically allows them with ranged weapons.

Feat Picks

You get two feats total: one at Bard 4 and one at Bard 8. Both are locked in — there's no flexibility here because both choices are objectively correct for this build.

Level 4 — Bard 4
Ability Score Improvement: +2 CHA → 18
Increases spell save DC by 1, grants an extra Bardic Inspiration die per rest, and boosts every Charisma skill check. You're the party face — CHA is priority #1. Sharpshooter at 4 is a trap because your to-hit is too low to offset -5 without Archery + advantage sources.
Level 8 — Bard 8
Sharpshooter
-5 to hit, +10 damage on every ranged weapon attack. By level 8 you have Archery (+2), Faerie Fire (advantage), and possibly +1 weapons — the accuracy math works. Also removes long range and cover penalties. This feat is the difference between "good ranged damage" and "best damage in the party."
Why Not Alert or Lucky?

Alert is great on other builds but Bards already add half proficiency to Initiative via Jack of All Trades. You're already going early. Lucky is universally strong but you need both CHA and Sharpshooter more. If you somehow got a third feat (Hag Hair, etc.), take +2 CHA to cap at 20.

Combat Guide

Standard Turn

  1. Pre-buff with Haste Before Combat
    Cast Haste on yourself (or have an ally cast it). This is always your first action in any serious fight. Lasts 10 turns — long enough for any encounter.
  2. Open with Faerie Fire or Hypnotic Pattern Turn 1
    If the enemy group is clustered, open with Hypnotic Pattern to incapacitate them. If they're spread out, Faerie Fire gives your whole party advantage. Use your Haste action to attack after.
  3. Attack with Defensive Flourish Every Turn
    First attack: toggle Defensive Flourish ON. Fire your Sharpshooter arrow for 1d8 + DEX + 10 + d10 (Flourish) damage. Gain the d10 as bonus AC. Second attack: normal Sharpshooter arrow. Haste attack: third arrow. You deal ~55 damage per turn sustainably.
  4. Bonus action: Healing Word or Bardic Inspiration As Needed
    Pick up downed allies with Healing Word (bonus action, 18m range). Or hand out Bardic Inspiration to your rogue/fighter before their turn.

Nova Turn (Boss Fights)

  1. Haste active, begin nova Turn 1
    Ensure Haste is running. If not, cast it first and attack with your remaining actions.
  2. Attack action: 2 Sharpshooter arrows (Flourish on first) Action
    First arrow: Slashing Flourish if enemies are close together, Defensive Flourish otherwise. Second arrow: straight Sharpshooter.
  3. Haste action: 1 Sharpshooter arrow Haste
    Third arrow from Haste. Pure damage.
  4. Action Surge: 2 more Sharpshooter arrows OR a leveled spell Surge
    Option A: 2 more arrows for 5 total Sharpshooter attacks in one turn (avg ~85 damage). Option B: Hold Monster on the boss, then let your melee allies auto-crit it to death. Read the situation.
  5. Bonus action: Healing Word or Second Wind Bonus
    Keep an ally alive or top yourself off. You still have a bonus action even on nova turns.
Damage Math: Nova Turn

5 arrows × (1d8 + 3 DEX + 10 Sharpshooter) = 5 × 17.5 avg = 87.5 damage in one turn. Add Flourish die (5.5 avg) and Gontr Mael's +1d4 radiant per hit (2.5 avg) = ~127 damage. Against a Vulnerable target (Bhaalist Armour), this doubles the piercing component. No other build delivers this much burst at range while also being a full caster.

Concentration Management

Haste requires concentration. If you lose it, you lose a full turn (Haste Lethargy). Prioritize: CON save proficiency from Fighter 1, War Caster (if you have a 3rd feat source), Amulet of Greater Health, and positioning out of enemy range. Defensive Flourish + ranged positioning = enemies rarely hit you = you rarely make concentration saves.

Support Role

What makes this build #7 in the game rather than just "good archer" is that you're a full support character simultaneously. You deal top-tier ranged damage AND provide the following to your party every single fight:

Bardic Inspiration

CHA mod (4-5) d10 dice per short rest. Hand them to allies before critical attack rolls, saving throws, or ability checks. A d10 Inspiration to your rogue's Sneak Attack roll or your fighter's crucial saving throw changes outcomes. In dialogue, it's +d10 to any Persuasion/Deception/Intimidation check.

Healing

Healing Word is a bonus action with 18m range. You never have to stop attacking to heal. Pick up downed allies from across the battlefield while maintaining full offensive pressure. This is the #1 reason every party wants a Bard.

Crowd Control

Hypnotic Pattern, Hold Person/Monster, Fear, Polymorph. You have the best crowd control spell list in the game outside of a dedicated Wizard. The difference is you can CC the biggest threat AND then shoot everything else dead. Wizards just CC.

Face Skills

Expertise in Persuasion + Deception with 18-20 CHA + Jack of All Trades on everything else. You pass almost every social check in the game. This alone justifies the build for story-focused players.

The Versatility Advantage

On any given turn you can: deal 50+ ranged damage, heal an ally, CC a group of enemies, buff a party member, counterspell an enemy caster, or teleport someone out of danger. No other build in BG3 has this many options available simultaneously. This is why the Swords Bard is the Swiss army knife of the game.

Honour Mode Tips

Honour Mode gives bosses Legendary Actions and raises overall difficulty. The Swords Bard Archer is one of the safest Honour Mode builds because you operate at range, have full spellcasting for emergencies, and can Counterspell boss abilities.

Legendary Action Economy

Honour Mode bosses get Legendary Actions that let them act on your turns. This makes concentration more vulnerable and melee more dangerous. Stay at maximum bow range (18m), use Defensive Flourish every turn for the AC bonus, and keep Counterspell ready. You are the safest damage dealer in the party.

Key Honour Mode Strategies

  1. Always open with Hypnotic Pattern
    In Honour Mode, action economy is everything. Removing 2-3 enemies from the fight on turn 1 is worth more than any amount of damage. Even if the boss saves, the adds won't.
  2. Save Action Surge for emergencies
    Don't blow Action Surge on turn 1 for damage. Save it for: emergency Counterspell + attack, or a clutch Hold Monster when the boss uses a Legendary Action you need to counter.
  3. Stack Radiating Orb (Gontr Mael)
    Each arrow hit applies Radiating Orb (-1 to attack rolls, stacking). After 3-4 arrows, the boss has -3 to -4 on attacks. Combined with Defensive Flourish AC bonus, the boss literally can't hit you.
  4. Position at max range, always
    18m longbow range means most melee enemies can't reach you in one turn. Dash away if they close. Mobile Flourish pushes them back. You should almost never take damage.
  5. Keep Healing Word for downed allies
    In Honour Mode, death saves are brutal. Healing Word is bonus action + 18m range. You can revive someone AND still do full damage on the same turn. This alone saves Honour Mode runs.
Honour Mode Boss Combo

Turn 1: Haste (if not pre-cast) + Faerie Fire. Turn 2: Hold Monster on boss + 2 Sharpshooter arrows (one Flourished). Your melee allies auto-crit the paralyzed boss. Turn 3: Action Surge for 5 arrows into the boss, Counterspell if it tries to escape. Most Honour Mode bosses die in 2-3 rounds with this rotation.

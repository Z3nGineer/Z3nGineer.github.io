CHIM

See the dream. Reshape it.

A hand-curated overhaul for Oblivion Remastered. 60+ mods, hand-tuned configs, custom patches. Zero bloat.

In Elder Scrolls lore, CHIM is the secret syllable of royalty — the state of achieving divine awareness within the Godhead's dream while maintaining your own identity. Where others who glimpse the truth zero-sum and cease to exist, those who achieve CHIM see the dream for what it is and reshape it. Tiber Septim used it to change the jungle of Cyrodiil into the grasslands you walk today. This modpack carries that name because it does the same thing — it sees Oblivion for what it was always meant to be and reshapes it, without breaking the dream.

Original Work

CHIM Patches

Where I had to write custom code to make things work together. These are the patches that come with the modpack.

Eternal Darkness — Night & Interior Tuning

Outdoor skylight hidden for real darkness, moonlight bumped to 0.7 so you can actually navigate. Interior skylight hiding disabled — brightness is handled by the UltraPlus profile instead. Generic interior lights left at vanilla so dungeons aren't pitch black.

UltraPlusExtensions — Interior Brightness Profile

Custom-tuned skylight_ultraplus_choice_brighter_v2.ini with per-location multipliers. Caves and ruins at 0.35, dungeons at 0.4, houses and inns at 0.6. Dark enough to feel atmospheric, bright enough to see what's trying to kill you.

LumenRemastered & ShadersRevised — API Fix

Both mods call ExecuteConsoleCommand with 3 params but UE4SS now needs 4. I patched both to pass playerController as the 4th arg so they stop crashing on fast travel and level transitions.

Controller Force Feedback Fix

Sustained spell effects trigger permanent controller vibration that never stops. Added bEnableForceFeedback=False to Engine.ini. Only affects Oblivion — other games keep rumble.

Curated Load Order

Hand-ordered Plugins.txt with every ESP in the right sequence. Base game, DLCs, unofficial patch, then mods by category. Tested for conflicts so you don't have to.

How It Works

The CHIM Installer

A custom-built tool that handles everything. You download the mods, it does the rest.

1. Choose Your Profile

The installer detects your hardware and offers two profiles. CHIM for PC — the full 59-mod experience with max quality visuals. CHIM Deck for Steam Deck — strips heavy visual mods, adds 7 optimization mods, and deploys custom configs that make the game actually playable.

2. Download the Mods

The installer walks you through each mod one at a time. Click the button, it opens the Nexus page, you hit "Slow Download" (free, no premium needed). The installer watches your Downloads folder and checks off each mod as it arrives. No guesswork.

3. Automatic Verification

Before touching your game files, the installer runs a full verification pass. It checks every archive for corruption, validates file types, scans for conflicts between mods, verifies disk space, and confirms your game directory structure. Nothing gets installed until every check passes.

4. One-Click Install

Every mod gets extracted and placed in the exact right directory. ESPs go to Data, PAKs go to the mods folder, OBSE plugins go to their folder, UE4SS mods get enabled. On Steam Deck, a custom Engine.ini with 50+ tuned variables gets deployed automatically. Your game is ready to launch.

Steam Deck

What CHIM Deck Actually Does

The game was barely playable. We fixed it.

The Problem

Oblivion Remastered on Steam Deck runs at 20-25 FPS on Low settings with constant stuttering, VRAM overflow crashes, and a loading screen bug that freezes the Deck ~90% of the time when entering outdoor areas. Valve marked it "Verified" anyway. It's not playable out of the box.

What CHIM Deck Does About It
Disables forced ray tracing — Lumen RT is the single biggest performance killer. The Deck can't do RT. CHIM Deck removes it entirely. 15-20 FPS gained.
Fixes VRAM crashes — The game tries to load unlimited textures into the Deck's shared 4GB GPU memory. CHIM Deck caps the streaming pool and fixes the memory leak.
Fixes loading screen crashes — The game spikes to 250 FPS during loads then crashes. CHIM Deck caps FPS at 30 even during load screens so the transition doesn't kill the GPU.
Reduces shadow cost by 80% — Shadow cascades dropped from 10 to 2, resolution from 8192 to 1024. You won't notice at 800p. Your framerate will.
Cuts grass/foliage density — Reduced to 40%. Still looks green and alive at 800p, but renders 60% less geometry. The open world is where the Deck struggles most.
Eliminates stutter — Shader pre-compilation, pipeline caching, and PSO disk caching prevent the compilation stutter that makes the game feel broken when you walk into a new area.
Disables fog and cloth physics — Volumetric fog and cloth simulation are pure GPU waste at 800p. Disabled. Frames reclaimed.
Custom GameUserSettings.ini — Every in-game setting optimized for 1280x800 with XeSS Performance upscaling. No guesswork about what to set where.
After Install — Quick Access Menu Settings

Press the ... button on your Deck and set these for Oblivion Remastered:

Framerate Limit: 30 FPS  |  Refresh Rate: 40 Hz  |  Allow Tearing: Off  |  TDP Limit: 12-15W  |  GPU Clock: 1200 MHz

Use Proton Experimental or GE-Proton 10-1+. Game must be on the internal SSD, not the SD card.

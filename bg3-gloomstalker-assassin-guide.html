Gloomstalker Assassin — Shadow Reaper

Wood Elf · Gloom Stalker 5 / Assassin 4 / Fighter 3 · The Ambush King
Burst DPS Stealth Ranged Multiclass Patch 8

Overview

The Gloomstalker Assassin is BG3's undisputed ambush king. By combining the Gloom Stalker ranger's Dread Ambusher (a free extra attack plus bonus damage and movement on your first turn) with the Assassin rogue's Assassinate (automatic critical hits on surprised enemies), and the fighter's Action Surge (an entire second action), you create a character that can fire 6-8 attacks on turn one — most of them critical hits — before the enemy even knows you exist. Entire encounters end in a single round.

This is a Ranger 5 / Rogue 4 / Fighter 3 multiclass. You take Ranger first to 5 for Extra Attack and the Gloom Stalker subclass, then Rogue to 4 for Assassin and an ASI, then Fighter to 3 for Action Surge and a martial archetype. Every piece synergizes: Dread Ambusher gives you the extra attack, Assassinate makes them all crits, Action Surge lets you do it again, and Sneak Attack adds free damage to one of those hits.

Why This Multiclass Works

Each class contributes something irreplaceable. Ranger 5 gives Extra Attack + Dread Ambusher (3 attacks base). Rogue 4 gives Assassinate (auto-crit on surprised) + 2d6 Sneak Attack + an ASI. Fighter 3 gives Action Surge (repeat your entire action) + a fighting style + a subclass. The sum is far greater than any single-class build for nova damage.

Race & Background

Primary: Wood Elf

  • Racial Fleet of Foot — 10.5m base movement (vs 9m). Crucial for positioning before combat and reaching ideal ambush spots. Stacks with Dread Ambusher's +3m first-turn bonus.
  • Racial Darkvision (12m) — See in the dark. Synergizes with Umbral Sight — you're invisible to darkvision creatures in darkness.
  • Racial Perception Proficiency — Free Perception — the most important skill in BG3 for spotting traps and hidden enemies.
  • Racial Fey Ancestry — Advantage against charm. Can't be put to sleep.

Alternative: Half-Orc

  • Racial Savage Attacks — Extra damage die on critical hits. Since Assassinate gives you auto-crits, every surprise round hit rolls an additional weapon die. Mathematically superior for raw damage.
  • Racial Relentless Endurance — Drop to 1 HP instead of 0 once per long rest. Safety net if your alpha strike doesn't end the fight.
  • Note Half-Orc trades movement speed and stealth utility for more burst damage. Pick Half-Orc for Honour Mode boss nuking, Wood Elf for everything else.

Background

  • Best Criminal / Urchin — Stealth + Sleight of Hand proficiency. Stealth is your most important skill — it determines whether Assassinate triggers.
  • Alt Outlander — Athletics + Survival. If someone else handles Stealth checks for the party, this works for a more ranger-flavored character.

Starting at 17 DEX lets you take a half-feat at level 4 (like Alert, which adds +5 initiative) and still round DEX to 18. Or take +2 DEX to hit 19, then use your Rogue ASI for Sharpshooter. Either way, you hit 20 DEX by mid-game.

Why We Stop Ranger at 5

Ranger 5 gives Extra Attack — the biggest power spike. Ranger 6+ only gives marginal benefits (Favoured Enemy improvement, land speed). The Rogue and Fighter levels give you far more: Assassinate, Sneak Attack, Action Surge, and another subclass. Don't keep going Ranger.

The auto-crit part of Assassinate ONLY triggers against enemies with the Surprised condition. You get advantage against anyone who hasn't acted, but the auto-crit requires actual surprise. This is why Pass Without Trace and high Stealth are non-negotiable. If even one party member fails their Stealth check, surprise can fail for the whole encounter.

Subclass Breakdown

Gloom Stalker (Ranger)

The Gloom Stalker is the engine of your first turn. Every feature is designed for ambush combat.

  • Core Dread Ambusher — On your first turn of combat: +3m movement and one additional weapon attack that deals an extra 1d8 damage. This triggers every combat, not just surprise rounds. The extra attack works with Action Surge — you get it on each action.
  • Core Umbral Sight — While in darkness, you are invisible to creatures that rely on darkvision. This is effectively permanent advantage on Stealth checks in dark environments. Most BG3 dungeons, caves, and night encounters qualify.
  • Core Superior Darkvision — 24m darkvision range (double normal). You can attack from maximum bow range while remaining invisible via Umbral Sight.
  • Spell Disguise Self — Free cast. Change appearance for dialogue, infiltration, or RP. Doesn't cost a spell slot.

Assassin (Rogue)

The Assassin converts your stealth advantage into raw killing power. The entire subclass rewards going first.

  • Core Assassinate: Ambush — Advantage on attacks against creatures that haven't taken a turn. If the target is Surprised, hits are automatic critical hits. This doubles ALL damage dice: weapon dice, Sneak Attack dice, Dread Ambusher dice, even Ensnaring Strike dice.
  • Core Assassinate: Initiative — +5 to Initiative. Combined with high DEX (already +5), you're looking at +10 to initiative before any other bonuses. You will almost always go first.
  • Feature Assassin's Alacrity — At Rogue 3, you restore your full movement speed when Assassinate triggers. Ensures you can reposition even after moving to attack position.

Battle Master (Fighter) — Recommended

  • Maneuver Precision Attack — Add a Superiority Die to an attack roll. Turns Sharpshooter misses into hits. Use this when you roll just below the target's AC.
  • Maneuver Riposte — When an enemy misses you, make a weapon attack as a reaction. Free extra Sneak Attack opportunity (different turn).
  • Maneuver Trip Attack — Add Superiority Die damage and knock prone on a failed STR save. Gives your melee allies advantage.

Champion (Fighter) — Alternative

  • Feature Improved Critical — Critical hit on 19-20. After the surprise round ends, you still crit 10% of the time instead of 5%. Combined with Killer's Sweetheart ring (auto-crit once per long rest), this extends your crit potential into subsequent rounds.
  • Note Champion is simpler but mathematically weaker than Battle Master in most situations. Take it if you prefer a hands-off playstyle.

Feat Selection

You get 2 feats (Ranger 4 and Rogue 4). Both should maximize your first-turn damage output.

Tier 1: Must-Have

  • Core Sharpshooter — -5 to hit, +10 damage. With Archery (+2) and advantage from Assassinate, the -5 penalty barely matters. +10 flat damage on each of your 6 surprise round attacks is +60 total. Non-negotiable for ranged builds.
  • Core +2 DEX (to cap at 20) — Caps your most important stat. +1 to hit, +1 damage, +1 AC, +1 initiative, +1 Stealth. Use your other feat slot for this.

Tier 2: Excellent (If DEX Is Already 20)

  • Great Alert — +5 Initiative and cannot be surprised. Guarantees you act first. With Assassinate: Initiative (+5), high DEX (+5), and Alert (+5), you have +15 to initiative. You will literally never go second. The "cannot be surprised" clause is also great insurance.
  • Great Lucky — 3 luck points per long rest. Re-roll an attack, save, or ability check. Use on critical Stealth checks or when Sharpshooter causes a miss on a high-value target.

Tier 3: Melee Variant

  • Melee Great Weapon Master — -5/+10 for melee heavy weapons. If you're playing a melee Gloomstalker with greatswords instead of bows, this replaces Sharpshooter. Less common but viable.
  • Melee Savage Attacker — Roll weapon damage twice and take the higher. Consistent damage increase, especially good when critting with multiple dice.

The Surprise Round — How It Actually Works

Surprise is the single most important mechanic for this build. Understanding exactly how it works in BG3 (which differs from tabletop D&D) is the difference between one-shotting encounters and having a mediocre build.

How Surprise Works in BG3

  • Step 1 Initiate from Stealth — Your party must be in stealth (not just you) when combat begins. Turn-based mode lets you position everyone precisely.
  • Step 2 Stealth vs Passive Perception — Each party member's Stealth check is compared against every enemy's Passive Perception. If all party members beat all enemies, the enemies are Surprised.
  • Step 3 Surprised Condition — Surprised enemies skip their first turn entirely. They can't move, act, or react. Your entire party gets a free round of actions.
  • Step 4 Assassinate Triggers — Against Surprised enemies, your Assassinate feature turns every hit into an automatic critical. This doubles all damage dice.

How to Guarantee Surprise

  1. Cast Pass Without Trace Before Combat
    +10 to Stealth for every party member within range. This alone makes surprise nearly guaranteed against most enemies. Cast it before entering any dangerous area.
  2. Separate from the party Before Combat
    Ungroup your character from the party (click the chain icon). Send yourself ahead to scout alone. If your heavy-armor companions have low Stealth, leave them far behind the encounter and only bring them in after surprise triggers. Alternatively: have everyone in stealth but only YOU initiate combat.
  3. Use darkness and elevation Positioning
    Umbral Sight makes you invisible in darkness to darkvision creatures. Stay in unlit areas. Elevation provides a bonus to ranged attacks. Position on high ground before firing.
  4. Attack to initiate Turn 1
    Your first attack from stealth initiates combat. All enemies that failed to detect you are Surprised. Dread Ambusher and Assassinate both trigger on this first turn. Fire all 6 attacks (Action + Action Surge) before the enemy gets a turn.
Common Surprise Killers

Party member detected: If ONE companion fails their Stealth check, enemies near that companion may not be Surprised. Use solo initiation to avoid this.
See Invisibility / Truesight: Some Act 3 enemies can see through stealth. Silence or line-of-sight breaking is needed.
Conversation-initiated combat: If combat starts from dialogue, there's no surprise. Always attack from stealth, not from conversation.

Solo Initiation Trick

Unchain your character. Have the party wait around a corner or behind a wall. Sneak into position alone. Attack to start combat. Only YOUR Stealth matters for determining surprise on the enemies YOU can see. Your party members enter combat normally on their turns. This is the most reliable way to guarantee Assassinate triggers.

Combat Guide

The Alpha Strike (Surprise Round)

  1. Cast Pass Without Trace Before Combat
    Lasts 10 turns. Cast it before entering any area with enemies. +10 Stealth to the entire party.
  2. Enter Turn-Based Mode & Stealth Before Combat
    Press Shift+Space for turn-based mode. Press C to enter stealth. Position yourself on high ground with clear line of sight to the priority target. Unchain from party if needed.
  3. First Action: 3 Attacks (all auto-crit) Turn 1
    Attack (1) + Extra Attack (2) + Dread Ambusher (3). All hits auto-crit against Surprised enemies. Use Sharpshooter on every attack — you have advantage so the -5 barely matters. Apply Sneak Attack to the first hit for maximum crit damage.
  4. Action Surge: 3 More Attacks (all auto-crit) Turn 1
    Activate Action Surge. Attack again: Attack (4) + Extra Attack (5) + Dread Ambusher (6). Dread Ambusher triggers again on the second action. Enemies are still Surprised — still auto-crit. The target should be dead. If not, the second target should be.
  5. Bonus Action: Hide or Disengage End of Turn 1
    If any enemies survived: Cunning Action: Hide to re-enter stealth (bonus action). This gives you advantage on attacks next turn. If you need to reposition, use Cunning Action: Dash or Disengage.

After the Surprise Round

  1. Cunning Action: Hide each turn Every Turn
    Bonus action Hide → attack with advantage → Sneak Attack triggers. This is your sustained DPS loop. 2 attacks per turn (Extra Attack), one of which gets 2d6 Sneak Attack.
  2. Use Battle Master maneuvers As Needed
    Precision Attack when Sharpshooter would miss. Trip Attack to knock enemies prone for melee allies. Riposte when an enemy misses you in melee.
  3. Spike Growth for area denial Reactive
    If enemies are clustering or trying to flee, cast Spike Growth. 2d4 per 5ft of movement shreds anything moving through it.
Target Priority

Boss/elite first: Your surprise round can kill or nearly kill any single target in the game. Focus your 6 attacks on the most dangerous enemy.
Casters second: Enemy spellcasters can heal, buff, or CC your party. Kill them before they act.
Mobs last: Your party handles cleanup. You already eliminated the threat.

Honour Mode Tips

Honour Mode adds Legendary Actions to boss enemies, meaning they can act out of turn even when Surprised. Your alpha strike is still devastating, but you need to account for these changes.

Legendary Actions Change Everything

In Honour Mode, bosses gain Legendary Actions that let them respond even during your turn. Some bosses can break free of Surprised status early. Your alpha strike still deals massive damage, but don't assume the boss will sit there for all 6 attacks. Be prepared for a counter-attack.

Key Adjustments for Honour Mode

  • Prep Always have a backup plan — If your alpha strike doesn't kill the boss, you need to survive their Legendary Action. Keep Cloak of Displacement or Mirror Image active. Position behind cover.
  • Prep Party coordination — Have your party ready to follow up immediately. A War Cleric's Spirit Guardians, a Wizard's Haste on you, or a Bard's Cutting Words can all ensure the boss goes down on round 1 even with Legendary Actions.
  • Tactic Silence on caster bosses — Cast Silence on spellcasting bosses before your ambush (have a party member cast it). Even if they get a Legendary Action, they can't cast spells in the Silence zone.
  • Tactic Elevation abuse — Position on unreachable high ground (ladders, Misty Step up). Melee bosses can't reach you even with Legendary movement. You can continue shooting with impunity.
  • Tactic Save scum your surprise — Honour Mode has a single save file. Quicksave before initiating. If surprise fails, reload. The Stealth roll is determined on initiation, so a reload and repositioning can change the outcome.

Honour Mode Boss Encounters

  • Act 1 Phase Spider Matriarch — Can teleport as a Legendary Action. Position your party to cover all teleport destinations. Alpha strike should kill her before she ports.
  • Act 2 Ketheric Thorm — Has 2 phases. Save Action Surge for phase 2 if possible. His Legendary Action is a devastating AoE — spread your party out.
  • Act 3 Orin the Red — Unstoppable as a Legendary Action — she can't be incapacitated. Focus on raw damage. Your 6-attack alpha strike is the best opener against her.
  • Act 3 Raphael — Extremely high HP + Legendary Actions. Focus on disabling his minions first, then concentrate fire. Spike Growth on the battlefield limits his movement.
Elixir Stacking

Elixirs persist through long rest and are cheap. Elixir of Bloodlust (extra action on kill) can give you a THIRD action on your surprise turn if you kill a target. That's 9 attacks total. Combine with Hill Giant Strength elixir on a STR weapon for even more damage.

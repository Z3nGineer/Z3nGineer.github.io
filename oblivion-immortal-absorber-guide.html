The Immortal Absorber

High Elf · The Atronach · Heavy Armor + Spell Absorption + Reflect Damage
Spell Absorb Invincible Heavy Armor Remastered 2025

Overview

The Immortal Absorber is the most powerful endgame build in Oblivion Remastered. The core mechanic is simple: reach 100% Spell Absorption so every spell cast at you is converted into free magicka, then stack 100% Reflect Damage so enemies kill themselves hitting you. You become literally invincible — immune to all magic, and anything that attacks you in melee dies from its own damage bouncing back.

The build requires patience through the early game (Stunted Magicka means zero natural regen), but once the pieces come together around level 15-20, nothing in the game can threaten you. Not Mehrunes Dagon. Not the final boss of Shivering Isles. Nothing.

Why This Build is #1

100% Spell Absorption = every spell heals your magicka instead of damaging you. Enemy mages become walking mana potions. Conjured creatures get absorbed on summon. Traps do nothing. 100% Reflect Damage = melee attackers take their own damage. Combined, you are immune to magic AND melee simultaneously.

Race — High Elf (Altmer)

  • Passive +100 Magicka — largest magicka bonus of any race, critical for an Atronach build
  • Weakness 25% Weakness to Magic, Fire, Frost, Shock — irrelevant once you hit 100% Spell Absorption
  • Skills +10 Destruction, Alteration, Mysticism · +5 Alchemy, Conjuration, Illusion
  • Why +100 Magicka + Atronach (+150) = +250 Magicka at level 1 — massive pool to work with before you solve the regen problem
Alternative — Breton

Breton gets 50% Magic Resistance and +50 Magicka. Safer early game but weaker endgame. With 100% Spell Absorption you don't need Magic Resistance at all — it becomes redundant. The High Elf's +100 Magicka is more valuable since your pool is everything before you get sigil stones. Pick Breton only if the early game difficulty worries you.

Class — Custom: The Absorber

Why These Major Skills

Alchemy is your lifeline — it's the only way to restore magicka with Stunted Magicka. Mysticism gives you Telekinesis and Spell Absorption spells. Conjuration provides summons that you can absorb to regain magicka. Every skill here has a purpose in the absorption loop.

First Steps After the Tutorial

The Atronach sign means you leave the sewers with zero magicka regen. You need to solve this immediately or you're a mage who can't cast. Here's the exact path:

  1. Collect Every Ingredient in the Tutorial
    Before leaving the sewers, pick up every ingredient you see — Rat Meat, Bonemeal, Cairn Bolete, etc. You need these for Restore Magicka potions. Alchemy is your only way to get magicka back. Buy a Mortar and Pestle from the first vendor you find.
  2. Go to Imperial City — Buy Restore Magicka Potions
    Head straight to the Imperial City Market District. Buy every Restore Magicka potion from The Main Ingredient (alchemy shop) and Edgar's Discount Spells. Also buy ingredients with the Restore Magicka effect: Milk Thistle Seeds, Flax Seeds, Steel-Blue Entoloma Cap, Bog Beacon Asco Cap.
  3. Buy Starter Spells
    From Edgar's Discount Spells and Mystic Emporium: buy Minor Life Detection (Mysticism trainer spell — cheap to cast), Flare (Destruction), Protect (Alteration Shield), and Summon Skeleton (Conjuration). At Mystic Emporium, buy Telekinesis if available.
  4. Join the Mages Guild
    Start the recommendation quest chain immediately. You need access to the Arcane University for the Spellmaking Altar AND the Enchanting Altar. This is non-negotiable for this build. Talk to any Mages Guild hall to start. You need recommendations from all 7 cities.
  5. Visit Leyawiin Mages Guild — Buy Telekinesis
    Travel to Leyawiin. Buy Telekinesis from Agata at the Mages Guild. This Mysticism spell is the cornerstone of your magicka recovery loop. Also buy Absorb Health and any other Mysticism spells they have. Do the Leyawiin recommendation quest while here.
  6. Grind Alchemy to 25+ Immediately
    Combine every ingredient pair that shares Restore Magicka. Flax Seeds + Milk Thistle Seeds is the easiest combo. Eat individual ingredients to discover their effects. Get Alchemy to Apprentice (25) so your potions are actually useful. This is survival — not optional.
  7. Start the Main Quest for Sigil Stones
    Deliver the Amulet to Jauffre at Weynon Priory, then progress the main quest until Oblivion Gates start opening. Sigil Stones from Oblivion Gates are how you enchant your armor with Spell Absorption and Reflect Damage. You need multiple gates, so start early.
The Magicka Problem (and How to Solve It)

Stunted Magicka means zero natural regeneration, ever. Your magicka bar only refills through: 1) Restore Magicka potions (Alchemy), 2) Absorbing spells cast at you (50% base from Atronach), 3) Absorbing your own summons (Conjuration + Spell Absorption loop), 4) Welkynd Stones (one-time use, found in Ayleid ruins). Early game, carry 10+ Restore Magicka potions at all times.

The Summon + Absorb Loop (Learn This Early)

Once your Spell Absorption is high enough, you can cast Summon Skeleton, then immediately re-cast it. When the first summon is dispelled, the re-summoning spell has a chance to be absorbed (giving you magicka back). At 100% Spell Absorption, this becomes infinite magicka — every summon gives more mana than it costs. This is the core engine of the build.

Why Stunted Magicka Doesn't Matter

It sounds terrifying but the build is designed around it. At 100% Spell Absorption, every enemy spell cast at you fully restores magicka. Conjuration summons absorbed refund their cost and then some. Alchemy potions cover the early game gap. By level 15, you'll have more magicka than you can spend.

The Moment Everything Changes

When you equip that last piece of Spell Absorption gear and hit 100%, the game fundamentally changes. Magicka problems vanish forever. Enemy mages become your battery. You can stand in a room full of Lich kings and just... absorb everything they throw at you. Then nuke them with the magicka they gave you.

What Can Still Kill You?

Almost nothing. Physical damage from traps (not magical) can still hurt you, but your heavy armor + HP handles that. Falling damage still applies. Poison from Alchemy ingredients (not spells) bypasses absorption. That's about it. You are functionally immortal against any combat encounter in the game.

Enchanting — The Path to 100%

Enchanting is not optional for this build — it IS the build. Your entire endgame revolves around stacking the right enchantments across all your gear slots to reach two critical thresholds: 100% Spell Absorption and 100% Reflect Damage.

Spell Absorption — Reaching 100%

The Math

50% — The Atronach birthsign (permanent, always active)

+20% — Sigil Stone on Cuirass (level 17+ stone)

+20% — Sigil Stone on Helmet or Greaves

+10% — Sigil Stone on Ring or remaining armor piece

= 100% Total. Every spell absorbed. Complete magic immunity.

Sigil Stones from Oblivion Gates are the primary source. At level 17+, stones grant the highest magnitude enchantments. The Absorb Spell effect on sigil stones provides approximately 20% per stone at max level. You need 2-3 stones with this effect to bridge the gap from 50% to 100%.

Reflect Damage — Reaching 100%

The Math

33% — Escutcheon of Chorrol (permanent while equipped)

+20% — Sigil Stone on armor piece

+20% — Sigil Stone on another armor piece

+15% — Amulet of Axes (or enchanted amulet)

+12% — Ring of Iron Fist (or enchanted ring)

= 100% Total. Every melee hit reflected. Enemies kill themselves.

Sigil Stone Farming Guide

  1. Progress the Main Quest until Oblivion Gates spawn
    After "Breaking the Siege of Kvatch," random Oblivion Gates start appearing near cities. Each gate contains a Sigil Stone at the top of the main tower. Closing the gate gives you the stone.
  2. Reach level 17 before farming
    Sigil Stone magnitudes scale with your level. At level 17+, you get the best possible enchantment values (Transcendent tier). Stones grabbed before level 17 give weaker enchantments. Wait if you can.
  3. Save before grabbing the stone
    The enchantment on the stone is randomly determined when you interact with it. Save your game right before touching it. If you get the wrong enchantment, reload and try again. You're looking for Absorb Spell and Reflect Damage specifically.
  4. Enchant at the Arcane University or Frostcrag Spire
    Take your sigil stones to the Enchanting Altar (Chironasium at the Arcane University, or Frostcrag Spire DLC). Apply the sigil stone directly to your armor piece. Unlike normal enchanting, sigil stones don't require a filled soul gem — they ARE the power source.
  5. Repeat for every gear slot
    You need 4-6 sigil stones total: 2-3 for Spell Absorption (on armor), 2-3 for Reflect Damage (on remaining armor + accessories). Farm Oblivion Gates until you have everything.

Don't Enchant Until Level 17

Sigil stones grabbed before level 17 give weaker enchantments that you'll want to replace. Use your early sigil stones on temporary gear, or ideally, wait until level 17 to start farming gates seriously. The difference between a level 12 stone (~12% Absorption) and a level 17+ stone (~20% Absorption) is massive.

Sigil Stones vs. Normal Enchanting

Sigil stones are applied directly — no soul gem needed. Normal enchanting at the altar requires filled soul gems and known spells. For Spell Absorption and Reflect Damage, sigil stones are the only practical source since these effects aren't available as learnable spells for normal enchanting. Always use sigil stones for your core build enchantments.

Combat Rotation

Early Game (Before 100% Absorption)

  1. Cast Summon Skeleton before entering combat
    Your summon tanks while you conserve magicka. Let it take aggro first. If your Spell Absorption procs on the summon spell, you get magicka back — free army.
  2. Let enemy mages cast at you
    Stand in their line of fire deliberately. Your 50% Spell Absorption means half their spells restore your magicka instead of damaging you. This is your primary magicka source in combat.
  3. Use Absorb Health in melee
    Touch-range Mysticism spell. Drains their HP into yours — a heal and damage spell in one. More magicka-efficient than casting Destruction + Restoration separately.
  4. Drink Restore Magicka potions as needed
    Your Alchemy-brewed potions are your safety net. Pop one when magicka drops below 25%. Always have 5+ in your inventory.
  5. Block with shield + counter-attack
    Use Escutcheon of Chorrol — its 33% Reflect Damage is already working for you. Block, let them take reflected damage, then swing back.

Endgame (100% Absorption + 100% Reflect)

  1. Walk into the room
    Literally just walk in. You don't need to prepare or prebuff. You are immune to everything.
  2. Let enemies attack you
    Every spell they cast restores your magicka (100% Spell Absorption). Every melee hit they land damages them, not you (100% Reflect Damage). They are killing themselves.
  3. Cast Destruction to speed things up (optional)
    With infinite magicka from absorption, spam your strongest Destruction spells. Lightning Bolt drains their magicka too. Or just AFK and let Reflect Damage do all the work.
  4. Summon + Absorb for magicka top-off
    If you somehow need more magicka (you won't), cast Summon Skeleton then immediately re-cast it. The absorption of the conjuration spell at 100% fully refunds the cost plus extra.
The AFK Build

At endgame, you can literally put your controller down during most fights. Enemies approach you, swing their weapons, take full reflected damage, and die. Mages cast at you, you absorb it all, and they run out of magicka while your bar stays full. This is not an exaggeration — this is how the build plays at 100%/100%.

Conjuration Absorption Trick

Enemy conjurers summon creatures against you — but with 100% Spell Absorption, their conjuration spells get absorbed before the summon materializes. The enemy wastes their magicka and you gain it. Eventually enemy mages run completely dry and can only feebly punch you (which reflects back at them). It's beautiful.

Tips for the Remaster

Alchemy Ingredient Farming

Your lifeline in the early game. The best Restore Magicka combos: Flax Seeds + Milk Thistle Seeds (found everywhere in the wild), Steel-Blue Entoloma Cap + Bog Beacon Asco Cap (found in swamps near Leyawiin/Bravil), Bugloss + Dryad Saddle Polypore. Buy ingredients from The Main Ingredient (Imperial City) — they restock every 3 in-game days.

Welkynd Stone Locations

Welkynd Stones fully restore your magicka on use (one-time). Found in Ayleid Ruins throughout Cyrodiil. They glow blue and sit on pedestals. Carry 5+ as emergency backup. Best early ruins: Vilverin (right outside the tutorial exit), Lipsand Tarn (north of Imperial City), Arpenia (west of Bravil).

Spell Absorption Quirks

Spell Absorption can absorb your own area-of-effect spells if you're standing in the blast radius. This is actually beneficial — cast a fire AoE at your feet and absorb part of it for free magicka. However, it also means buff spells you cast on yourself have a chance to be absorbed (wasted). At 100%, self-buffs will always be absorbed. Use potions or enchantments for self-buffs instead of casting them.

The Self-Buff Problem at 100%

At 100% Spell Absorption, all spells targeting you are absorbed — including your own healing and buff spells. This means you cannot cast Restore Health, Shield, or Fortify spells on yourself. Solutions: 1) Use potions for healing (Alchemy), 2) Use enchanted gear for buffs (Fortify Health on armor), 3) Temporarily unequip a Spell Absorption piece to drop below 100%, cast your buff, then re-equip. This is the build's only real inconvenience.

Oblivion Gate Respawning

Random Oblivion Gates spawn near cities as the main quest progresses. After closing one, new gates can appear nearby. You can farm indefinitely for sigil stones. The most reliable gate locations are near Kvatch, Bruma, and along the Gold Road. Each gate takes about 15-20 minutes to clear.

Level Scaling Works in Your Favor

Unlike most builds where Oblivion's level scaling can punish you, the Absorber benefits from it. Higher-level enemies deal more damage — which means Reflect Damage kills them faster. Higher-level enemy mages cast more powerful spells — which means you absorb more magicka per spell. The harder the game tries to scale against you, the stronger you get.

Shivering Isles — Madness Armor

The Shivering Isles DLC (included in Remastered) has the best heavy armor in the game: Madness Armor. It's crafted, not found — bring Madness Ore (found throughout the Shivering Isles) to the smith in Crucible, New Sheoth. The armor is cosmetically unique (dark, organic-looking) and has the highest AR of any heavy armor. Enchant every piece with your sigil stones.

Poison Still Works

Spell Absorption does NOT block poison damage from Alchemy-applied poisons (only poison from spells is absorbed). Enemies with poisoned weapons can still hurt you. This is extremely rare in practice — only a handful of NPCs use applied poisons. Carry Cure Poison potions just in case, but you'll almost never need them.

Repair Your Gear

Your armor and weapons still degrade with use. Reflect Damage doesn't prevent armor condition loss — you're still getting hit, the damage is just reflected. Carry Repair Hammers and repair after every major fight. A broken piece of enchanted armor loses its enchantment effectiveness.

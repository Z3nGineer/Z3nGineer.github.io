Sorcadin — Divine Tempest

Half-Orc · Paladin 6 / Sorcerer 6 · #1 Ranked Build
#1 Build Nova Damage Frontline Half-Caster Patch 8

Overview

The Sorcadin is the undisputed #1 build in Baldur's Gate 3. By combining Paladin 6 with Sorcerer 6, you get the deadliest single-target burst in the game: Extra Attack with Divine Smite on every hit, powered by an expanded spell slot pool from Sorcerer, and Quickened Spell Metamagic to cast a full spell (like Hold Person) as a bonus action before swinging your greatsword. Nothing else in BG3 comes close to this level of burst damage.

Why It's #1

Divine Smite adds 2d8–5d8 radiant damage per hit (extra d8 vs undead/fiends). Extra Attack means you smite twice per turn. Sorcery Points give you more spell slots to fuel smites. Quickened Spell lets you Hold Person (bonus action) → Attack (action) for guaranteed critical hits, which DOUBLE all smite dice. A single Quickened Hold Person + two crit smites can deal 100+ damage in one turn.

The Math

A crit Divine Smite with a 3rd-level slot deals 8d8 radiant (doubled from 4d8) + weapon damage. Two of those in one turn = 80+ radiant damage before weapon dice. Add Great Weapon Master and you're deleting bosses in a single round. No other build in BG3 achieves this burst window.

Core Synergies

  • Smite Divine Smite — 2d8–5d8 radiant per hit, uses spell slots. Sorcerer adds more slots to burn.
  • Meta Quickened Spell — Cast Hold Person/Haste as a bonus action, then full Attack action with smites.
  • Crit Hold Person + Smite — Paralyzed = auto-crit within 3m. All smite dice doubled. Devastating.
  • Aura Aura of Protection — +CHA mod to ALL saving throws for you and nearby allies. Insanely good.

Race & Background

Best Race: Half-Orc

  • Racial Savage Attacks — On a crit, roll one extra weapon damage die. Since this build crits constantly (Hold Person), this triggers every nova round.
  • Racial Relentless Endurance — Drop to 1 HP instead of 0 once per long rest. Frontline insurance.
  • Racial Darkvision — 12m range. Useful in Act 2's perpetual darkness.

Alternative: Zariel Tiefling

  • Racial Searing Smite (free) — Free smite spell at level 3. Extra fire damage stacks with Divine Smite.
  • Racial Branding Smite (free) — Free at level 5. More smite fuel without spending slots.
  • Racial Fire Resistance — Halves fire damage. Relevant against many BG3 enemies.
Other Viable Races

Shield Dwarf — +2 CON, medium armor proficiency (redundant but Dwarven Toughness is nice). Human — carrying capacity, Civil Militia for shield proficiency (already have it). Githyanki — free Misty Step and medium armor, but you already have both from Paladin.

Background

Pick Soldier for Athletics and Intimidation proficiency, or Noble for History and Persuasion. As the party face (high CHA), you want social skill proficiencies. Soldier is the strongest pick for a frontliner who also leads in dialogue.

Why CHA is Everything

Charisma is your spellcasting modifier for BOTH Paladin and Sorcerer. It powers your spell save DC (Hold Person, Command), your Aura of Protection bonus (+CHA mod to all saves for the party), and your attack rolls if you take Pact of the Blade... wait, wrong build. The point is: CHA 16 → 20 by level 8 is non-negotiable. Every point of CHA improves your spell DC, your aura, and your social skills.

Feat Priority for Ability Scores

  • Level 4 +2 CHA → 18 — Paladin 4 feat. Better aura, better spell DC.
  • Level 8 +2 CHA → 20 — Sorcerer 2 (character level 8). Cap CHA for maximum Aura of Protection.
  • Alt L4 Great Weapon Master — If you want raw damage over CHA. +10 damage per hit, -5 attack (offset by Vow of Enmity advantage). Take CHA +2 at Sorc level instead.

You are the most versatile and deadly character in BG3.

Subclass Choices

Paladin Oath: Vengeance (Recommended)

  • L3 Vow of Enmity — Bonus action: advantage on all attacks against one creature for 1 minute. Your #1 tool for guaranteeing smite hits. Use on bosses.
  • L3 Inquisitor's Might — +2 radiant damage on weapon attacks. Small but consistent early damage bump.
  • Free Hunter's Mark + Misty Step — Always prepared. Hunter's Mark adds 1d6 per hit (stacks with smite). Misty Step for mobility.

Alternative: Oath of the Ancients

  • L3 Healing Radiance — AoE heal centered on you. Decent but wastes your bonus action (Quickened Spell wants it).
  • L7 Aura of Warding — Resistance to spell damage for you and allies. Extremely strong defensively, but you stop at Paladin 6, so you never get this. Not recommended for Sorcadin.
Oath of the Ancients Trap

Ancients is amazing for pure Paladins because Aura of Warding (level 7) is one of the best defensive features in the game. But since Sorcadin stops at Paladin 6, you NEVER get it. Vengeance's Vow of Enmity is available at level 3 and carries the whole build.

Alternative: Oathbreaker

  • L3 Spiteful Suffering — Curse a target: 1d4+CHA necrotic damage to them at start of each of YOUR turns. Decent DoT.
  • L3 Control Undead — Dominate an undead creature. Situational but powerful in Act 2.
  • Aura Aura of Hate — +CHA to melee damage for you, allies, AND nearby undead/fiends. If you run summons or have undead allies, this is very strong. Also buffs enemy undead though.

Sorcerous Origin: Draconic Bloodline (Recommended)

  • L1 Draconic Resilience — +1 HP per Sorcerer level. Extra durability on a frontliner. Base AC 13 (irrelevant in plate but the HP matters).
  • L6 Elemental Affinity — +CHA mod to fire damage spells. Makes Fireball and Fire Bolt hit significantly harder.
  • Pick Red or Gold Dragon — Fire is the most versatile element. Most enemies are NOT fire resistant (and you can use Smite for those who are).

Alternative Origin: Storm Sorcery

  • L1 Tempestuous Magic — After casting a leveled spell, fly 3m as a bonus action without provoking opportunity attacks. Nice mobility but less impactful than Draconic HP.
  • L6 Heart of the Storm — Resistance to lightning/thunder, deal bonus lightning damage when casting lightning/thunder spells. Less useful since your best spells aren't lightning-typed.

Metamagic

You choose 2 Metamagic options at Sorcerer 3. These define how the Sorcadin operates in combat. You can pick a 3rd at Sorcerer 10, but you stop at 6.

Quickened Spell 3 SP Cast a spell as a bonus action instead of an action THE Sorcadin Metamagic. Bonus action Hold Person → action Attack with auto-crit smites. Or Quickened Fireball + Attack same turn. Lets you cast AND attack every round.

Sorcery Point Economy

  • Pool 6 SP at Sorcerer 6 — That's 2 Quickened Spells per long rest, or 1 Quickened + 1 Twinned Hold Person, with points leftover.
  • Convert Slots → SP — Burn a 1st-level slot for 1 SP, 2nd-level for 2 SP, etc. In fights where you need more Quickened casts, sacrifice low slots.
  • Convert SP → Slots — 2 SP = 1st-level slot, 3 SP = 2nd-level slot, 5 SP = 3rd-level slot. Convert SP into smite fuel between fights.
Pre-Combat Setup

Before a big fight, convert any surplus 1st-level slots into sorcery points. You'll want maximum SP for Quickened Hold Person openers. After the fight, convert leftover SP back into spell slots for smite fuel. This cycling is the engine that makes Sorcadin so resource-efficient.

Combat Guide

The Nova Round — Maximum Burst

This is the sequence that makes Sorcadin the #1 build. Execute this on the first round of any boss fight.

  1. Quickened Hold Person Bonus Action
    Spend 3 sorcery points. Cast Hold Person as a bonus action on the boss (or Twinned Hold Person on two targets for 5 SP total). Target must fail a WIS save or be Paralyzed. Paralyzed = auto-crit on melee hits within 3m.
  2. First Attack + Divine Smite Action
    Move within 3m. Attack with greatsword. Auto-crit against paralyzed target. Trigger Divine Smite with a 3rd-level slot: 4d8 radiant → DOUBLED to 8d8 on crit. Total: 4d6 weapon (crit) + 8d8 radiant + STR mod = ~55 damage.
  3. Extra Attack + Divine Smite Action (Extra)
    Second attack from Extra Attack. Same auto-crit, same smite. Another ~55 damage. Running total: ~110 damage in one turn.
  4. Haste Attack (if active) Haste Action
    If Haste is active (cast last turn or by an ally), you get a third attack. Smite again. Running total: ~165 damage. Most bosses are dead.
Hold Person is Humanoid Only

Hold Person only works on humanoids. For non-humanoid bosses, use Vow of Enmity (advantage on all attacks) instead, or have a party member cast Hold Monster (4th level, Wizard/Bard). Without the auto-crit, your damage is still excellent — just not one-round-kill territory.

Standard Combat Flow

  1. Pre-combat: Haste (Twinned on self + ally) Pre-Fight
    If you see the fight coming, Twinned Haste yourself and a martial ally before combat starts. Costs 3 SP but is worth it for multi-round fights.
  2. Turn 1: Quickened Hold Person → Attack × 2 with Smite Turn 1
    The nova round described above. Target the biggest threat and delete them.
  3. Subsequent turns: Attack × 2 with Smite, Shield as needed Every Turn
    Without Hold Person, you still deal great damage. Smite on every hit if resources allow. Use Shield (reaction) when attacked. Fall back on Fire Bolt if enemies are out of reach.
  4. Cleanup: Quickened Fireball + Attack As Needed
    When multiple weaker enemies remain, Quickened Fireball (bonus action) to AoE, then charge in and clean up with melee attacks (action). Versatile.

Defensive Options

  • React Shield (+5 AC) — Reaction. Use when an attack would hit. With plate + Shield spell, your AC can reach 26+. Nearly unhittable.
  • Free Mirror Image — No concentration. Cast before combat for 3 free "misses" on incoming attacks. Stacks with everything.
  • Aura Aura of Protection — Passive +5 to all saves. Your party rarely fails saves with this active. Stay near allies.
  • Escape Misty Step — Bonus action teleport out of danger. You have multiple sources of this.

Honour Mode Tips

Honour Mode is BG3's hardest difficulty: one save file, permadeath, bosses have Legendary Actions, and enemies are smarter with better stats. The Sorcadin is arguably the best Honour Mode build because it combines overwhelming burst damage with the best defensive aura in the game.

Key Honour Mode Changes

  • Bosses Legendary Actions — Major bosses get extra actions between turns. They can break your Hold Person with a legendary save. You MUST burst them down before they use it.
  • Saves Single Save File — TPK = game over. Aura of Protection (+5 to all saves) is your insurance policy. Shield (+5 AC reaction) prevents the hits that kill.
  • AI Smarter Enemies — Enemies target your squishiest party members, focus fire, and use abilities intelligently. Position your Sorcadin to protect the backline with Aura range.
  • Stats Buffed Enemies — Higher HP, better saves, more damage. This makes one-round kills harder but also makes your burst MORE valuable (kill them before they kill you).

Honour Mode Boss Strategy

  1. Identify if boss is humanoid Before Fight
    Hold Person only works on humanoids. Ketheric, Orin, Gortash = humanoid (Hold Person works). Ansur, Myrkul, Netherbrain = not humanoid (use Vow of Enmity instead).
  2. Pre-buff before initiating Before Fight
    Mirror Image (no concentration), Aid for extra HP, Longstrider for movement. If possible, have Gale cast Haste on you out of combat.
  3. Nova round 1 — all-in burst Turn 1
    Quickened Hold Person + double smite crit. Spend your highest slots. In Honour Mode, bosses have legendary actions — they might break free of Hold Person on their next turn. You need maximum damage NOW.
  4. Counterspell enemy spells Reaction
    Many Honour Mode bosses have devastating spells (Ketheric's Finger of Death, etc). Counterspell is your safety net. Hold your reaction for it when facing casters.
  5. Fall back on Aura + Shield for survival Every Turn
    If the fight goes long, your passive Aura of Protection and Shield reaction keep the party alive. You don't need to win in one round — you just need to survive long enough for a second nova window.

Hardest Honour Mode Fights — Sorcadin Approach

  • Act 1 Grym (Adamantine Forge) — Immune to most damage except forge hammer. Don't waste smite slots. Lure onto platform, pull lever. Save your nova for meaningful fights.
  • Act 2 Ketheric Thorm — Humanoid form 1 = Hold Person + smite. Second phase as Apostle of Myrkul = not humanoid. Use Vow of Enmity + radiant smites (extra d8 vs undead).
  • Act 3 Raphael (House of Hope) — Fiend, not humanoid. Legendary Actions. Extremely dangerous. Vow of Enmity for advantage, smite hard (extra d8 vs fiends). Counterspell his spells. Bring Scroll of Globe of Invulnerability.
  • Act 3 Orin the Red — Humanoid. Hold Person works. She's squishy but has Unstoppable legendary action. Double smite crit should kill her before she uses it.
  • Final The Netherbrain — Not humanoid. Massive HP. Use Haste + Vow of Enmity + smite every attack. Radiant damage is effective here. Fireball for adds. This is a war of attrition — Aura of Protection keeps the party up.
Honour Mode Golden Rule

Never fight fair. Pre-buff. Ambush. Use environmental advantages. The Sorcadin's burst means most fights end in round 1–2 before enemies get their Legendary Actions. That's the point: kill them before they kill you.

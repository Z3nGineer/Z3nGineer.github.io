Nightblade

Breton · The Shadow · Blade + Illusion + Sneak
Magic Assassin Nightblade Stealth Remastered 2025

Overview

The Nightblade is the magical assassin — a shadow operative who uses Illusion magic instead of poisons. Where a mundane assassin relies on arrows and alchemy, the Nightblade controls the battlefield with Paralyze, Calm, Frenzy, and Invisibility. Sneak behind enemies to deliver devastating 6x dagger backstabs, then vanish before anyone knows you were there. When things go sideways, Calm resets combat instantly so you can re-stealth and try again.

Breton is the perfect race. 50% innate Magic Resistance means enemy mages can't touch you, and the extra Magicka fuels your Illusion spells. Combined with The Shadow birthsign's daily Invisibility power, you have a guaranteed escape button from level 1.

Why Illusion Over Poisons?

Paralyze has no saving throw. Unlike Alchemy poisons that can be resisted, Illusion's Paralyze spell at Expert rank is a guaranteed disable on any enemy in the game. Calm instantly exits combat, letting you re-stealth for another sneak attack. Frenzy turns a room against itself. This build doesn't fight fair — it doesn't have to.

Race — Breton

  • Passive 50% Magic Resistance — best defensive racial in the game, permanent. Enemy mages are a non-issue.
  • Passive +50 Magicka — fuels your Illusion and Mysticism spells from the start
  • Daily Dragon Skin — 50% physical damage shield for 60 seconds. Emergency panic button.
  • Skills +10 Conjuration, Mysticism, Restoration · +5 Alchemy, Alteration, Illusion

Class — Nightblade (Custom)

  • Spec Stealth
  • Favored Agility, Willpower
  • Major Blade, Sneak, Light Armor, Illusion, Mysticism, Destruction, Acrobatics
Why Custom Over Pre-Made?

The pre-made Nightblade class uses Alteration and Restoration instead of Destruction and Acrobatics. Our custom version is better for a pure assassin: Destruction gives you a ranged finisher, and Acrobatics lets you escape by jumping over enemies. You don't need Alteration when you have Invisibility and Paralyze.

First Steps After the Tutorial

You emerge from the sewers as a walking shadow. Here is exactly what to do:

  1. Use your Shadow birthsign power immediately
    The Shadow gives you a daily Invisibility power (60 seconds). Test it right outside the sewers. This is your "get out of jail free" card for the entire game. Use it once per day when you're outmatched or need to bypass something dangerous.
  2. Go to Bravil Mages Guild — buy Illusion spells
    Travel to Bravil. At the Mages Guild, buy Starlight (Night-Eye) and Soothing Touch (Calm) from Ita Rienus. Also pick up Chameleon if you can afford it. These are your bread and butter.
  3. Buy Detect Life from Mysticism trainers
    Visit Leyawiin Mages Guild and buy Detect Life from Dagail or Agata. This spell reveals enemies through walls — essential for scouting before you commit to a room.
  4. Join the Dark Brotherhood
    Murder an innocent NPC (a bandit at a camp works). Sleep in any bed. Lucien Lachance will visit you with an invitation. The Dark Brotherhood questline gives you the Blade of Woe and Shrouded Armor — both perfect for this build. Start this early.
  5. Start the Mages Guild recommendation chain
    Visit any Mages Guild hall to begin recommendations. You need access to the Arcane University for custom spellmaking later. Do these between Dark Brotherhood contracts.
  6. Steal a horse — or earn one
    Deliver the Amulet of Kings to Jauffre at Weynon Priory for a free horse. Or just steal one from the Imperial City Stables at night (you're an assassin — act like one).
Level Illusion by Casting Constantly

In the remaster, higher-cost spells give more XP. Cast your most expensive Illusion spell whenever you have spare Magicka. Calm on friendly NPCs, Charm on merchants, Starlight while exploring — every cast levels the skill. Rush Illusion to Expert (75) for Paralyze.

Leveling — Virtue System

The remaster replaced the old attribute multiplier system. At each level-up you get 12 Virtues to distribute freely. Max 5 per attribute, max 3 attributes per level. Luck costs 4 per point.

Paralyze Has No Counter

Unlike Calm (which can be resisted by high-level enemies), Paralyze in Oblivion has no saving throw. If the spell hits, the target is frozen. Period. This makes it the strongest crowd-control ability in the entire game, and it belongs to the Illusion school — your specialty.

Illusion Mastery

Illusion is the heart and soul of this build. Every spell in this school serves a tactical purpose. Master it, and you become untouchable.

Paralyze — The Ultimate Disable

Expert Illusion (Skill 75)

Paralyze has no saving throw. If the spell connects, the target is frozen in place for the duration. No resistance check, no magic resist, no level scaling. A level 1 Nightblade with Paralyze could freeze Mankar Camoran. This is the single most powerful crowd-control ability in Oblivion and the reason Illusion Expert is your #1 priority.

Craft a custom Paralyze Touch 3 sec at the Spellmaking Altar — it costs a fraction of the vendor version's Magicka and works just as well. Follow with power attacks while the target is helpless.

Calm — Instant Combat Reset

Calm forces an enemy to stop fighting and sheathe their weapon. The moment combat ends, you can crouch and re-enter stealth. This means every failed sneak attack gets a second chance. Cast Calm, crouch, wait for the eye to close, backstab again. Repeat until the enemy is dead. Against groups, Calm one enemy while you deal with the others.

Frenzy — Turn Enemies Against Each Other

Frenzy makes a target attack the nearest creature — friend or foe. In a dungeon with multiple enemies, cast Frenzy on one from stealth. The room erupts into infighting. You stay hidden, wait for the dust to settle, and execute the wounded survivor. Zero risk, maximum reward.

Invisibility — Superior to Chameleon (Mostly)

Invisibility makes you completely undetectable but breaks on any action (attacking, opening doors, casting another spell). Chameleon reduces your visibility percentage but lets you act freely. For assassination, Invisibility is better — it guarantees the first strike. Use Chameleon for sustained stealth (pickpocketing, bypassing entire dungeons).

100% Chameleon = God Mode

If you stack Chameleon effects on enchanted gear to reach 100%, you become permanently invisible AND can still attack. Ring of Khajiiti gives 35%. Enchant a helmet, cuirass, greaves, and boots with Chameleon to cover the remaining 65%. At 100%, nothing in the game can detect you — ever.

Detect Life — Scout Through Walls

Detect Life (Mysticism school, but critical to this build) reveals all living creatures within a large radius as glowing auras — visible through walls, floors, and doors. Cast it before entering any room. Know exactly how many enemies are inside, where they're standing, and whether they're moving or stationary. Plan your approach, choose your target, and execute flawlessly.

The Illusion Assassin's Toolkit

Detect Life → scout the room. Invisibility → walk in unseen. Backstab → 8x dagger damage. Paralyze → freeze survivors. Calm → reset combat if needed. Frenzy → make them fight each other. You have an answer for every situation.

Combat Guide

The Nightblade Kill Chain

  1. Cast Detect Life to scout the room
    Before entering any space, cast Detect Life. Count the enemies, note their positions and patrol routes. Identify isolated targets. If the room is too crowded, plan your Frenzy or Calm strategy before going in.
  2. Cast Invisibility and approach your target
    Full Invisibility lets you walk right up to any enemy unseen. Position yourself directly behind them for the backstab angle. If you have 100% Chameleon from enchantments, skip this step — you're always invisible.
  3. Dagger sneak attack — 6x or 8x damage
    At Sneak Journeyman (50) you deal 6x damage; at Expert (75) it's 8x. With a Glass Dagger, this one-shots most enemies. Use a power attack from stealth for even more damage. This is your primary kill method.
  4. Paralyze any survivors
    If the target survives the backstab, immediately cast Paralyze Touch. They freeze in place — no saving throw. Walk behind them and deliver another power attack while they're helpless. Repeat until dead.
  5. Finish with Destruction at range
    If an enemy is low HP and running, hit them with Lightning Bolt or Flare. Shock drains their Magicka too, preventing enemy mages from retaliating. Don't chase — let spells do the work.
  6. Calm if overwhelmed — reset to stealth
    If you're detected and multiple enemies are closing in, cast Calm on the most dangerous one. They stop fighting and sheathe their weapon. Immediately crouch to re-enter stealth. The Calmed enemy forgets you exist. Now you're back to step 1.
  7. Re-stealth and repeat the cycle
    The beauty of this build: every fight is a controlled assassination. If anything goes wrong, Calm resets the encounter. You never have a "fair fight" unless you choose to. The cycle is: Detect → Approach → Strike → Control → Repeat.
Group Fights — The Frenzy Strategy

When facing 3+ enemies, don't fight them yourself. Cast Frenzy on the strongest enemy from stealth. They attack the nearest ally. The room erupts into chaos while you stay invisible. Wait for the fighting to end, then execute the battered survivor with a backstab. This works on any group in the game, including Oblivion gate defenders.

Boss Fights — The Paralyze Lock

Against a single powerful enemy: Paralyze → Power Attack → Paralyze → Power Attack. The target never gets to swing. With enough Magicka (stack Intelligence), you can chain Paralyze indefinitely. The boss dies having never taken a single action. This trivializes every boss in the game, including the final one.

Tips — Remastered Changes

Agility Governs Daggers (New in Remastered)

In the original Oblivion, Strength governed all melee damage. In Remastered, Agility governs dagger damage. This is a massive buff to Nightblades — you were already stacking Agility for Sneak, and now it also boosts your primary weapon. No wasted stats.

Magic XP Scales with Cost

In Remastered, higher-cost spells give more XP toward leveling that school. This means expensive Illusion spells like Invisibility and Paralyze level your Illusion skill faster than cheap ones like Starlight. Cast your most expensive available spell whenever you have Magicka to spare.

Calm, Paralyze, and Frenzy Are Unchanged

These three spells work identically to the original game. Paralyze still has no saving throw. Calm still instantly resets combat. Frenzy still makes enemies attack each other. The Remastered version didn't nerf any of these. The Nightblade is just as broken as it was in 2006.

Join Both Dark Brotherhood and Thieves Guild

The Dark Brotherhood gives you the Blade of Woe, Shrouded Armor, and access to assassination contracts for gold. The Thieves Guild gives you the Boots of Springheel Jak and access to fences for selling stolen goods. Both questlines are built for this character — do them both.

Sleep to Level Up

When the game says "you should rest and meditate on what you've learned," you need to sleep in any bed to trigger the level-up screen. Consider your timing — the world scales with your level. If you're steamrolling everything, delay sleeping. If you need more Magicka for bigger spells, level up for the Virtue points.

Don't Get Caught Stealing in the Mages Guild

You need Mages Guild access for the Spellmaking Altar. Getting expelled means no custom Paralyze spell until you pay fines and do a penance quest. Steal from everyone else — just not the Mages Guild.

Enchanting is Your Endgame

The Nightblade's endgame is 100% Chameleon through enchanted gear. Ring of Khajiiti gives 35%. You need 65% more spread across helmet, cuirass, greaves, boots, and amulet. Use Grand Soul Gems for the strongest enchantments. With 100% Chameleon, you can attack without breaking stealth — permanent invisibility with full combat capability.

Alchemy: Invisibility Potions as Backup

Even without investing in Alchemy, you can brew Invisibility potions from Bloodgrass + Nirnroot. These serve as backup Invisibility when your Magicka is depleted. Pick up Nirnroot whenever you find it near water — it glows and hums. Carry 5-10 Invisibility potions at all times as an insurance policy.

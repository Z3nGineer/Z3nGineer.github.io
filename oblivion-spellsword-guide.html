Breton Spellsword

Breton · The Mage · Blade + Destruction + Heavy Armor
Melee + Magic Spellsword Tank Remastered 2025

Overview

The Breton Spellsword is the most versatile build in Oblivion Remastered. 50% innate magic resistance, +50 Magicka, full heavy armor, and devastating destruction spells. Open with fire at range, close with a blade, and heal between swings. The remastered leveling system (Virtues) means no more micromanaging — just play.

Remastered Changes That Matter

No more efficient leveling. The Virtue system gives you 12 free attribute points per level-up. Endurance HP is retroactive. Magicka regen is based on Willpower only. You can just play the game.

Race — Breton

  • Passive 50% Magic Resistance — best defensive racial in the game, permanent
  • Passive +50 Magicka — permanent bonus to your mana pool
  • Daily Dragon Skin — 50% physical damage shield for 60 seconds. Save for tough fights.
  • Birthsign The Mage — +100 Magicka. Massive spell pool from the start.
  • Skills +10 Conjuration, Mysticism, Restoration · +5 Alchemy, Alteration, Illusion

Class — Spellsword (Pre-Made)

  • Spec Magic
  • Favored Willpower, Endurance
  • Major Blade, Block, Heavy Armor, Destruction, Restoration, Alteration, Illusion
Custom Class Alternative

If you want summons, swap Illusion for Conjuration. Summoned Daedra tank for you while you cast. Both work great — the pre-made class is solid for a first playthrough.

First Steps After the Tutorial

Once you leave the sewers, the whole world opens up. Here's exactly what to do in order:

  1. Go to the Imperial City Market District
    Fast travel to the Imperial City. Visit Edgar's Discount Spells and Mystic Emporium to buy Snowball, Protect, and Open Very Easy Lock. Also visit "A Fighting Chance" to buy starter heavy armor if you don't have any.
  2. Join the Mages Guild
    Go to the Arcane University (southeast Imperial City). You can't enter yet — talk to any Mages Guild hall in any city to start the recommendation quest chain. You need recommendations from all 7 cities to access the Arcane University and its Spellmaking Altar. Start this early, do them between other quests.
  3. Travel to Skingrad Mages Guild
    This is your one-stop spell shop. Buy Flare and Snowball from Druja, and Frost Touch from Sulinus Vassinus. Do Skingrad's Mages Guild recommendation quest while you're here.
  4. Get Chillrend in Chorrol
    Travel to Chorrol and do the quest "The Killing Field" (talk to Valus Odiil at the Grey Mare inn). Keep both his sons alive during the fight. Reward: Chillrend — your best weapon for the first 20 levels.
  5. Start the Main Quest... or don't
    Deliver the Amulet of Kings to Jauffre at Weynon Priory (near Chorrol). This progresses the main story and gives you a free horse. Or ignore it entirely and explore — the world doesn't scale to pressure you. Do what's fun.
Level Up Your Skills While Traveling

While walking between cities, spam Heal Minor Wounds to level Restoration, and cast Flare at nothing to level Destruction. In the remaster, higher-cost spells give more XP, so use your most expensive spell when grinding. Your skills level just by using them — never stop casting.

Leveling — Virtue System

The remaster replaced the old attribute multiplier system. At each level-up you get 12 Virtues to distribute freely. Max 5 per attribute, max 3 attributes per level. Luck costs 4 per point.

AttributePriorityWhy
EnduranceMAX FIRSTHealth + HP regen. At 45 you need this higher — invest heavily.
WillpowerMAX FIRSTMagicka regen (quadratic scaling). Already at 61 — keep pushing to 100.
StrengthSECONDMelee damage + carry weight + contributes to max HP. At 44, needs work.
IntelligenceSECONDMax Magicka pool. Already at 67, solid. Less urgent than Willpower since regen matters more.
AgilityLATERFatigue pool. Needed for power attacks. At 50, decent for now.
SpeedLATERMovement speed. At 33, you're slow — bump this eventually.
LuckSKIPCosts 4 Virtues per point. Already set to 60 — no need to invest further.
Suggested Distribution

Levels 2–8: Endurance 5, Willpower 5, Strength 2

Levels 9–15: Strength 5, Intelligence 5, Agility 2

Levels 16+: Fill remaining attributes. Speed, then whatever needs topping off.

Enchanting Your Gear

Enchanting lets you add magic effects to your weapons and armor. As a Spellsword, this is where your build goes from strong to broken — caster stats on heavy armor means you never have to choose between defense and magicka.

Where to Enchant

Enchanting Altars

Arcane University — in the Chironasium building, inside the university grounds. Requires all 7 Mages Guild recommendations.

Frostcrag Spire — your own personal tower (DLC). Has an enchanting altar you can use anytime without going to the university.

What You Need

  1. A filled Soul Gem
    Soul gems hold the energy that powers enchantments. You need to fill them by killing a creature while a Soul Trap effect is active on it. Either cast the Soul Trap spell (buy from Mages Guild vendors) or use a weapon with Soul Trap (like Umbra). Bigger souls = stronger enchantments. Grand Soul Gems with Grand Souls give the best results.
  2. The item you want to enchant
    Any unenchanted weapon, armor piece, ring, amulet, or clothing. You cannot enchant items that already have an enchantment. Your Dwarven armor pieces are perfect candidates.
  3. A spell you already know
    The enchantment options at the altar are based on spells you've learned. If you want to enchant armor with Fortify Magicka, you need to know a Fortify Magicka spell first. Buy spells from Mages Guild vendors to unlock more enchantment options.

Don't Waste Grand Souls

Grand Soul Gems are rare and expensive. Don't use them on low-level gear you'll replace soon. Save them for your endgame armor (Ebony/Daedric) or Goldbrand. Use Petty/Lesser gems to practice and enchant throwaway gear.

Pro Tip — Umbra for Easy Souls

The sword Umbra has built-in Soul Trap. Every kill automatically fills a soul gem in your inventory. Carry empty soul gems and use Umbra to stock up passively while you quest. You can get Umbra at level 1 from Vindasel ruins.

Combat Guide

The Spellsword Loop

  1. Open with Destruction at range
    Fire a Lightning Bolt or Fireball as enemies approach. Higher-cost spells give more skill XP in the remaster. Lead with your strongest.
  2. Cast Shield + buff before they reach you
    Alteration Shield spell reduces incoming damage. Cast Fortify Strength if you have it. These stack with your heavy armor.
  3. Paralyze Touch → Power Attack
    Custom Paralyze Touch (1-2 sec) knocks them flat. Immediately follow with a power attack while they're on the ground. This is your kill combo.
  4. Block → Counter → Heal
    Block incoming attacks (Souls-like in the remaster). Counter-attack between their swings. Cast Restoration heals between melee exchanges. You can't cast while blocking.
  5. Dragon Skin for emergencies
    Daily power: 50% physical damage absorption for 60 seconds. Save for boss fights or when overwhelmed by multiple enemies.
Fatigue Management (New in Remaster)

Power attacks and sprinting cost Fatigue. If Fatigue is depleted, you can be staggered through your block. Keep Restore Fatigue spells or potions ready. Don't spam power attacks — use them strategically after Paralyze or when the enemy is mid-swing.

Why Shock is Best

Shock damage drains enemy Magicka in addition to Health. Against enemy mages (who are your biggest threat with 50% magic resist), shock removes their ability to cast. Combined with Spellbreaker's 30% Reflect Spell, mages become trivial.

Spellmaking

Custom spells are where the Spellsword build becomes truly broken. You need access to the Spellmaking altar.

How to Unlock Spellmaking

Option A: Complete all 7 Mages Guild recommendation quests → gain Arcane University access → use the Altar of Spellmaking there.

Option B (shortcut): If you have the Frostcrag Spire DLC (included in Remastered), buy Magetallow Candles from Aurelinwae at the Mystic Emporium (Imperial City Market District). Bring them to Frostcrag Spire to activate its altar. No Mages Guild needed.

Tips for New Players

The World Levels With You

Oblivion uses level scaling — enemies get stronger as you level up. This means there's no rush to level. Take your time exploring and don't worry about falling behind. If anything, leveling too fast without good gear can make things harder. The Virtue system in the remaster makes this much less punishing than the original game.

Repair Your Gear

Weapons and armor degrade with use. Carry Repair Hammers (buy them from any blacksmith) and repair between fights. A broken weapon does almost no damage. You can also pay blacksmiths to repair for you, but hammers are cheaper long-term.

Sleep to Level Up

Unlike most RPGs, you don't level up automatically. When the game says "you should rest and meditate on what you've learned," you need to sleep in any bed to trigger the level-up screen and spend your Virtues. Any bed works — inns, your house, bedrolls in dungeons.

Buy a House

Houses give you free storage (chests don't reset) and a bed to level up anytime. The cheapest is in Imperial City Waterfront (Shack, 2,000 gold) — talk to Vinicia Melissaeia in the Office of Imperial Commerce. The best value is Benirus Manor in Anvil (free after completing the "Where Spirits Have Lease" quest).

Alchemy is Broken (In a Good Way)

Even without investing skill points, Alchemy lets you make Restore Health and Restore Magicka potions from common ingredients. Pick up every ingredient you find. Buy a Mortar and Pestle from any alchemist. Combine 2 ingredients that share an effect. Free potions = infinite sustain.

Don't Steal in the Mages Guild

If you're caught stealing or attacking Mages Guild members, you get expelled and lose access to spell vendors and the Spellmaking Altar until you pay a fine and do a penance quest. Not worth it. Steal from everyone else, just not the Mages Guild.

Enchanting Your Gear

Once you have access to the Arcane University (or Frostcrag Spire), you can enchant your own gear. The best enchantments for a Spellsword are Fortify Magicka, Fortify Willpower, and Resist Magic. You need filled Soul Gems — kill creatures with a weapon enchanted with Soul Trap (Umbra does this automatically) to fill them.

Fast Travel Exists

Click any discovered location on your map to fast travel there instantly. You discover locations by walking near them or by completing quests. Fast travel is free but time passes during travel, so your daily power (Dragon Skin) may reset. You can also use the fast travel posts between cities.

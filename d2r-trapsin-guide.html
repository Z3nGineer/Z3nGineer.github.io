Lightning Sentry Assassin

Assassin · #5 Ranked · Drop Traps, Walk Away, Collect Loot
Lightning Fire Physical CE Assassin Season 13 Beginner Friendly

Overview

The Trapsin is the most laid-back build in Diablo 2 Resurrected. Drop a cluster of Lightning Sentries, place a Death Sentry for Corpse Explosion chains, and walk away while your traps annihilate everything on screen. The Assassin doesn't need to be in the fight — her traps are autonomous turrets that fire independently. You're a trap engineer, not a fighter.

Three damage types make this build nearly skip-proof. Lightning Sentry handles the majority of content. Death Sentry chains Corpse Explosion (physical damage — bypasses all resistances) off the first kill. Fire Blast covers lightning immunes as a fallback. Almost nothing in the game resists all three.

Patch 2.6 Trap Buff

Patch 2.6 introduced -enemy resistance scaling for trap skills. As your trap skill level increases, enemy lightning resistance is reduced. This was a massive quality-of-life buff that pushed Trapsin from niche to top-tier. Higher skill levels now double-dip: more damage and lower enemy resistance.

Why Trapsin?

  • Playstyle Fully Automated — traps fire on their own, you just place and move
  • Damage 3 Types — lightning + fire + physical CE = almost nothing is immune
  • Safety Off-Screen Kills — traps do the fighting, you stay behind them
  • Budget Spirit + Spirit — functions on zero-cost runewords, scales with investment
  • Farming Key Runner — one of the best Countess, Summoner, Nihlathak farmers
  • Patch 2.6 -Enemy Res — trap skills now reduce enemy lightning resistance at high levels
Beginner Friendly

Trapsin is one of the easiest builds to learn in D2R. You don't need to manage attack speed, positioning, or complex skill rotations. Drop traps, let them work. The only "skill" is knowing where to place them. Budget gear (double Spirit) carries you through Hell difficulty.

Prerequisite Points

You'll spend a few points on prerequisites to reach Lightning Sentry, Death Sentry, Mind Blast, Burst of Speed, Fade, and Shadow Master. These are 1-point investments in skills like Claw Mastery, Psychic Hammer, Cloak of Shadows, Weapon Block, and Shadow Warrior. Plan for roughly 8-10 prerequisite points.

Synergy Math

Lightning Sentry benefits from synergies: Charged Bolt Sentry and Shock Web each add +12% lightning damage per hard point. At 20/20 synergies, that's +480% bonus damage. This is why maxing both synergies before Fire Blast is critical — the damage scaling is enormous.

Shield vs. Dual Claw

Spirit Monarch (156 STR) is the standard choice — +2 skills, 35% FCR, mana, and resistances. Dual +3 Traps claws give higher skill levels but no block and require less STR. Budget builds always go Spirit. Endgame min-maxers may prefer claws for raw damage.

Level Progression

Click to expand each phase. The Trapsin has one of the smoothest leveling curves in D2R.

Lvl 1–24 Normal — Fire Blast Leveling Fire Phase ▶
Fire Blast Wake of Fire Stealth Armor Leaf Staff

You don't get Lightning Sentry until level 24. Until then, Fire Blast is your primary skill. Max it first — it does solid fire damage and will become your fire filler later anyway.

Wake of Fire (level 12) is an excellent leveling skill. One point is enough early, but you can invest a few more for speed. These points won't be wasted since WoF synergizes with Fire Blast.

At level 24, put your first point in Lightning Sentry. If the fire leveling feels slow, use your free Normal respec (Akara) to redistribute into the lightning tree. Most players respec at 24.

Leveling Gear

Stealth (Tal + Eth) in 2-socket armor — 25% FCR, FHR, run/walk. Leaf (Tir + Ral) in 2-socket staff — +3 Fire Skills, huge Fire Blast boost. Both are level 17+ runewords and trivially farmable.

Lvl 24–60 Nightmare — Lightning Sentry Online Core Build ▶
Lightning Sentry Death Sentry Spirit Sword Spirit Monarch Lore Helm

After respec at 24, Lightning Sentry becomes your primary. Max it first. Put 1 point in Death Sentry at level 30 — even at level 1, the Corpse Explosion component is based on monster corpse HP, so it's immediately powerful.

Establish the core playstyle: drop 3-5 Lightning Sentries, place 1 Death Sentry behind them, watch the chain reaction. Pick up 1 point in Burst of Speed, Fade, Mind Blast, and Shadow Master as they become available.

After maxing Lightning Sentry, start pumping Charged Bolt Sentry (synergy) for the +12% per level damage boost.

Spirit + Spirit

Farm a Crystal Sword in Normal Cows (4 sockets) and a Monarch shield in Hell (4 sockets). Tal + Thul + Ort + Amn in each. Double Spirit gives +4 All Skills, 70% FCR, massive mana. This is your weapon setup until endgame.

Lvl 60–90+ Hell — Three Damage Types Endgame ▶
Full Synergies Fire Blast Filler Death Sentry CE Chains Enigma / HotO

By Hell, all four 20-point investments should be done (LS, DS, CBS, Shock Web). Remaining points go into Fire Blast for dealing with lightning immunes.

The three damage types handle almost everything in Hell:

  • Lightning 90% of enemies — Lightning Sentry melts non-immunes, -res from Patch 2.6 helps
  • Physical Corpse Explosion — Death Sentry CE is physical + fire, ignores most resistances
  • Fire Lightning Immunes — Fire Blast handles the ~10% of monsters immune to lightning

With Infinity on your mercenary, most lightning immunes get broken, making Fire Blast nearly unnecessary except for a handful of monsters.

FCR / Trap Laying Speed Breakpoints

Unique to the Assassin: trap laying speed scales with Faster Cast Rate (FCR), not IAS. This is one of the most important mechanics to understand. More FCR = faster trap placement = faster kill speed. Hit these breakpoints.

Target: 102% FCR

The 102% breakpoint is the sweet spot for endgame Trapsin. With HotO (40%), Spirit Monarch (35%), Arachnid Mesh (20%), and a 10% FCR ring, you hit exactly 105%. This gives you the fastest practical trap laying speed — the 174% breakpoint is unrealistic to hit without sacrificing critical stats.

IAS Does Not Affect Traps

Increased Attack Speed (IAS) does nothing for trap laying speed. Don't waste gear slots on IAS. FCR is the only stat that speeds up trap placement. However, IAS does affect your regular melee attacks (irrelevant for this build) and kick attacks (also irrelevant).

Enigma is Not Optional

The Assassin has no teleport skill. Without Enigma, you're walking everywhere. In a game where Sorceress teleports across the map in seconds, walking is a massive efficiency loss. Enigma is the single most impactful upgrade for Trapsin. Prioritize it over every other piece of gear. Jah + Ith + Ber is expensive, but it transforms the build.

Dual Claw Alternative

For max damage, dual +3 Traps / +3 Lightning Sentry magic claws give +6 to traps (vs. +5 from HotO + Spirit). Shop from Anya in Act 5 Hell. The downside: no FCR from weapons, no Spirit shield stats, and you lose block chance. This is a glass cannon setup for experienced players who want peak trap damage.

Death Sentry — The Force Multiplier

Death Sentry is the reason Trapsin clears entire screens in seconds. Understanding its Corpse Explosion mechanic is the difference between a good Trapsin and a great one.

How Corpse Explosion Works

Lightning Sentry kills the first monster. That corpse is now fuel. Death Sentry's Corpse Explosion detonates it, dealing 40-80% of the corpse's maximum HP as damage in an area of effect. Half physical, half fire.

The explosion kills nearby monsters. Those new corpses also explode. Chain reaction. One kill becomes ten. Ten becomes the entire screen.

Corpse Explosion damage scales with monster HP, not your stats. In Hell difficulty where monsters have massive HP pools, CE deals enormous damage. A single corpse from a high-HP monster can one-shot everything around it.

The Chain Reaction

1. Drop Lightning Sentries into a pack → first monster dies

2. Death Sentry detonates the corpse → AoE damage kills nearby monsters

3. New corpses created → Death Sentry detonates those too

4. Chain reaction continues until the screen is clear

This is why Trapsin feels "automated" — you set up the initial kill condition, and the chain handles the rest. Dense packs melt fastest because more corpses = more chain reactions.

Why Max Death Sentry?

Higher levels of Death Sentry increase both the lightning bolt damage (it fires lightning in addition to CE) and the Corpse Explosion radius. Larger radius = more monsters caught in each explosion = faster chains. At level 20+, the CE radius covers most of the screen. The lightning bolts also serve as supplementary damage while waiting for the first corpse.

Death Sentry vs. Lightning Immunes

CE deals half physical, half fire damage — not lightning. This means Death Sentry's Corpse Explosion works against lightning immune monsters. If your Lightning Sentries can kill even one non-immune monster in a mixed pack, Death Sentry's CE chain will clear the lightning immunes through physical/fire splash damage. This is the build's secret weapon against immunities.

Infinity + Traps: How It Actually Works

There's a common misconception: Infinity's Conviction aura on your mercenary applies to all enemies in range, lowering their lightning resistance. Since traps deal lightning damage, they benefit from the resistance reduction even though the aura comes from the merc, not the traps. However, Conviction cannot break all lightning immunes — only those with less than ~117% base resistance. Monsters with very high innate immunity (like Gloams) remain immune. That's where Fire Blast and Death Sentry CE come in.

Playstyle

The Trapsin rotation is the simplest in D2R. Drop, wait, move. Here's the step-by-step for clearing any area:

  1. Teleport into a pack (Enigma)
    Use Enigma's Teleport to position yourself near a dense monster pack. Without Enigma, Burst of Speed + run. Position so traps can cover the largest cluster.
  2. Drop 3–5 Lightning Sentries
    Place them in a tight cluster near the center of the pack. Traps fire autonomously — you don't need to target anything. 5 trap limit total (LS + DS combined). Typical setup: 3-4 LS + 1-2 DS.
  3. Place 1–2 Death Sentries
    Drop Death Sentries slightly behind your Lightning Sentries. They'll fire lightning bolts until the first corpse appears, then switch to Corpse Explosion. Place them where corpses will be — where the pack is dying.
  4. First corpse triggers CE chain
    Lightning Sentry kills the first monster. Death Sentry immediately detonates the corpse. The chain reaction begins. Dense packs evaporate in 1-2 seconds. You don't need to do anything — just watch.
  5. Move to the next pack
    Teleport ahead while your current traps finish cleaning up. Start placing new traps before the old ones expire. Efficient Trapsin gameplay is a leapfrog pattern — always one pack ahead.
  6. Fire Blast on lightning immunes
    If you encounter lightning immune monsters that Conviction doesn't break: spam Fire Blast manually. It's your only "active" skill. Kill one immune, let Death Sentry CE chain the rest. Mind Blast to stun if they're dangerous.
Burst of Speed vs. Fade

Burst of Speed is your default buff — faster run/walk and faster trap laying (even though it's technically attack speed, it does affect trap animation). Use it 95% of the time.

Fade for dangerous areas — Chaos Sanctuary, Uber Tristram, anywhere you're taking big hits. The All Resistances and Physical Damage Reduction can save your life. They don't stack — one replaces the other.

Farming Spots

Trapsin excels at key farming and targeted runs. The automated playstyle means less fatigue during long farming sessions.

Chaos Sanctuary
Trapsin's best area. Dense packs, three seal bosses. Drop traps at each seal, let CE chain through the room. Diablo kill is straightforward with Fade + Shadow Master tanking.
Countess (Key of Terror)
Tower levels are narrow corridors — perfect for traps. Place LS at chokepoints, everything walks into them. Countess herself is fire immune, use LS + CE.
Summoner (Key of Hate)
Arcane Sanctuary pathways force enemies into single-file lines. Trap the narrow bridges, enemies can't avoid them. Summoner dies to any damage.
Nihlathak (Key of Destruction)
Halls of Vaught. Trapsin is one of the safest Nihlathak runners — traps kill from range, avoiding his Corpse Explosion. Place traps before engaging.
Terror Zones
Any Terror Zone with dense monster packs. The automated kill speed means you can farm TZs efficiently regardless of the zone layout. CE chains scale with monster HP (higher in TZs).
Worldstone Keep / Baal
Dense WSK packs are perfect for trap clusters. Each wave in Baal's throne room is a CE chain waiting to happen. Drop traps at the spawn point, let waves walk into death.
Key Farming Efficiency

Trapsin is arguably the best overall key farmer in D2R. She handles all three key bosses efficiently: Countess (lightning), Summoner (anything), Nihlathak (safe range). Most builds excel at one or two — Trapsin does all three. If you're farming Uber keys, this is the build.

Tips & Tricks

5 Trap Limit

You can only have 5 traps active at once (combined Lightning Sentry + Death Sentry). Placing a 6th trap destroys the oldest one. Typical split: 3 LS + 2 DS, or 4 LS + 1 DS for boss kills. Never place more than you need — extra traps just replace existing ones and waste mana.

Trap Placement Positioning

Traps fire at the nearest enemy in range. Place them where enemies will walk, not where enemies currently are. Chokepoints, doorways, and corridor entrances are ideal. In open areas, place traps slightly ahead of where you expect enemies to path. They have a fixed firing range — enemies outside range won't be targeted.

Mind Blast — Your Panic Button

Mind Blast is massively underrated. It stuns enemies for 0.6 seconds and has a chance to convert them (like Necromancer's Confuse). Use it when: you teleport into a dangerous pack, your merc is about to die, you're surrounded, or you need time to place traps. The stun gives your traps time to start firing. Spam it — it costs almost nothing.

Shadow Master is Free Defense

Even at 1 point, Shadow Master is a free meat shield that uses your skills (including traps). She draws aggro, tanks hits, and occasionally stuns enemies. Don't neglect her. Recast her when she dies — she's disposable but extremely useful. With +skills gear, she becomes surprisingly tanky.

Cloak of Shadows

1 point in Cloak of Shadows (prerequisite) reduces enemy Defense and disables ranged attacks for its duration. Cast it on dangerous ranged packs (Gloams, Burning Souls) to stop them from attacking while your traps work. It also blinds enemies, reducing their aggro range. Incredibly strong utility skill for 1 point.

Weapon Swap for Utility

Keep a Call to Arms (CTA) runeword on weapon swap. Cast Battle Command (+1 skill) and Battle Orders (+life, +mana) before entering dangerous areas. The buff lasts several minutes. This is standard endgame practice for every build in D2R, and Trapsin is no exception. CTA + Spirit on swap is the default.

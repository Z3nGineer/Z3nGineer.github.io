Oathbreaker Paladin — The Fallen Champion

Half-Orc · Oathbreaker Paladin 12 · #5 Ranked Build (Deltia's #2)
#5 Overall Nova Damage Frontline Summon Army Patch 8

Overview

The Oathbreaker Paladin is the dark side of BG3's deadliest class. You get everything that makes Paladin dominant — Divine Smite, Extra Attack, heavy armor, Aura of Protection — plus the Oathbreaker's unique Aura of Hate, which adds your Charisma modifier to melee damage for you, your undead summons, and any fiends in range. With Animate Dead raising a small army and every one of them getting +5 damage per hit from your aura, the Oathbreaker commands the highest sustained damage output of any pure Paladin build.

Why Oathbreaker

Aura of Hate adds +CHA modifier (up to +5) to ALL melee damage rolls for you, your undead, and fiends within the aura. That's +5 on every single hit. With 3 undead from Animate Dead each attacking once per turn, that's +15 free damage per round from your summons alone — before your own attacks. Combined with Divine Smite burst and Extra Attack, this is the highest single-target and sustained damage pure Paladin build in BG3.

The Math

Your turn: Greatsword (2d6+5 STR +5 Aura of Hate +10 GWM = ~27 avg) x2 attacks = ~54 weapon damage. Add two 3rd-level Divine Smites (4d8 each = 18 avg each) = 36 radiant. Total: ~90 damage per round from you alone. Your three skeletons add another ~36 damage per round with Aura of Hate. On crit turns with Half-Orc Savage Attacks, a single smite hit can exceed 60 damage.

Core Synergies

  • Aura Aura of Hate — +CHA mod to melee damage for you, undead, and fiends. The backbone of this build.
  • Smite Divine Smite — 2d8–5d8 radiant per hit. Paladin's signature burst, still fully available.
  • Summon Animate Dead — Oathbreaker domain spell. Raise skeletons/zombies that benefit from Aura of Hate.
  • Capstone Dread Lord — Level 11 aura. Frightens enemies, grants extra necrotic damage on your attacks.

Race & Background

Best Race: Half-Orc

  • Racial Savage Attacks — On a crit, roll one extra weapon damage die. Since you're swinging a greatsword with GWM, crits from this build are catastrophic. Pairs beautifully with smite crit fishing.
  • Racial Relentless Endurance — Drop to 1 HP instead of 0 once per long rest. You're the frontline tank — this is insurance.
  • Racial Darkvision — 12m range. Critical for Act 2's Shadow Curse, where you'll be fighting in near-total darkness.

Alternative: Zariel Tiefling

  • Racial Searing Smite (free) — Free smite spell at level 3. Extra fire damage stacks with Divine Smite and Aura of Hate.
  • Racial Branding Smite (free) — Free at level 5. More smite fuel without spending your limited Paladin slots.
  • Racial Fire Resistance — Halves fire damage. Thematic for a dark paladin, and practical against many BG3 enemies.
Other Viable Races

Dragonborn (Red/Gold) — fire breath AoE + fire resistance, no feat competition. Shield Dwarf — extra HP, poison resistance, very tanky. Human — carrying capacity bonus helps when you're in heavy armor with a greatsword and loot.

Background

Pick Soldier for Athletics (shove/grapple) and Intimidation (dark paladin fantasy + high CHA). Alternatively, Noble gives you History and Persuasion for a more silver-tongued fallen knight. Either way, your 16+ CHA makes you an excellent party face.

CHA Is Your God Stat

Charisma does more for Oathbreaker than any other Paladin subclass. It simultaneously powers: (1) Aura of Hate melee damage bonus for you AND your undead, (2) Aura of Protection saving throw bonus for the whole party, (3) your spell save DC for Command, Hold Person, Fear, (4) social skill checks. Every point of CHA is multiplied across your entire army. Reaching CHA 20 by level 8 is the single biggest power spike in this build.

Feat Priority for Ability Scores

  • Level 4 Great Weapon Master — +10 damage per hit, -5 attack. The -5 penalty is offset by advantage from Vow of Enmity or allies using Faerie Fire.
  • Level 8 +2 CHA → 18 — Better aura for you and your army, better DC, better saves for the party.
  • Level 12 +2 CHA → 20 — Cap CHA. +5 Aura of Hate, +5 Aura of Protection. Your undead army hits like trucks.

6 Oathbreaker 6 Aura of Protection Core Online ▸
Aura of Protection
  • Aura Aura of Protection — +CHA mod to ALL saving throws for you and allies within 3m (10ft). At CHA 16, that's +3 to every save for your whole frontline. The single best defensive feature in BG3.

This is the reason every Paladin build goes to at least level 6. Aura of Protection is arguably the best feature in the entire game — it makes your whole party significantly harder to crowd control or kill.

7 Oathbreaker 7 Aura of Hate ★ Power Spike ▸
Aura of Hate
  • Aura Aura of Hate — +CHA modifier to melee weapon damage for you, undead allies, and fiend allies within 3m. At CHA 16, +3 damage per hit; at CHA 20, +5. This applies to EVERY melee attack from every affected creature.
  • Math Army Damage — 3 Animate Dead skeletons each get +3 to +5 per hit. Your Spellcasting Wraith gets it too. You personally stack it with GWM for +15 per hit (GWM +10, Aura +5). The damage scaling is absurd.
Why Pure Paladin

If you multiclass out before level 7, you never get Aura of Hate. This single feature is the entire reason to play Oathbreaker over Sorcadin. A Sorcadin has more spell slots for smites, but an Oathbreaker has a permanent +5 damage per hit for their entire army. Over a full adventuring day, the Oathbreaker's sustained damage output exceeds the Sorcadin's.

How to Break Your Oath

You cannot choose Oathbreaker at character creation. You must take any Paladin oath (Devotion, Ancients, or Vengeance) and then perform an action that violates your oath's tenets. Once broken, the Oathbreaker Knight appears at your camp. He offers to restore your oath for 1,000 gold — decline to stay Oathbreaker permanently.

Important

You can break your oath at ANY level after taking your subclass at Paladin 1. The earliest opportunity is in the Nautiloid prologue. Breaking your oath is permanent unless you pay the Oathbreaker Knight to restore it. If you change your mind later, it costs 1,000 gold per restoration.

Oathbreaker Features

Spellcasting Wraith (Channel Divinity)

  • Type Channel Divinity — Action — Summon a powerful wraith. It has its own turn, multiattack, and life drain. Benefits from Aura of Hate. One of the strongest summons available at level 3.
  • Scaling Aura of Hate synergy — The wraith's melee attacks gain +CHA mod from Aura of Hate. At CHA 20, your wraith deals +5 on every hit, making it deal real damage throughout the game.
  • Tactics Positioning — Keep the wraith within 3m of you to benefit from Aura of Hate. It can also tank hits and apply Life Drain to heal itself.

Control Undead (Channel Divinity)

  • Type Channel Divinity — Action — Target an undead creature. If it fails a WIS save, it becomes your ally. Incredible in Act 2 where powerful undead enemies are everywhere.
  • Best Use Act 2 Shadow Cursed Lands — Control powerful undead like Shadow Mastiffs, Ghasts, or even Shadow-cursed Undead to fight for you. They all benefit from Aura of Hate.

Aura of Hate (Level 7)

  • Range 3m (10ft) radius — Passive aura. Adds CHA modifier to melee weapon damage for you, undead allies, and fiend allies within range.
  • Stacking Stacks with everything — GWM (+10) + STR (+5) + Aura of Hate (+5) + Improved Divine Smite (1d8) = each hit deals ~25 before weapon dice. This is the highest per-hit base damage in BG3.

Dread Lord (Level 11 — BG3 Implementation)

  • Note Improved Divine Smite — In BG3 Patch 8, Paladin 11 grants Improved Divine Smite: +1d8 radiant damage on all melee hits passively. This replaces the tabletop Dread Lord feature but is extremely powerful as free, unlimited damage.

Feats

Level 4 — Great Weapon Master

  • Effect +10 damage / -5 attack — Toggle on for heavy weapons. The -5 attack penalty sounds bad, but Bless (+1d4), advantage (from Darkness or allies), and high STR offset it. The +10 damage is multiplicative with Extra Attack and GWM bonus action attacks.
  • Effect Bonus action attack on kill/crit — Kill an enemy or land a critical hit → free bonus action melee attack. With Half-Orc Savage Attacks and smite crits, you'll trigger this regularly.

Level 8 — +2 Charisma (18)

  • Why Multiplied value — Every point of CHA improves damage for you AND every undead summon (Aura of Hate), improves saves for your entire party (Aura of Protection), and improves your spell DCs. No other ASI provides this much value.

Level 12 — +2 Charisma (20) or Sentinel

  • Option A CHA 20 — If you haven't capped CHA from gear yet, always take this. +5 to everything that matters.
  • Option B Sentinel — If CHA is already 20 from items (Birthright, Mirror of Loss). Reaction attacks when enemies hit your allies. Enemies can't Disengage from you. Perfect for protecting your backline while your undead army flanks.
  • Option C Alert — +5 initiative, can't be surprised. Going first means your Aura of Hate is active before enemies act, and you can set up Animate Dead before combat starts properly.

Combat Rotation

Pre-Combat Setup

  1. Cast Animate Dead Pre-Combat
    Raise 1–3 skeletons before every major fight. They persist until killed, so maintain your army between encounters. Use corpses from previous fights.
  2. Position Aura Range Pre-Combat
    Keep your undead within 3m of you. Aura of Hate only works within range. Group your skeletons near you before initiating combat.
  3. Summon Spellcasting Wraith Turn 1
    Channel Divinity to summon your wraith. It gets its own turn, benefits from Aura of Hate, and can tank/deal significant damage.

Turn-by-Turn Rotation

  1. Bless (if not already active) Turn 1
    Bless yourself + 2 allies. The +1d4 to attacks offsets GWM's -5 penalty. Concentration, so only cast once.
  2. Move to priority target Every Turn
    Close to melee range. Target the highest-threat enemy. Your undead should follow if within aura range.
  3. Attack x2 with GWM + Divine Smite Every Turn
    Both attacks deal: 2d6 (greatsword) + 5 (STR) + 5 (Aura of Hate) + 10 (GWM) + smite dice. Save your highest slots for crits or priority kills.
  4. GWM Bonus Attack (on kill/crit) Bonus
    If you killed or crit, swing again as a bonus action. Third smite opportunity. This is where the overkill happens.
  5. Hellish Rebuke (reaction) Reaction
    When an enemy hits you (they will, you're the frontliner), Hellish Rebuke fires back for 2d10 fire. Free damage on the enemy's turn.

Nova Round (Boss Kill)

  1. Command: Grovel (bonus action) Bonus
    Force the boss prone. Prone targets give advantage on melee attacks within 3m. Sets up your crits.
  2. Attack #1: 3rd-level Smite Action
    Greatsword + GWM + Aura of Hate + 4d8 Divine Smite. With advantage from prone, high crit chance. If it crits: 2d6+1d6(savage)+5+5+10+8d8 = ~60+ damage.
  3. Attack #2: 3rd-level Smite Action
    Same again. Two 3rd-level smites in one turn = 8d8 radiant + all modifiers. Boss melts. Total nova: ~100+ damage in one turn from you alone.
Smite Economy

You only have 9 spell slots at level 12 (4/3/2). Don't smite on every hit — save your 3rd-level slots for crits or bosses. Use 1st-level smites for trash mobs. Your Aura of Hate + GWM already deal enough base damage to kill most enemies without smiting. Let your undead army handle the fodder while you save resources for the big fights.

Honour Mode Tips

Honour Mode Warning

Honour Mode bosses have Legendary Actions and much higher stats. Your smite slots are limited and precious. The Oathbreaker's advantage in Honour Mode is action economy — your undead army gives you 3–4 extra attacks per round that don't cost any resources.

  • Tip 1 Animate Dead before EVERY boss fight — Your skeletons are free meat shields that draw enemy attacks AND deal real damage with Aura of Hate. Even if they die, they absorbed hits meant for your party.
  • Tip 2 Smite only on crits in Honour — Your base damage with GWM + Aura of Hate is already ~25 per hit. Save smites for crits where the dice double. A 3rd-level crit smite with Half-Orc Savage Attacks does 8d8+1d6 = ~40 extra damage.
  • Tip 3 Aura of Protection is your survival — +5 to all saves for your frontline. In Honour Mode, enemies have higher DCs. This aura is the difference between your party making saves or getting wiped. Position carefully.
  • Tip 4 Control Undead is broken in Act 2 — Shadow-Cursed Lands are full of powerful undead. Use Control Undead to steal enemy Shadow Mastiffs or Ghasts. They all benefit from Aura of Hate. You can have a small army of controlled undead + raised dead.
  • Tip 5 Hellish Rebuke is free value — You're the frontliner. You WILL get hit. Hellish Rebuke punishes enemies for targeting you. In Honour Mode, this free reaction damage adds up significantly over a fight.
  • Tip 6 Don't waste Channel Divinity early — You have one Channel Divinity per short rest. Spellcasting Wraith is usually the better choice over Control Undead unless there's a very strong undead enemy to steal. Plan your CD usage per fight.

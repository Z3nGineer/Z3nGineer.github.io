Open Hand Monk — Quivering Palm

Githyanki · Way of the Open Hand 9 / Thief Rogue 3 · #9 Ranked Build
Martial Arts Melee DPS Stunning Strike Patch 8

Overview

Open Hand Monk is the ultimate melee martial artist in Baldur's Gate 3. You get more attacks per turn than any other build in the game, Stunning Strike shuts down bosses for an entire round, and at Monk 9 you unlock Quivering Palm — an instant-kill ability that forces a CON save or reduces the target to 0 HP. Multiclassing into Thief Rogue 3 gives you a second bonus action, meaning you can Flurry of Blows twice per turn for a total of 6+ attacks.

Why Open Hand + Thief?

Open Hand Techniques add a free rider to every Flurry of Blows hit — push enemies back, knock them prone, or prevent reactions (no save). Thief Rogue's Fast Hands gives a second bonus action, letting you Flurry twice. That's 2 main attacks + 4 Flurry hits = 6 attacks per turn, each one threatening a Stunning Strike or Open Hand effect. No other build gets this many chances to lock enemies down.

The build comes online in stages: Monk 1-4 is solid, Monk 5 (Extra Attack + Stunning Strike) is a massive spike, Monk 9 (Quivering Palm) is the peak, then Thief 1-3 adds the second bonus action. You're a relentless striker who controls the battlefield through sheer volume of hits and crowd control.

Playstyle Summary

Dash into melee → Stunning Strike the biggest threat → Flurry of Blows (push/prone everything) → Flurry again (Thief bonus action) → enemies are stunned, prone, or dead. On boss turns, Quivering Palm for the instant kill. You are the action economy.

Race Selection

Best: Githyanki

  • Racial Medium Armour Proficiency — Wear medium armour in early game before Unarmoured Defence catches up. Huge early survivability.
  • Racial Greatsword Proficiency — Irrelevant for unarmed, but useful if you want a versatile weapon early.
  • Racial Githyanki Psionics: Jump — Free Misty Step at level 5. Incredible for a melee build — teleport to backline targets.
  • Racial Astral Knowledge — Free skill proficiency after every long rest. Pick whatever you need.
Alternative: Wood Elf

+1.5m movement speed (10.5m base vs 9m) and Darkvision 12m. Fleet of Foot stacks with Monk's Unarmoured Movement for the fastest character in the game — 18m+ movement by level 6. Better for a pure DEX monk if you don't want Tavern Brawler. Wood Half-Elf also works (same speed + shield proficiency + Darkvision).

Why Not Human?

Humans get +1 to all stats and Civil Militia (shield/light armour proficiency), but you don't need shields or armour. The Githyanki jump ability alone is worth more than any Human feature for a melee monk.

WIS 16 is Non-Negotiable

WIS powers your Stunning Strike DC, Quivering Palm DC, and Unarmoured Defence AC. Every point of WIS below 16 makes your save-or-suck abilities less reliable. Do not dump WIS. A monk with low WIS is just a fighter with less HP.

Tavern Brawler in BG3

In BG3 specifically, Tavern Brawler adds your STR modifier twice to unarmed damage (once from the feat, once from the normal modifier). This is why STR monks outdamage DEX monks. With STR 17, each unarmed hit does 1d6+6 at this level. At STR 20, that becomes 1d8+10.

  • Ki Stunning Strike — After hitting: target must CON save or be Stunned until end of your next turn. The most powerful single ability in the game.

Everything Changes at Level 5

This is the build's breakpoint. You now have 4 attacks per turn (2 main + 2 Flurry), each one capable of triggering Stunning Strike. A stunned target auto-fails STR and DEX saves, all attacks against it have advantage, and it loses its turn. You can stun-lock a boss for the entire fight.

The Instant Kill

Quivering Palm targets CON save (DC = 8 + proficiency + WIS mod). With WIS 16 and proficiency +4, that's DC 15. Failed save = dead. Passed save = 10d10 necrotic damage (avg 55). Either way, the target is having a bad day. Stack this with Stunning Strike first — stunned targets have disadvantage on CON saves in some interpretations, and regardless, you soften them up with your other 5 attacks.

  • Feature Second Story Work — Resistance to falling damage. Stacks with Slow Fall.
  • Sneak Sneak Attack 2d6 — Increased to 2d6. Not the main draw, but free damage is free damage.
The Final Form

Your full turn: Attack (2 hits) → Flurry of Blows (2 hits + Open Hand effects) → Flurry of Blows again via Fast Hands (2 hits + Open Hand effects). That's 6 attacks per turn, 4 of which trigger Open Hand Techniques (push/prone/no reactions), and any of them can spend ki on Stunning Strike. With Haste from a party member, you get 7 attacks. This is the highest attack volume in BG3.

Ki Points — Resource Management

Ki is your fuel. Every special ability costs ki, and you get a number of ki points equal to your Monk level (max 9 with this build). Ki restores on short rest, not long rest — this is huge. You should be short resting after every 2-3 encounters to stay topped off.

Don't Blow All Ki on Stunning Strike

It's tempting to Stunning Strike every hit, but at 1 ki per attempt you'll burn through 9 ki in 2 turns. The optimal play: Stunning Strike once or twice on the scariest target, Flurry of Blows (1 ki for 2 hits + Open Hand effects) on everything else. Save 4 ki for Quivering Palm against bosses. Short rest frequently.

Short Rest is Your Best Friend

BG3 gives you 2 short rests per long rest. Each one fully restores all 9 Ki Points. Plan your fights around this: burn ki freely in the first 2-3 fights, short rest, burn again, short rest, then long rest. You effectively have 27 Ki Points per long rest.

Open Hand Techniques

Every time you hit with Flurry of Blows, you choose one of three effects. These are free — no extra ki cost, no save required (except the initial attack roll). With Thief's second bonus action, you trigger these effects 4 times per turn.

TechniqueEffectBest Used
Push 5mShove the target 5 metres in any directionCliffs, chasms, hazard surfaces, away from squishies
Knock ProneTarget falls prone (can't move, melee has advantage)Before your party's turn — everyone attacks with advantage
Prevent ReactionsTarget can't use reactions until end of your next turnDisabling opportunity attacks, counterspell, sentinel
Combo: Prone + Party Follow-Up

Knock an enemy prone with your first Flurry, then all your remaining attacks (and your party's melee attacks) have advantage. If Lae'zel or Karlach goes after you in initiative, they get advantage on every single attack. This is better than Stunning Strike in many cases because it costs nothing extra.

Environmental Kills

Push is an instant kill near ledges. The Underdark, Grymforge, Moonrise Towers, and the final act all have fights near cliffs. One Flurry hit pushes a full-HP enemy to their death. No save. No ki cost beyond the Flurry itself. Map awareness turns you into the deadliest character in the game.

Stunning Strike

The single most powerful ability in BG3. After landing a melee hit, spend 1 ki to force a CON save (DC = 8 + proficiency + WIS mod). On failure, the target is Stunned until the end of your next turn.

What Stunned Does

  • Effect Can't move or take actions — The target loses its entire turn. Boss fights become trivial.
  • Effect Auto-fail STR and DEX saves — Your wizard's Hold Person, Fireball, etc. all auto-hit or deal max effect.
  • Effect Attacks against have advantage — Your entire party crits on 19-20 with advantage. Assassin rogues auto-crit.
  • Effect Lasts until end of YOUR next turn — This means the entire party gets a full round of free attacks with advantage.
Stun-Locking Bosses

With 6 attacks per turn, you have 6 chances to land Stunning Strike. Even if the boss has +8 CON save, the math is in your favour: land one Stun, then next turn your party unloads while you Stun again. Bosses in BG3 don't have legendary resistances (except Honour Mode) — they just fail and lose their turn. Repeat until dead.

Targets that Resist

High-CON enemies (Stone Golems, undead, some demons) will save more often. Against these, rely on Open Hand pushes/prone instead. Don't burn all your ki on Stunning Strike attempts against a target with +10 CON save — push it off a cliff instead.

Quivering Palm — Instant Kill

The crown jewel of the Open Hand Monk. Unlocked at Monk 9, Quivering Palm costs 4 Ki Points and triggers on a successful unarmed hit. The target must make a CON save (same DC as Stunning Strike). On a failed save, the target drops to 0 HP. On a success, they take 10d10 necrotic damage (average 55).

DC = 8 + 4 (proficiency) + 3 (WIS mod) = 15. Most enemies in Act 2 have +3 to +6 CON save. A +5 CON save means they need to roll 10+ on a d20 = 55% chance to die instantly.

Optimal Quivering Palm Usage

1. Stun the boss first with a regular Stunning Strike (1 ki).
2. On your next turn, open with Quivering Palm on the stunned target.
3. If Quivering Palm fails the kill, the 10d10 necrotic still chunks them, and you have 5 more attacks to finish the job.
4. Save Quivering Palm for fights that matter — 4 ki is nearly half your pool.

Honour Mode Warning

In Honour Mode, bosses get Legendary Resistance (auto-pass one save). They will burn it on Quivering Palm. This means you need to bait out Legendary Resistance with Stunning Strike first (cheaper at 1 ki), THEN use Quivering Palm after it's gone. See the Honour Mode section below.

Tavern Brawler vs DEX Monk

The great debate. Both work, but Tavern Brawler is mathematically superior for damage in BG3 due to how the feat interacts with unarmed strikes.

Verdict

Tavern Brawler for maximum damage. The +5 extra damage per hit across 6 attacks is +30 DPR — that's an entire extra character's worth of damage. The lower AC is offset by Stunning Strike keeping enemies from attacking you at all. If they can't take their turn, your AC doesn't matter.

Pure DEX for a safer, more well-rounded character. Better AC, better saves, better initiative. Recommended for Honour Mode if you're not comfortable with the aggressive Tavern Brawler playstyle.

Gloves of Soul Catching Changes Everything

This one item transforms the build. +1d10 damage per hit across 6 attacks = +33 average DPR. The heal-10-HP-per-hit option means you're unkillable in melee. And it sets CON to 20, fixing your HP problem. Rush the House of Hope as early as possible in Act 3.

Combat Rotation

Full Rotation (Level 12, Thief Online)

  1. Position & Open Turn 1
    Use your massive movement (15m+) to reach the most dangerous enemy. If you can't reach, Step of the Wind: Dash or use Githyanki Jump to teleport.
  2. Stunning Strike on the priority target Action
    Attack action (2 hits). Spend 1 ki on the first hit for Stunning Strike. If it lands, the boss is out. If it misses the save, try again on the second hit. 2 chances to stun for 2 ki.
  3. Flurry of Blows #1 Bonus Action
    1 ki for 2 unarmed strikes. Choose Open Hand Technique on each hit: Prone for advantage, Push for environmental kills, or Prevent Reactions for safe repositioning. If target is already stunned, just deal damage.
  4. Flurry of Blows #2 (Thief) 2nd Bonus Action
    Your Thief second bonus action. Another Flurry of Blows (1 ki, 2 hits). More Open Hand effects. You've now attacked 6 times this turn, stunned or knocked prone 1+ enemies, and dealt 80-100+ damage.
  5. Quivering Palm (Boss Fights Only) Priority Target
    Instead of Stunning Strike, use Quivering Palm on your first successful hit against a boss. 4 ki for instant kill or 55 avg damage. Only use on targets you need dead immediately.

Trash Mob Rotation (Ki Conservation)

  1. Attack action (2 hits) Action
    Just swing. Don't spend ki on Stunning Strike against weak enemies.
  2. Flurry of Blows Bonus Action
    1 ki for 2 hits + Open Hand push/prone. Push weakened enemies off ledges for instant kills.
  3. Save second bonus action 2nd Bonus Action
    Either Flurry again if there are many targets, or save ki. 4 attacks per turn without spending extra ki is enough for trash.
Ki Budget Per Fight

Easy fight: 1-2 ki (one Flurry, maybe a Step of the Wind). 4 attacks total.
Medium fight: 3-4 ki (one Stun + two Flurries). 6 attacks total.
Boss fight: 6-8 ki (Quivering Palm + Stun + two Flurries). 6 attacks, one potential instant kill.
Short rest after 2-3 fights to reset all 9 ki.

Honour Mode Tips

Honour Mode adds Legendary Actions/Resistances to bosses. This directly impacts monks because bosses can auto-pass your Stunning Strike and Quivering Palm saves. Here's how to adapt.

Legendary Resistance

Bosses get 1-3 Legendary Resistances that let them auto-pass any saving throw. Your job is to bait these out cheaply. Stunning Strike costs 1 ki — burn through their resistances with 1-ki Stuns before spending 4 ki on Quivering Palm. Never lead with Quivering Palm against a boss with Legendary Resistance up.

Honour Mode Strategy

  1. Bait Legendary Resistance Turn 1
    Open with Stunning Strike on your first hit. Boss burns a Legendary Resistance. Hit again, Stun again. Another Legendary Resistance burned. You've spent 2 ki to remove 2 of their auto-passes.
  2. Party burns the last resistance Team
    Have your caster throw a save-or-suck spell (Hold Person, Banishment). Boss uses last Legendary Resistance. Resistances are now gone.
  3. Quivering Palm Turn 2+
    Now use Quivering Palm. No more auto-passes. They roll normally against your DC 15+. Failed save = dead boss. Even on a success, 55 necrotic damage.
Honour Mode Gear Priority

Prioritize survivability: Helldusk Armour (AC 21) and Gloves of Soul Catching (heal 10 per hit) make you nearly unkillable. In Honour Mode you can't reload — one death ends the run. The monk's weakness is HP and AC, so plug those gaps with gear.

Honour Mode Legendary Actions

Some Honour Mode bosses get extra actions between turns. This means stunned targets might still act via Legendary Actions even while stunned. The workaround: Open Hand prone (costs no save, just happens) still works even against Legendary Action users, because prone applies disadvantage to their attacks. Stack prone + stun for maximum lockdown.

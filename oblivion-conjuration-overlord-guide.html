Conjuration Overlord

Breton · The Atronach · Summons + Debuffs + Ranged Support
Summoner Overlord Max Difficulty Remastered 2025

Overview

The Conjuration Overlord is the strongest build for high-difficulty play in Oblivion Remastered. On maximum difficulty, the player deals 1/6 normal damage with weapons and spells, while receiving 6x damage. Summoned creatures bypass this entirely — they deal full, unscaled damage regardless of difficulty slider. Your army fights at 100% effectiveness while every other build in the game is crippled. You are not the weapon. You are the general.

Why Summons Break Difficulty Scaling

Oblivion's difficulty slider only modifies damage dealt by the player and damage received by the player. Conjured creatures are classified as NPCs — their attacks and defenses are completely unaffected. A summoned Xivilai hits just as hard on max difficulty as on minimum. This makes Conjuration the only school that scales linearly with difficulty: the harder you set it, the more valuable your summons become relative to everything else.

Race — Breton

  • Passive 50% Magic Resistance — best defensive racial in the game, permanent
  • Passive +50 Magicka — permanent bonus to your mana pool
  • Daily Dragon Skin — 50% physical damage shield for 60 seconds. Emergency button.
  • Racials +10 Conjuration, Mysticism, Restoration · +5 Alchemy, Alteration, Illusion

Breton is non-negotiable. The +10 Conjuration head start means you begin with Apprentice-tier summons available immediately. The 50% Magic Resistance covers your biggest weakness — enemy mages — and Dragon Skin gives you an emergency panic button for the rare moment when your summons fall and you're face-to-face with something nasty.

Class — Custom: Conjuration Overlord

  • Spec Magic
  • Favored Intelligence, Endurance
  • Major Alchemy, Conjuration, Destruction, Illusion, Marksman, Restoration, Security
Why These Majors?

Conjuration is your entire identity — summon progressively stronger creatures. Illusion for Courage/Rally to buff your summons and Command Humanoid for crowd control. Destruction for Weakness to Magic/Fire/Frost/Shock debuffs that make your summons hit even harder. Restoration for healing yourself and sustain. Marksman for ranged damage while your army tanks — bows bypass the Conjuration duration penalty from Heavy Armor. Alchemy for magicka potions (critical with The Atronach). Security because you have no room for Alteration, and lockpicking is essential.

First Steps After the Tutorial

The Conjuration Overlord has a very specific shopping list. Your first hour sets the tone for the entire playthrough.

  1. Buy Summon Skeleton at Edgar's Discount Spells
    Imperial City Market District. Summon Skeleton is your first soldier. It fights, it tanks, it dies so you don't. Also buy Flare (Destruction) and Heal Minor Wounds (Restoration) if you don't have them. Grab Courage (Illusion) from Calindil at Mystic Emporium to buff your skeleton's damage.
  2. Join the Mages Guild — all 7 cities
    Start the recommendation quest chain immediately. You need Arcane University access for the Spellmaking Altar, where you'll craft buffed-duration summons and custom debuff combos. Talk to any Mages Guild hall to begin. Do one recommendation per city as you travel.
  3. Buy Summon Zombie + early spells in Skingrad
    Visit Sulinus Vassinus at the Skingrad Mages Guild. Buy Summon Zombie (stronger than Skeleton) and any Destruction spells available. Do the Skingrad Mages Guild recommendation quest while you're here.
  4. Set up the Telekinesis absorption loop
    Buy Telekinesis from any Mysticism vendor (Ita Rienus in Bravil, or Calindil in Imperial City). With The Atronach birthsign, casting Telekinesis on yourself and having it absorbed restores magicka. This is your primary sustain mechanic — see the Birthsign section for the full loop.
  5. Get a bow — any bow
    Buy from "A Fighting Chance" in the Imperial City Market District. Your bow is your personal DPS tool while summons tank. Marksman levels quickly and doesn't depend on magicka. Even a cheap iron bow works — you just need to start leveling the skill.
  6. Collect every ingredient you see
    With The Atronach birthsign, you have zero natural magicka regen. Alchemy potions are your lifeline. Pick up everything — food, flowers, mushrooms. Buy a Mortar and Pestle and start brewing Restore Magicka potions immediately. Ingredients with Restore Magicka: Milk Thistle Seeds, Mandrake Root, Bonemeal, Frost Salts.
Skill XP While Traveling

While walking between cities, keep a summon active at all times. Every time you re-cast a summon spell, you gain Conjuration XP. Spam Summon Skeleton as it expires to grind the skill. Also cast Flare at nothing for Destruction XP and Heal Minor Wounds for Restoration XP. In the remaster, higher-cost spells give more XP, so always use your most expensive summon.

Birthsign — The Atronach

The Atronach is the most powerful and most punishing birthsign in Oblivion. It gives you the largest magicka pool possible and a 50% chance to absorb incoming spells — but you will never regenerate magicka naturally. Not ever. This is a trade-off that defines your entire playstyle.

The Absorption Loop — Your Sustain Engine

With The Atronach, you never regenerate magicka naturally. Instead, you gain magicka by absorbing spells. Every spell that hits you has a 50% chance to be absorbed, restoring its magicka cost to your pool. This means enemy mages are not a threat — they're fuel.

How to Refuel Magicka

Method 1 — Let enemies cast at you. Stand in front of enemy mages and let them blast you. 50% of their spells restore your magicka instead of hurting you, and your 50% Magic Resistance handles the other 50%. Against pure mages, you're practically immortal AND they're filling your tank.

Method 2 — Alchemy potions. Brew Restore Magicka potions constantly. This is your out-of-combat sustain. Key ingredients: Bonemeal + Frost Salts, Milk Thistle Seeds + Mandrake Root, any "Restore Magicka" combo.

Method 3 — Welkynd Stones. Found in Ayleid ruins. Each stone fully restores your magicka. Hoard them for boss fights. They're single-use but respawn after the cell resets (3 in-game days).

Method 4 — Chapel Altars. Praying at any chapel altar fully restores Health, Magicka, and Fatigue. Free, unlimited, always available in cities.

Avoid — The Apprentice

+100 Magicka but 100% Weakness to Magic. Your 50% Breton resistance gets negated AND you take extra damage. On max difficulty with 6x incoming damage, this is suicidal.

Gear — Light Armor and Robes

CRITICAL — Heavy Armor Kills Conjuration Duration

In Oblivion Remastered (2025), wearing Heavy Armor reduces the duration of Conjuration spells. A Storm Atronach that should last 60 seconds might only last 30-35 seconds in full heavy plate. This is a Remastered-specific change that did not exist in the original game. Wear Light Armor or Robes. If you insist on Heavy Armor, you MUST enchant every piece with Fortify Conjuration to compensate — see the Enchanting section.

As a Conjuration Overlord, your enchanting priorities are different from any other build. You're not stacking damage — your summons handle that. You're stacking summon duration, magicka pool, and survivability.

If you insist on wearing Heavy Armor despite the Conjuration duration penalty, you must enchant every piece with Fortify Conjuration to claw back the lost duration. Combat — The Overlord Rotation

Your combat loop is fundamentally different from every other build in Oblivion. You never engage enemies directly. You summon, buff, debuff, and shoot from behind your army. Think of it as an RTS played in first person.

The Full Combat Rotation

  1. Summon your creature before entering combat
    Always have a summon active. Pre-cast before opening doors, entering new areas, or approaching enemy camps. Storm Atronach for damage, Frost Atronach for tanking, Xivilai for versatility. Never walk into a fight alone.
  2. Cast Rally on your summon
    Immediately after summoning, cast Rally (or Courage if you're still low-level) directly on your creature. This significantly boosts its attack damage. The difference between a buffed and unbuffed summon is enormous — always buff.
  3. Cast Weakness to [Element] on the enemy
    Match the weakness to your summon. Flame Atronach → Weakness to Fire. Storm Atronach → Weakness to Shock. Frost Atronach → Weakness to Frost. Xivilai → Weakness to Magic (covers all its attacks). This doubles or triples your summon's effective damage.
  4. Shoot with your bow from behind your summon
    Position yourself behind your summon and shoot arrows at the same target. Your summon draws aggro while you deal supplemental ranged damage. On max difficulty your bow damage is reduced, but it still contributes — and Marksman XP is free.
  5. Heal yourself if your summon falls
    If your summon dies, immediately re-summon. If you can't (out of magicka), cast Heal Major Wounds, drink a Restore Magicka potion, and re-summon. Dragon Skin (daily) gives you 60 seconds of breathing room in emergencies.
  6. Against multiple enemies: prioritize debuffs
    Cast Weakness to Magic on the toughest enemy first. Your summon will target whoever is closest — use the debuff to ensure the hardest target goes down fast. Against groups, the Frost Atronach's area frost damage is invaluable.
Advanced: The Double Army Trick

With the Staff of Worms (Mages Guild questline reward), you can reanimate a dead enemy to fight alongside your summoned creature. This gives you TWO combatants — one from Conjuration, one from the staff. If you summoned a Xivilai (which summons its own Clannfear), you now have three entities fighting for you plus a reanimated corpse. Four-versus-one in your favor, every fight.

Enemy Mages Are Your Best Friends

With 50% Magic Resistance (Breton) + 50% Spell Absorption (Atronach) + additional absorption from gear, enemy mages literally refuel your magicka pool when they cast at you. Let them. Stand in the open and absorb their spells. Every absorbed spell restores its full magicka cost to your pool. Against a room of mages, you become more powerful the longer the fight lasts.

Tips and Critical Warnings

CRITICAL — Heavy Armor + Conjuration Duration Penalty

This is the single most important piece of information for this build in Oblivion Remastered. Wearing Heavy Armor significantly reduces the duration of Conjuration spells. This is a new Remastered mechanic that did not exist in the original 2006 game. If you wear full Heavy Armor without compensating with Fortify Conjuration enchantments, your Storm Atronach might despawn mid-fight. Wear Robes or Light Armor unless you have enough Fortify Conjuration enchantments to counteract the penalty.

Summons Don't Scale With Difficulty

This is why the build works. Oblivion's difficulty slider modifies player damage dealt (reduced on higher difficulty) and player damage taken (increased on higher difficulty). Summoned creatures are classified as NPCs — they are completely unaffected by either modifier. A Storm Atronach on maximum difficulty deals the same damage as one on minimum difficulty. On max difficulty, where your sword deals 1/6 damage, your summon still deals 6/6. This is not an exploit — it's how the game engine works.

Welkynd Stones Are Your Best Resource

Found in every Ayleid ruin, Welkynd Stones fully restore your magicka when activated. With The Atronach's zero regen, these are invaluable. Clear Ayleid ruins early and often. Stones respawn after the dungeon cell resets (3 in-game days). Keep 5-10 Welkynd Stones in your inventory at all times for emergency magicka refills during tough fights.

Alchemy Is Non-Negotiable

With The Atronach birthsign, Alchemy is not optional — it is survival. You need a constant supply of Restore Magicka potions. Key combos: Bonemeal + Frost Salts (common from undead), Milk Thistle Seeds + Mandrake Root (found in the wild), Flax Seeds + Lady's Mantle Leaves. Buy a Mortar and Pestle from any alchemist. Carry it always. Brew potions after every dungeon. Your Alchemy skill will level fast because you're using it constantly.

Level Conjuration Fast — The Re-Summon Trick

Conjuration XP is granted every time you cast a summon spell, not when the summon fights. You can gain XP by spamming Summon Skeleton while walking between locations. Cast, wait for it to appear, cast again (replacing the old one). Higher-cost summons give more XP in the remaster. Once you can afford it, spam Summon Flame Atronach for faster leveling than Summon Skeleton.

The Summon Limit

You can only have one summoned creature active at a time (from Conjuration spells). Casting a new summon despawns the old one. This is why the Xivilai is so strong — it summons its own Clannfear, effectively giving you two creatures. The Staff of Worms reanimation does NOT count toward this limit, so you can have a summon + a reanimated corpse simultaneously.

Custom Spells at the Spellmaking Altar

Once you have Arcane University access, create custom summon spells with extended durations. A custom Summon Storm Atronach lasting 120 seconds costs more magicka but means fewer recasts — critical when you have no natural regen. Also craft combo debuff spells: Weakness to Magic 100% + Weakness to Shock 100% in a single cast for maximum efficiency.

Don't Melee — Ever

On maximum difficulty, you deal 1/6 melee damage and take 6x damage. Swinging a sword is actively suicidal. Your bow damage is also reduced, but at least you're at range and behind a summon. Never close to melee range. If an enemy reaches you, re-summon between you and them, then back away. You are the general, not the soldier.

Sleep to Level Up

You don't level up automatically in Oblivion. When the game says "you should rest and meditate," find any bed (inns, your house, bedrolls in dungeons) and sleep to trigger the level-up screen. Each level gives you 12 Virtues to distribute — push INT and END first.

Best Daedric Quests for This Build

1. Azura's Shrine (level 2+) — Azura's Star, reusable Grand Soul Gem. Get this first.

2. Malacath's Shrine (level 10+) — Hatreds Soul bow with Soul Trap. Your endgame weapon.

3. Peryite's Shrine (level 10+) — Spellbreaker shield, 30% Reflect Spell. Backup defense.

4. Clavicus Vile's Shrine (level 20+) — Umbra sword for backup Soul Trap weapon if needed.

The Ghost

Breton · The Mage · 100% Chameleon · Permanent Invisibility
100% Chameleon Stealth S-Tier Remastered 2025

Overview

The Ghost is the most powerful build in Oblivion Remastered, full stop. By stacking 100% Chameleon across five enchanted armor pieces, you become permanently undetectable — not just invisible, but completely removed from the AI's awareness. You can walk up to any enemy, any NPC, any boss, and deliver a guaranteed sneak attack. With the remastered 8x dagger multiplier from Agility, every single hit is a one-shot kill on most enemies.

Unlike Invisibility, Chameleon does not break when you attack, cast spells, open doors, loot containers, or interact with objects. At 100%, enemies will never detect you. You are a permanent ghost in the world — combat becomes a formality, dungeons become loot runs, and every fight starts and ends on your terms.

Why 100% Chameleon is S-Tier

Permanent undetectability. No spell to maintain, no timer, no magicka cost. Just walk through the entire game killing everything with impunity. Every hit is a sneak attack. Every dungeon is trivial. Every quest that requires stealth is automatic. The Dark Brotherhood and Thieves Guild become effortless. Even the Arena can be cheesed.

Race — Breton

  • Passive 50% Magic Resistance — best defensive racial; AoE spells and traps barely scratch you
  • Passive +50 Magicka — fuels your Illusion and Conjuration spells before Chameleon gear
  • Daily Dragon Skin — 50% physical damage shield for 60 seconds — emergency button for early game
  • Skills +10 Conjuration, Mysticism, Restoration · +5 Alchemy, Alteration, Illusion

Class — Custom: "The Ghost"

  • Spec Stealth
  • Favored Intelligence, Agility
  • Major Alchemy, Blade, Conjuration, Destruction, Illusion, Light Armor, Sneak
Why Stealth Specialization?

+10 Sneak, +5 Agility & Speed from Stealth spec. In the remaster, Agility governs dagger damage and the sneak attack multiplier. High Agility = higher dagger damage = bigger one-shots. The entire build revolves around Sneak + Blade (daggers) + Chameleon, so Stealth spec is the only choice.

First Steps After the Tutorial

Your early game is about setting up the foundations: faction membership for quest rewards, Grand Soul Gems for enchanting, and closing Oblivion Gates for Sigil Stones. Here is the exact order.

  1. Join the Dark Brotherhood
    Sleep in any inn after killing an innocent NPC (easiest: a beggar in the Waterfront at night, or use a Frenzy spell on a civilian and let guards handle it, then claim the kill). Lucien Lachance visits you in your sleep. Accept his offer. The Brotherhood questline gives you the Blade of Woe immediately and eventually Shadowhush armor and other stealth gear. This is your primary early questline.
  2. Join the Thieves Guild
    Read one of the "Gray Fox" wanted posters in any city, then find the beggar Armand Christophe at midnight in the Imperial City Waterfront Garden. Complete the initiation quest. The Thieves Guild gives you fencing contacts for stolen goods and eventually the Gray Cowl of Nocturnal — one of the best items in the game.
  3. Buy Starter Spells — Imperial City
    Visit Edgar's Discount Spells for cheap Illusion spells (especially Chameleon). Visit Mystic Emporium for Soul Trap. Buy a dagger from any weaponsmith — you need Blade skill gains from the start. Buy Flare and Snowball for Destruction leveling.
  4. Start Collecting Grand Soul Gems
    You need at least 5 Grand Soul Gems filled with Grand Souls to enchant your five Chameleon pieces. Buy empty Grand Soul Gems from Mages Guild vendors (Calindil at Mystic Emporium, Rindir at Rindir's Staffs). Learn the Soul Trap spell and start filling them by killing Grand-soul creatures (Xivilai, Liches, Gloom Wraiths, Minotaur Lords). Alternatively, close Oblivion Gates for Sigil Stones with Chameleon.
  5. Join the Mages Guild
    Visit any Mages Guild hall and start collecting recommendations from all 7 cities. You need Arcane University access for the Enchanting Altar. Do one recommendation per city visit — they're quick sidequests. This can be done alongside Dark Brotherhood contracts.
  6. Start Closing Oblivion Gates (Main Quest)
    Progress the main quest until Oblivion Gates start appearing (after Kvatch). Each gate you close rewards a random Sigil Stone. At level 17+, Sigil Stones can roll 20% Chameleon — this is the fastest path to 100%. Save before grabbing the Sigil Stone and reload until you get Chameleon.
Critical — Don't Rush the Main Quest

Oblivion Gates with Transcendent Sigil Stones (the best tier, including 20% Chameleon) only appear at level 17+. If you close gates before level 17, the Sigil Stones will be weaker and give less than 20% Chameleon per piece. Level up first, then farm gates. You need exactly 5 pieces at 20% each, or you'll need more gear slots.

Alternative — Enchanting at the Arcane University

If you have Arcane University access and Grand Soul Gems filled with Grand Souls, you can enchant your own gear with Chameleon using the Enchanting Altar. You need to know a Chameleon spell first (buy from Bravil Mages Guild — Ita Rienus sells it). Grand Souls give 20% Chameleon per piece on armor. This is more reliable than farming Sigil Stone RNG.

Why The Mage Over The Thief?

Before your 100% Chameleon gear is complete (levels 1-17), you're a fragile stealth character who relies on Invisibility spells, Chameleon spells, and Conjuration summons to survive. All of these cost Magicka. The +100 Magicka from Breton + The Mage means you can cast multiple Invisibility spells per fight, summon a skeleton to tank, and still have mana for Destruction. Once Chameleon gear is online, Magicka becomes less critical — but you still want it for Conjuration summons and AoE Destruction.

Avoid — The Apprentice

+100 Magicka but 100% Weakness to Magic. Negates your Breton 50% resist and makes you take extra magic damage. Even with 100% Chameleon, AoE spells and traps still hit you. Don't take it.

Leveling — Virtue System

The remaster replaced the old attribute multiplier system. At each level-up you get 12 Virtues to distribute freely. Max 5 per attribute, max 3 attributes per level. Luck costs 4 per point. For The Ghost, your priority is getting to level 17 ASAP for Transcendent Sigil Stones, while building Agility for dagger damage.

Level Fast — Sleep Often

You want to reach level 17 as efficiently as possible for Transcendent Sigil Stones. Your Major Skills (Sneak, Blade, Illusion, etc.) all level by using them. Sneak everywhere — even in town. Cast Chameleon spells while walking around. Stab rats with your dagger. Every action levels you. Sleep in beds frequently to trigger level-ups.

Enchanting — Reaching 100% Chameleon

This section explains exactly how to enchant your gear for 100% Chameleon. There are two methods: Sigil Stones from Oblivion Gates, or Enchanting at the Arcane University. Both work. The Sigil Stone method is faster if you're lucky; the Enchanting method is more reliable.

Method 1: Sigil Stones (Faster, RNG-Based)

  1. Reach Level 17+
    Transcendent Sigil Stones (the highest tier) only appear at level 17 and above. These give 20% Chameleon when applied to armor. Lower-level Sigil Stones give less (5-15%), requiring more pieces.
  2. Progress the Main Quest until Oblivion Gates open
    After completing the Kvatch quest, Oblivion Gates begin appearing near cities. Each gate contains a tower with a Sigil Stone at the top. You must fight through Daedra to reach it.
  3. Save before grabbing the Sigil Stone
    The Sigil Stone's enchantment is randomly determined when you pick it up. Save your game right before touching it. If you don't get Chameleon, reload and try again. This is the core farming loop.
  4. Apply Chameleon Sigil Stones to 5 armor pieces
    Open your inventory, select the Sigil Stone, and apply it to an unenchanted armor piece. Each Transcendent Chameleon Sigil Stone adds 20% Chameleon. Do this 5 times across 5 pieces = 100%.
  5. Equip all 5 pieces — become The Ghost
    Once all 5 pieces are equipped, your active effects will show Chameleon 100%. You are now permanently undetectable. Congratulations.

Method 2: Arcane University Enchanting (Reliable, Resource-Intensive)

  1. Complete all 7 Mages Guild recommendations
    Visit each city's Mages Guild hall and complete their recommendation quest. Once all 7 are done, you gain access to the Arcane University in the Imperial City — including the Enchanting Altar in the Chironasium building.
  2. Learn a Chameleon spell
    You must already know a spell with the Chameleon effect to enchant it onto armor. Buy Chameleon from Ita Rienus in the Bravil Mages Guild. Requires Illusion Journeyman (skill 50+).
  3. Acquire 5 Grand Soul Gems filled with Grand Souls
    Grand Souls come from the most powerful creatures: Xivilai, Liches, Gloom Wraiths, Minotaur Lords. Cast Soul Trap on them before they die (or use a weapon with Soul Trap, like Umbra). You need a Grand Soul Gem in your inventory — the soul will auto-fill the appropriately sized gem. Buy empty Grand Soul Gems from Calindil at the Mystic Emporium.
  4. Enchant each armor piece with Chameleon
    At the Enchanting Altar, select your unenchanted armor piece, choose the Chameleon effect, and use a filled Grand Soul Gem. With a Grand Soul, each piece gets 20% Chameleon. Repeat for all 5 pieces.
  5. Equip and verify
    Check your Active Effects in the magic menu. It should show Chameleon 100%. If it shows less, you used a smaller soul or missed a piece. Fix and re-enchant.

Combat — The Sneak Attack Loop

Once you have 100% Chameleon, combat is fundamentally different from any other build. There is no "rotation" — there is only the backstab. Every single hit is a sneak attack because enemies never detect you.

The Ghost Kill Loop

  1. Walk up to the enemy (don't even crouch)
    At 100% Chameleon, you don't need to crouch. You can walk at full speed directly toward any enemy and you will not be detected. The game treats you as permanently hidden. However, crouching activates the sneak attack multiplier UI indicator, so crouch right before striking.
  2. Crouch and draw your dagger
    Enter sneak mode (crouch). You'll see the sneak eye indicator appear as "hidden" — it will always be hidden. Draw your dagger (Blade of Woe early, Mehrunes' Razor late). Position yourself behind the enemy for a backstab if possible, though positioning doesn't matter at 100% Chameleon.
  3. Strike — 8x Dagger Sneak Attack
    In Oblivion Remastered, daggers deal 8x sneak attack damage (compared to 6x for other melee weapons). With Mehrunes' Razor (base 14) and high Agility, a single sneak attack can deal 100+ damage before enchantment effects. Most enemies die in one hit.
  4. If the enemy survives — just hit them again
    Here's the broken part: Chameleon does not break on attack. After your first strike, you remain at 100% Chameleon. The enemy takes damage, looks around confused, and goes back to "searching." Your next hit is ALSO a sneak attack. Repeat until dead.
  5. For groups — summon + backstab each one
    Against multiple enemies, summon a Dremora or Scamp first. The enemies focus on your summon while you walk behind each one and deliver guaranteed sneak attacks. Efficient, effortless, elegant.
  6. For bosses — Mehrunes' Razor instant kill
    Mehrunes' Razor has a chance to instantly kill on each hit. Since you can hit bosses endlessly with sneak attacks (they never fight back), just keep stabbing until the instant kill procs. Even without the proc, 8x dagger damage kills most bosses in 2-3 hits.
Damage Breakdown

Most enemies in Oblivion have 100-300 HP. You one-shot or two-shot everything.

Threats That Still Hurt You

AoE spells and traps still do damage even though enemies can't target you. Fire traps in Ayleid ruins, poison gas in dungeons, and AoE Destruction spells from mages will still hit. Your 50% Breton Magic Resistance helps, but carry Restore Health potions. Also: falling damage is still real. You're invisible, not invincible.

The Arena Cheese

You can complete the entire Arena questline with 100% Chameleon. Enter the arena, crouch, and backstab your opponent. They won't fight back. Every match is a free win. The Grand Champion fight is just another backstab. It's hilarious.

Tips — Remastered Changes

Agility Governs Daggers (New in Remastered)

In the original Oblivion, Strength governed all melee damage. In the remaster, Agility governs dagger damage specifically. This makes Agility your most important damage stat. Stack it with Virtues. A Ghost with 80+ Agility deals significantly more sneak attack damage than one with 50 Agility.

Virtue System — No More Efficient Leveling

The old "sleep at the right time, train the right skills" micromanagement is gone. The Virtue system gives you 12 free attribute points per level-up. Endurance HP is retroactive. You can invest purely in Agility and Intelligence early without fear of permanently gimping your health pool. Just play the game.

Chameleon vs. Invisibility

Chameleon makes you partially transparent and reduces detection. At 100%, enemies cannot detect you at all. It never breaks — you can attack, interact, cast spells, and remain hidden. Invisibility makes you fully transparent but breaks on any action. For this build, Chameleon is strictly superior. Invisibility is only useful before you have your Chameleon gear.

You Can Still Talk to NPCs

At 100% Chameleon, NPCs can't see you, but you can still initiate conversation by walking up and pressing activate. They'll talk to you normally. Shopkeepers still sell to you. Quest givers still give quests. You just look like a shimmering ghost to them. Merchants don't care about your metaphysical state — gold is gold.

Save Before Enchanting

Always save before applying Sigil Stones or using the Enchanting Altar. Enchantments are permanent — you cannot remove or replace them. If you accidentally enchant the wrong piece or get the wrong effect, reload. Grand Soul Gems are rare and expensive. Don't waste them.

Crime is Free

At 100% Chameleon, you can steal anything in plain sight. Shopkeeper watching? Doesn't matter — they can't detect you. You can empty every shop, every house, every guild hall. Combine with the Gray Cowl of Nocturnal for a separate criminal identity, so if guards somehow catch you (they won't), the bounty goes to "The Gray Fox."

Repair Hammers Still Matter

Your daggers and armor still degrade with use. Carry Repair Hammers and repair between fights. A broken dagger does almost no damage, which means your sneak attack might not one-shot. Keep your gear maintained. Buy hammers from any blacksmith — they're cheap.

Alchemy for Free Potions

Even without investing in Alchemy, you can make Restore Health and Restore Magicka potions from common ingredients. Pick up everything. You'll need heals for traps and environmental damage. Poison potions are also excellent — apply Damage Health poisons to your dagger for even more burst on sneak attacks.

The Build is Modular

If 100% Chameleon feels "too easy" and you want more challenge, try running 80% Chameleon (4 pieces). Enemies will occasionally detect you in well-lit areas or at very close range, adding tension. You can also swap one Chameleon piece for a different enchant (Fortify Agility, Fortify Blade, Resist Magic) for a more balanced setup.

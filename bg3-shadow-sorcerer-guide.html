Shadow Magic Sorcerer — Umbral Executioner

Half-Elf / Drow · Sorcerer 12 · #5 Ranked Build
#5 Build Shadow Blade Darkness Combo Full Caster Patch 8

Overview

Patch 8 introduced the Shadow Magic subclass, and it immediately displaced Draconic Bloodline as the premier Sorcerer build in BG3. The core loop is devastating: cast Darkness on yourself for free (Eyes of the Dark), then attack with Shadow Blade — a finesse weapon that deals psychic damage and grants advantage on every attack in dim light or darkness. You see through your own Darkness; enemies don't. Every single swing has advantage while they swing blind.

Why Shadow Magic Replaced Draconic

Draconic Bloodline gave +CHA mod to one damage type and permanent Mage Armor (13 + DEX AC). Shadow Magic gives you permanent advantage on attacks via Darkness + Shadow Blade, a free shadow wolf summon (Hound of Ill Omen) that forces disadvantage on target saves, and Strength of the Grave to cheat death. The sustained damage from always-advantage Shadow Blade plus Quickened Spell metamagic creates a higher damage floor AND ceiling than Draconic ever could.

The Math

Shadow Blade at 3rd level deals 3d8 psychic per hit. With advantage on every attack (Darkness combo), your hit rate jumps from ~65% to ~88%. Quickened Shadow Blade lets you attack twice per round: one with your action, one quickened cantrip or another attack via Haste. That's roughly 27 expected psychic damage per round at level 5 — from a full caster who still has Fireball, Haste, and Counterspell in the tank. Draconic Fire Sorcerer can't compete with this sustained throughput.

Core Synergies

  • Combo Darkness + Shadow Blade — Free Darkness (Eyes of the Dark) + Shadow Blade = advantage on all attacks while enemies are blinded. You see through it; they don't.
  • Meta Quickened Spell — Quicken a cantrip or spell as bonus action, then attack with Shadow Blade. Or quicken Shadow Blade itself to attack on the same turn you cast Darkness.
  • Summon Hound of Ill Omen — Shadow wolf that gives targets disadvantage on saves against YOUR spells. Heightened Spell + Hound = near-guaranteed Hold Person.
  • Survive Strength of the Grave — When reduced to 0 HP, CHA save to stay at 1 HP instead. A free Relentless Endurance that recharges on long rest.

Race & Background

Best Race: Half-Elf (Drow Ancestry)

  • Racial Superior Darkvision (24m) — Extended darkvision range synergizes perfectly with Darkness + Shadow Blade. You operate at maximum effectiveness in low light environments.
  • Racial Fey Ancestry — Advantage on saves against being charmed, immunity to magical sleep. Solid defensive perk.
  • Racial Two Extra Skill Proficiencies — CHA-based class with flexible skill picks. Grab Persuasion and Deception for the party face role.
  • Racial Civil Militia — Light armor and shield proficiency. Useful early before your AC alternatives come online.

Alternative: Drow

  • Racial Superior Darkvision (24m) — Same extended range as Half-Elf Drow ancestry. Critical for the Darkness combo.
  • Racial Dancing Lights (cantrip) — Free cantrip for party utility when you're NOT using Darkness.
  • Racial Faerie Fire (free 1/day) — Gives advantage to the whole party against a group of enemies. Great when Darkness would hurt your allies.
Other Viable Races

Tiefling (Asmodeus) — fire resistance, free Darkness at level 5 (redundant but saves a spell known). Human — carrying capacity + Civil Militia. Half-Orc — Relentless Endurance stacks with Strength of the Grave for double death insurance, but no darkvision bonus.

Background

Pick Charlatan for Deception and Sleight of Hand (thematic and useful), or Noble for History and Persuasion. As a high-CHA character, you're the natural party face. Charlatan fits the shadowy manipulator fantasy.

Point buy. CHA 17 (odd for +1 feat later). DEX 14 is mandatory — Shadow Blade is finesse, so you attack with DEX, and it boosts AC with light/medium armor. CON 14 for concentration saves (Haste, Shadow Blade are both concentration).

Why DEX 14 Matters

Shadow Blade is a finesse weapon, meaning you use DEX for attack and damage rolls. Unlike Draconic Sorcerer who could dump DEX entirely, Shadow Magic needs 14 DEX for both the Shadow Blade attack bonus and medium armor AC. CHA is still your spellcasting stat for everything else — spell save DC, spell attack rolls for cantrips, and Strength of the Grave saves. The 17 CHA is intentional: take Resilient (CON) at level 4 for +1 CON (to 15) and concentration save proficiency, then +2 CHA at level 8 to reach 19, and use Hag's Hair for the 20 cap.

Feat Priority

  • Level 4 +2 CHA → 19 — Or take Resilient (CON) if you want concentration save proficiency early. Both are strong.
  • Level 8 +2 CHA → 20 (with Hag Hair) — Visit Auntie Ethel in Act 1 for +1 CHA (Hag's Hair). This lets you reach 20 CHA with just one ASI.
  • Level 12 Alert — +5 Initiative, can't be surprised. Going first means Darkness + Shadow Blade before enemies act. Devastating opener.
  • Alt L4 War Caster — Advantage on concentration saves. If you didn't take Resilient (CON), this is your concentration insurance.

Concentration Warning

Shadow Blade and Haste are both concentration. You can't run both on yourself. The play: Twin Haste on two allies, then use Shadow Blade + Darkness on yourself. Or: Shadow Blade on yourself, skip Haste entirely. Both work — Shadow Blade + Darkness alone is strong enough.

Hound of Ill Omen Heightened Spell
  • Subclass Hound of Ill Omen (3 SP) — Spend 3 Sorcery Points to summon a shadow dire wolf next to an enemy. The target has disadvantage on all saving throws against your spells while the Hound is within 5m. The Hound moves toward the target automatically.

Level 6 is a massive spike. The Hound turns your save-or-suck spells from "maybe they fail" to "they almost certainly fail." Plus the wolf itself deals decent damage and has HP to soak hits.

Shadow Magic Features

Shadow Magic Sorcerer gains four subclass features across 12 levels. Each one is build-defining.

Eyes of the Dark (Level 1 / 3)

Eyes of the Dark

Level 1: You gain Superior Darkvision (24m range). You can see in complete darkness as if it were dim light.
Level 3: You can cast Darkness using 2 Sorcery Points instead of a spell slot. When you do, you can see through the Darkness. This doesn't apply when casting with a spell slot — only with Sorcery Points. This is the single most important feature of the build.

Strength of the Grave (Level 1)

  • Trigger Reduced to 0 HP — Make a CHA saving throw. DC = the damage that reduced you. On success, you drop to 1 HP instead of falling unconscious.
  • Limit Once per long rest — Doesn't work against radiant damage or critical hits. Recharges on long rest. Stacks with Half-Orc Relentless Endurance if you chose that race.
  • Scaling CHA-dependent — Higher CHA = higher save bonus = more likely to succeed. Another reason to cap CHA at 20.

Hound of Ill Omen (Level 6)

  • Cost 3 Sorcery Points — Bonus action to summon. The hound appears within 9m of a target you can see.
  • Effect Disadvantage on saves — While the hound is within 5m of the target, the target has disadvantage on saving throws against YOUR spells only.
  • Stats Dire Wolf statblock — Uses dire wolf stats but is a monstrosity, not a beast. It has temporary HP equal to half your Sorcerer level. Moves toward target each turn automatically.
  • Combo Hound + Any save spell — The Hound makes Hold Person, Hold Monster, Banishment, and Disintegrate far more reliable. A Sorcerer-exclusive advantage that no other subclass gets.

Shadow Walk (Level 10)

  • Cost Free (Bonus Action) — No spell slot, no Sorcery Points. Requires you to be in dim light or darkness.
  • Range Up to 36m — Teleport to an unoccupied space you can see that is also in dim light or darkness. Double the range of Misty Step.
  • Combo Inside your Darkness — Since you're always in your own Darkness (and can see through it), Shadow Walk is always available. Free 36m teleport every turn as a bonus action.

Metamagic

You pick 2 Metamagic options at level 2 and a 3rd at level 3 (totaling 3 by level 3), then a 4th at level 10. Here's the priority order.

Twinned Spell SP = Level Twin Haste on two allies (6 SP). Twin Hold Person on two enemies (2 SP). Incredible value for single-target concentration spells.

Sorcery Point Economy

At level 12, you have 12 SP plus whatever you convert from spell slots. A typical combat: Darkness (2 SP) + Hound (3 SP) + Heightened Hold Monster (3 SP) = 8 SP in one round. That's expensive but fight-ending. For trash fights, just use Darkness (2 SP) + Shadow Blade (spell slot) and swing with advantage all day. Save the SP combos for bosses.

Combat Rotation

Standard Opener (Darkness + Shadow Blade)

  1. Cast Darkness (2 SP via Eyes of the Dark) Turn 1
    Center it on yourself or slightly ahead. You can see through it; enemies can't. All enemies inside are Blinded (disadvantage on attacks, can't target spells). All enemies outside can't see into it. Cost: 2 Sorcery Points (not a spell slot).
  2. Cast Shadow Blade (2nd+ level slot) Quickened
    Quicken Shadow Blade (3 SP) to cast it as a bonus action, then attack with it as your action on the same turn. Or cast it normally this turn and start attacking next turn. Upcast to 3rd/4th/5th level for 3d8/4d8/5d8 psychic.
  3. Attack with Shadow Blade (with advantage) Every Turn
    You're in Darkness. Shadow Blade grants advantage on attacks in dim light or darkness. Swing every turn with advantage. Enemies attack you with disadvantage (they're blinded). This is your sustained damage loop.
  4. Shadow Walk to reposition Bonus (Lv10+)
    At level 10, use Shadow Walk (free bonus action teleport up to 36m) to move to the next target without provoking opportunity attacks. You're always in darkness, so it's always available.

Boss Killer Combo (Hound + Hold)

  1. Summon Hound of Ill Omen (3 SP, bonus action) Pre-Cast
    Summon the shadow wolf next to the boss. It automatically moves toward the target and gives disadvantage on saves against your spells while within 5m.
  2. Heightened Hold Monster (3 SP + 5th level slot) Turn 1
    The target has disadvantage from both Hound and Heightened Spell. Even creatures with +8 WIS saves will fail most of the time. On failure: paralyzed, incapacitated, auto-fail STR/DEX saves, attacks within 3m auto-crit.
  3. Quickened Shadow Blade + attack (auto-crit) Same Turn
    Quicken Shadow Blade, then attack the paralyzed target. Automatic critical hit. 5d8 psychic doubled to 10d8 = average 45 psychic damage in a single hit. Have martial allies pile on for more crits.
  4. Disintegrate if still standing Turn 2
    If the boss survived the crit round, Disintegrate (10d6+40 force, DEX save with disadvantage from Hound) finishes them. If it kills them, they're completely destroyed — no revival, no death saves.

AoE / Ranged Fallback

  1. Fireball or Chain Lightning for groups Turn 1
    When enemies are spread out or flying, Shadow Blade isn't optimal. Drop a Fireball (8d6 fire) or Chain Lightning (10d8 + chain). Cast from outside your Darkness or drop concentration first.
  2. Quickened cantrip for extra damage Bonus
    After casting a leveled spell, Quicken Fire Bolt for bonus action damage. With Potent Robe, cantrips deal an extra +5 (CHA mod) damage.
Friendly Fire Warning

Your Darkness field blinds EVERYONE except you. Don't center it on your party. Position it so enemies are inside but allies are outside, or communicate with your team to stay clear. Alternatively, use Greater Invisibility instead of Darkness when your allies need line of sight.

Your Darkness field complicates party dynamics since allies can't see through it either. Build your party around ranged allies who stay outside the field, or martial allies who can fight without sight.

The Darkness Positioning Rule

Place Darkness so YOU are inside it and enemies are inside it, but your ranged allies are OUTSIDE looking in. They can't target specific enemies inside (Blinded), but they can use AoE spells on the area. Martial allies with Blindsight or Reckless Attack can fight inside the field. This is the key to making the party work.

Honour Mode Tips

Shadow Magic Sorcerer is one of the safest Honour Mode builds thanks to permanent defensive advantages (enemies attack at disadvantage in Darkness) and Strength of the Grave as death insurance.

  1. Darkness + Shadow Blade on turn 1, always Opener
    In Honour Mode, enemies hit harder and have more HP. Forcing disadvantage on all enemy attacks (via Darkness) is the single best defensive tool. Never skip the setup turn.
  2. Save Hound + Heightened for Legendary enemies Resource
    Honour Mode bosses have Legendary Actions and Legendary Resistance. Don't waste your Hound + Heightened combo on trash. Save the 6+ SP investment for boss encounters where a failed save means death.
  3. Subtle Counterspell is mandatory Every Caster Fight
    Honour Mode casters WILL Counterspell your key spells. Subtle Counterspell (1 SP) can't be countered. Always hold your reaction for caster fights.
  4. Strength of the Grave + backup plan Safety Net
    In Honour Mode, if you die it's over. Strength of the Grave gives you one free save from death. Stack it with Amulet of Greater Health (CON 23 = massive HP pool) and Shield reactions. You should almost never go down.
  5. Use Greater Invisibility against immune enemies Adaptation
    Some Honour Mode enemies have Devil's Sight or Blindsight, negating your Darkness. Switch to Greater Invisibility for advantage on attacks without relying on darkness. Always have a plan B.

Hardest Honour Mode Fights — Shadow Sorcerer Approach

  • Act 1 Grym (Adamantine Forge) — Immune to most damage. Don't waste spells — use the forge hammer mechanic. Save resources for real fights.
  • Act 2 Ketheric Thorm — Phase 1 is humanoid: Hold Person + Shadow Blade crits. Phase 2 (Apostle of Myrkul): use Hold Monster + Hound. The Darkness field protects against his AoE darkness — ironic, since you see through it.
  • Act 3 Raphael (House of Hope) — Fiend with Legendary Resistance. Burn through his resistances with cheap save spells first, then Hound + Heightened Hold Monster. He has fire immunity, so use Shadow Blade (psychic) and Disintegrate (force). Your Darkness protects against his Hellfire orbs.
  • Act 3 Orin the Red — Humanoid. Hold Person with Hound + Heightened. She has Unstoppable (Legendary Action) but only one charge. Force a save, she burns Unstoppable, then Hold Person again. Shadow Blade crits finish her.
  • Final The Netherbrain — Not humanoid, massive HP. Use Hold Monster with Hound. Darkness field protects your party from adds. Chain Lightning for tentacle adds. This is a war of attrition — your defensive Darkness advantage keeps you alive long enough to win.
Honour Mode Golden Rule

Pre-buff before every fight. Mage Armor should already be active. Position Darkness before combat starts (via stealth). Shadow Blade can be pre-cast if you anticipate combat. The Shadow Sorcerer's advantage is that the setup only costs 2 SP (Darkness) + one spell slot (Shadow Blade) — sustainable across multiple fights without long resting.

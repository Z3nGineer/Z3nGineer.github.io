Throwzerker — Avernus Catapult

Shield Dwarf · Berserker 5 / Thief 4 / Champion 3 · Thrown Weapon Specialist
Damage Thrown Builds Multiclass Patch 8

Overview

The Throwzerker is the most explosive damage build in Baldur's Gate 3. You hurl weapons at enemies from range with the force of a freight train, using Tavern Brawler to add double your Strength modifier to every thrown attack. With Berserker Frenzy, Thief's extra bonus action, Extra Attack, and Fighter's Action Surge, you can unleash 5–6 thrown attacks in a single turn — each one dealing massive damage with returning weapons that fly right back to your hand.

This build peaks at level 12 with a terrifying combination: Rage for bonus damage on every throw, Frenzy for a bonus action throw, Thief for a second bonus action throw, Extra Attack for two throws per action, and Action Surge for another full action of throws. That is up to 6 thrown weapon attacks in one turn, each benefiting from Tavern Brawler, Rage damage, and whatever enchantment your weapon carries.

Why Thrown Weapons?

In BG3, thrown weapons use Strength for attack and damage rolls. Tavern Brawler adds your STR modifier a second time to thrown attacks, effectively doubling your damage bonus. A character with 20 STR adds +10 damage to every thrown attack instead of +5. Multiply that by 5–6 attacks per turn and you get the highest sustained single-target DPR in the game.

Race — Shield Dwarf (Recommended)

  • Racial Dwarven Resilience — Advantage on poison saves, resistance to poison damage. Very common enemy type.
  • Racial Darkvision — 12m range. Essential for Underdark and Shadow-cursed lands.
  • Racial Dwarven Toughness — +1 HP per level. Adds up to 12 extra HP, compensating for no heavy armor.
Alternative: Half-Orc

Savage Attacks adds an extra damage die on crits. With 5–6 attacks per turn, you crit more often through sheer volume. Relentless Endurance saves you from going down once per long rest. Both are excellent for an aggressive build like this.

Tavern Brawler — The Key Feat

Tavern Brawler is the single most important feat in this build. Without it, this is just a regular Barbarian. With it, you become a siege weapon.

How It Works in BG3

BG3's Tavern Brawler is different from tabletop D&D 5e. In BG3, it reads: "When you make an unarmed attack, or attack with an improvised weapon, or throw something, your Strength modifier is added twice to the damage and Attack Rolls."

This means with 20 STR (+5 modifier), every thrown weapon attack gets +10 to hit and +10 to damage instead of +5/+5. The feat also gives +1 STR, bringing your 17 to 18 at level 4.

Tavern Brawler also boosts attack rolls

Double STR to hit means you almost never miss. With 20 STR and proficiency, your attack bonus is +15 or higher. You will hit even high-AC enemies on Honour Mode reliably.

  • Feature Danger Sense — Advantage on DEX saves against things you can see. Helps survive AoE.
  • Tip Always use Reckless Attack. Advantage on 5+ attacks per turn is an enormous DPS increase.

Frenzy Frenzied Strike Enraged Throw

  • Feature Enraged Throw — Bonus action to throw a weapon or object. Deals extra damage equal to Rage bonus. This is your bread and butter.
  • Tip In BG3, Frenzy does NOT cause exhaustion like in tabletop. It is pure upside.
No Exhaustion Penalty

In tabletop 5e, Berserker Frenzy gives a level of exhaustion. BG3 completely removed this penalty. Frenzy is free bonus action attacks with zero downside.

Tavern Brawler +1 STR → 18
  • Feat Tavern Brawler — Double STR mod on thrown attack and damage rolls. Also +1 STR (17→18). This is the entire build.
  • STR 18 +4 modifier, doubled to +8 on all thrown attacks. Massive jump in both accuracy and damage.
Do NOT Skip This Feat

Tavern Brawler is non-negotiable. Without it, this build does not work. Every other feat choice (GWM, Sentinel, etc.) is strictly worse for a throw build. Take it at Barbarian 4, no exceptions.

End of Barbarian Levels

Barbarian 5 is your last Barbarian level. You now have everything you need: Extra Attack, Frenzy, Tavern Brawler, Reckless Attack. Time to multiclass into Rogue for even more bonus actions.

Fast Hands (2nd Bonus Action!) Second-Story Work
  • Subclass Thief — The only subclass in the game that gives an extra bonus action
  • Feature Fast Hands — You gain an additional bonus action each turn. That means TWO Enraged Throws per turn (Frenzy + Fast Hands). Your throw count per turn goes from 3 to 4.
  • Feature Second-Story Work — Resistance to falling damage. Minor but nice for positioning.
The Second Build-Defining Level

Fast Hands is the second pillar of this build. Frenzy gives one bonus action throw. Fast Hands gives another. Combined with Extra Attack (2 throws), you now throw 4 weapons per turn without spending any resources beyond Rage.

9 Rogue 4 Second Feat — +2 STR ▸
+2 STR → 20 (Max!)
  • Feat +2 STR → 20 — Caps Strength. +5 modifier, doubled to +10 by Tavern Brawler. Your throws now hit like a ballista.
  • Alt Alert — +5 initiative. Going first is huge for a burst damage build. Take this if you already have Elixirs boosting STR to 21+.
Elixir Strategy

If you use Elixir of Hill Giant Strength (STR 21) or Cloud Giant Strength (STR 27), your STR score doesn't matter. In that case, take Alert or Savage Attacker instead of +2 STR. Cloud Giant at level 12 means +8 modifier doubled to +16 per throw.

10 Fighter 1 Fighting Style + Second Wind ▸
Archery (+2 ranged) Second Wind
  • Feature Fighting Style: Archery — +2 to ranged attack rolls. Thrown weapons ARE ranged attacks, so this stacks with Tavern Brawler. Your to-hit becomes absurd.
  • Feature Second Wind — Bonus action heal 1d10+Fighter level. Nice emergency sustain.
11 Fighter 2 Action Surge ★ Power Spike ▸
Action Surge
  • Feature Action Surge — Once per short rest, gain an additional action. That's 2 more throws (Extra Attack applies to the surge action too). Turns your 4-throw turn into a 6-throw nuke turn.
The Nuke Turn

Action (2 throws) + Action Surge (2 throws) + Frenzy Throw (1) + Fast Hands Throw (1) = 6 thrown weapon attacks in a single turn. With Nyrulna and 20+ STR, this one-shots most bosses.

12 Fighter 3 Champion — Improved Critical ▸
Improved Critical (19–20)
  • Subclass Champion — Crit on 19 or 20 instead of just 20
  • Feature Improved Critical — Doubles your crit chance. With Reckless Attack (advantage = 2 rolls per attack) and 5–6 attacks per turn, you crit multiple times per combat.
  • Alt Battle Master — Superiority Dice add damage and utility (Trip Attack, Precision Attack). More consistent but lower ceiling than Champion's crit fishing with this many attacks.
Build Complete

At level 12 you have the full kit: Tavern Brawler, Extra Attack, Frenzy, Fast Hands, Action Surge, Improved Critical, Reckless Attack. You are the highest single-target damage dealer in Baldur's Gate 3. Enjoy deleting bosses.

Subclass Breakdown

Berserker (Barbarian — Required)

Berserker is the only Barbarian subclass that works for this build. Frenzy gives you a bonus action attack (Enraged Throw) while raging, which is the entire reason you take Barbarian past level 2. Wild Magic is random and unreliable; Wildheart gives resistances but no extra attacks. Berserker is locked in.

  • Level 3 Frenzy — Bonus action Enraged Throw or Frenzied Strike while raging
  • Level 3 Enraged Throw — Throw a weapon or heavy object as bonus action. Deals extra Rage damage.
  • Level 3 Frenzied Strike — Bonus action melee attack. Use when in melee range and out of throwable weapons (rare).

Thief (Rogue — Required)

Thief is the only Rogue subclass that provides a second bonus action. Assassin gives auto-crit on surprised enemies (nice but situational). Arcane Trickster gives minor spells (useless while raging). Thief gives a permanent extra bonus action every single turn. No competition.

  • Level 3 Fast Hands — Additional bonus action. Throw again, Dash, Disengage, use items — your choice every turn.
  • Level 3 Second-Story Work — Falling damage resistance. Lets you jump off high ground without worry.

Champion vs Battle Master (Fighter)

Both are strong. Champion is the default recommendation because Improved Critical synergizes with your high attack volume.

Champion

Crit on 19–20. With Reckless Attack (advantage) and 5–6 attacks per turn, you effectively roll 10–12 d20s. The probability of at least one crit per turn is extremely high. Crits double all damage dice (weapon die + Sneak Attack die + Rage bonus).

Battle Master (Alternative)

4 Superiority Dice (d8). Trip Attack knocks prone on hit (advantage for party). Precision Attack adds d8 to an attack roll (guarantee hits). Menacing Attack frightens targets. More tactical, slightly lower peak damage. Pick this for Honour Mode if consistency matters more than burst.

Thrown Weapon Mechanics in BG3

Thrown weapons in BG3 have unique rules that make this build possible. Understanding them is critical.

Key Mechanics

  • Return Returning weapons come back — Weapons with the "Returning" property fly back to your hand after being thrown. Without this, you'd need a full inventory of throwable weapons. Nyrulna and the Returning Pike are essential.
  • STR Thrown = STR-based ranged — Thrown weapons use your Strength modifier for attack and damage. They count as ranged attacks (so Archery fighting style applies) but use STR, not DEX.
  • Range 18m standard range — Most thrown weapons have 18m range in BG3. That is well beyond melee and lets you attack from safety.
  • Tavern Tavern Brawler applies — TB specifically says "throw something" — all thrown weapon attacks get doubled STR modifier.
  • Rage Rage bonus applies — Rage bonus damage (+2/+3) applies to thrown weapon attacks. Thrown weapons count as "weapon attacks" for Rage purposes.
Non-Returning Weapons

If you throw a weapon that does NOT have the Returning property, it stays where it lands and you must pick it up. Early game (Acts 1–2), stock up on Handaxes and Javelins from vendors. Late game, Nyrulna solves this permanently.

Throw Types

  • Action Regular Throw — Use your action to throw. Extra Attack means 2 throws per action. Your main damage source.
  • Bonus Enraged Throw — Berserker Frenzy bonus action. Deals weapon damage + STR mod + Rage bonus. Can also throw heavy objects (environmental kills).
  • Bonus Fast Hands Throw — Your second bonus action from Thief. Throw again with the same weapon.

Combat Guide

Standard Turn (No Resources Spent)

  1. Rage / Frenzy Turn 1 Only
    Activate Frenzy (replaces Rage) at the start of combat. Costs a bonus action on the first turn only. Lasts 10 turns. You need this active for Enraged Throw.
  2. Reckless Attack + Throw x2 Every Turn
    Toggle Reckless Attack on. Use your action to throw your returning weapon twice (Extra Attack). Advantage on both throws from Reckless Attack. Sneak Attack triggers on the first hit.
  3. Enraged Throw (Frenzy) Bonus Action 1
    Use your first bonus action for an Enraged Throw. This is the Berserker's Frenzy ability. Same weapon, same Tavern Brawler bonus.
  4. Throw Again (Fast Hands) Bonus Action 2
    Use your second bonus action (from Thief) to throw again. 4 total throws per turn, every turn, no resources spent.

The Nuke Turn (Action Surge)

  1. Reckless Attack + Throw x2 Action
    Two throws with advantage. Sneak Attack on first hit.
  2. Action Surge + Throw x2 Surge
    Action Surge gives a second action. Two more throws with advantage. 4 throws from actions alone.
  3. Enraged Throw + Fast Hands Throw Both Bonus Actions
    Two more bonus action throws. Total: 6 thrown weapon attacks in one turn. With Nyrulna, each hit deals 1d6+3+10 (TB)+3 (Rage)+3d4 (thunder) = ~27 average damage. Six hits = ~162 damage.
Damage Estimate — Nuke Turn with Nyrulna + 20 STR

6 attacks x (3.5 + 3 + 10 + 3 + 7.5) average = 6 x 27 = 162 base damage. Add Sneak Attack (3.5), Ring of Flinging (6 x 2.5 = 15), crits, and advantage rerolls. Realistic nuke turn: 180–220+ damage. This one-shots the Apostle of Myrkul on Tactician.

Positioning Tips

  • Range Stay at 12–18m — You are a ranged attacker. No need to stand in melee. Throw from behind your tank.
  • Height Use high ground — High ground gives +2 to ranged attack rolls. Stack with Reckless + TB for near-guaranteed hits.
  • Frenzy Keep Rage active — Rage drops if you end a turn without attacking or taking damage. Always throw at least once per turn.
  • Objects Throw environment objects — Enraged Throw can hurl heavy objects (barrels, rocks, enemies). Throw explosive barrels for massive AoE.
Throw Enemies at Enemies

Enraged Throw can throw creatures. If you succeed on the STR check (easy with 20 STR + Expertise Athletics), you throw one enemy into another, dealing damage to both and knocking both prone. This is hilarious and effective.

Honour Mode Tips

The Throwzerker is one of the safest Honour Mode builds because you deal massive damage from range while Raging for physical resistance. Here are specific tips for the hardest difficulty.

Honour Mode Changes

Bosses have Legendary Actions in Honour Mode. They can interrupt your turn, add extra attacks, and resist conditions. Your best defense is overwhelming offense — kill them before they use Legendary Actions.

Boss Strategy

  • Prep Pre-buff before boss rooms — Drink Elixir of Cloud Giant Strength (STR 27). Pre-position party. Have a Haste caster ready.
  • Alpha Nuke turn 1 — Open with Action Surge turn. 6 throws at a single target. Many Honour Mode bosses can be killed or nearly killed in one turn.
  • Sustain 4 throws per turn baseline — Even without Action Surge, 4 throws per turn is enormous sustained damage. Short rest to get Surge back between fights.
  • Safety Rage gives resistance — Half damage from physical attacks while raging. Combined with medium armor and decent DEX/CON, you are surprisingly tanky.

Dangerous Fights

  • Act 1 Grym (Adamantine Forge) — Immune to most damage except when standing on lava. Use the forge hammer mechanic. Your throws won't help here — this is a puzzle boss.
  • Act 2 Ketheric Thorm — Two-phase fight. Save Action Surge for phase 2 (Apostle of Myrkul). Nuke the Apostle immediately.
  • Act 3 Raphael — High AC, Legendary Resistance. Use Reckless Attack + TB for near-guaranteed hits. Focus Raphael, ignore adds. Nuke turn can end him in 1–2 rounds.
  • Act 3 Orin — 1v1 duel (if chosen). Your throw build works perfectly solo. Rage, Reckless, throw 4 times per turn. She dies fast.
  • Final Netherbrain — Multiple phases with adds. Throw at the brain, ignore minions (party handles them). Action Surge on the final phase.
Battle Master for Honour Mode?

If consistency over burst appeals to you, Battle Master with Precision Attack (add d8 to attack roll) guarantees hits against high-AC Honour Mode bosses. Trip Attack knocks prone, giving advantage to your entire party. It is a valid alternative to Champion in Honour specifically.

Recommended Party

You are the primary damage dealer. Build your party around enabling your throws and covering your weaknesses (healing, crowd control, utility).

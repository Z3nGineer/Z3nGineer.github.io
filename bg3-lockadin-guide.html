Lockadin — Infernal Crusader

Half-Elf · Paladin 7 / Warlock 5 · #3 Ranked Build (Patch 8)
Melee Nova Multiclass Short Rest Smites Patch 8 Honour Viable

Overview

The Lockadin is the most devastating single-target damage dealer in Baldur's Gate 3. By combining Paladin's Divine Smite with Warlock's short-rest-recharging spell slots, you get a character that can smite on virtually every attack, every fight, without ever running dry. Add Eldritch Blast for ranged firepower, Hexblade's Curse-style crit fishing, and Aura of Protection for party-wide saving throw boosts, and you have a build that excels at everything.

The Core Mechanic — Infinite Smites

Warlock spell slots recharge on a Short Rest. Divine Smite lets you burn any spell slot to add radiant damage to melee hits. Since BG3 gives you 2 short rests per long rest, you effectively triple your smite fuel. A normal Paladin burns through slots by Act 2. A Lockadin never stops.

Why #3?

This build is ranked #3 because it combines top-tier burst damage, excellent sustained damage via short rest slots, strong party utility from Paladin Auras, and a viable ranged option with Eldritch Blast. Only the Swords Bard (#1) and Sorlock (#2) compete, and neither matches the Lockadin's mix of defense, aura support, and melee devastation.

Race & Background

Race — Half-Elf (High or Wood)

  • Racial Darkvision — 12m range. See in the dark without torches.
  • Racial Fey Ancestry — Advantage on saves vs charm. Can't be put to sleep.
  • Racial Civil Militia — Shield + weapon proficiencies (redundant but free).
  • High Free Cantrip — Take Fire Bolt or Minor Illusion from the Wizard list.
  • Wood Fleet of Foot — +1.5m movement. Better for melee uptime.
Alternative Races

Zariel Tiefling — Thematic and strong. Free Searing Smite (level 3) and Branding Smite (level 5) once per long rest. Fire resistance.

Human — Civil Militia + extra carrying capacity. Solid but boring.

Githyanki — Free Misty Step at level 5. Medium armor + sword proficiency. Good if you want mobility.

Background

Pick Soldier (Athletics + Intimidation) for the most thematic and mechanically useful option. Athletics fuels Shove attempts and Intimidation synergizes with your high Charisma. Noble works too for Persuasion if you want to be the party face.

Half-Elf bonuses: +2 CHA (included above), +1 STR, +1 CON. STR for melee attacks early game. CHA is your spellcasting stat for both classes and powers Aura of Protection. You'll cap CHA at 20 by level 8.

STR vs CHA for Attacks

You start with STR-based attacks (levels 1-4). Once you get Pact of the Blade at Warlock 3, your pact weapon uses CHA for attack and damage rolls. After that, STR only matters for Athletics checks and carrying capacity. CHA does everything: attack rolls, damage, spell DC, spell attack, and Aura of Protection.

Smite Math

With 2 short rests per long rest, you have: 4 Paladin slots + (2 Warlock slots × 3 rests) = 10 smites per long rest. Each level 3 Warlock smite: 3d8 radiant (13.5 avg). That's 135 bonus radiant damage per day, just from smites.

Subclass Choices

Paladin Oath

Oath of Vengeance (Recommended)

Vow of Enmity — Advantage on all attacks against one target for 10 turns. No concentration. Turns every attack into a near-guaranteed hit, and doubles your crit chance (crit = bonus smite damage).

Oath Spells: Hunter's Mark (free), Hold Person, Misty Step, Haste (level 9).

Relentless Avenger (Lvl 7): Free movement after opportunity attacks.

Oathbreaker (Highest Damage)

Spiteful Suffering — 1d4+CHA necrotic per turn to a target. No save, no concentration. Free damage.

Aura of Hate (Lvl 7): Add CHA modifier to melee damage for you AND any undead/fiend allies. +5 damage per hit at CHA 20. Stacks with everything.

How to break: Kill the tieflings in the Druid Grove, or betray certain NPCs. Easy to trigger in Act 1.

Verdict

Vengeance is safer and more consistent — Vow of Enmity is always good. Oathbreaker has a higher damage ceiling with Aura of Hate stacking on every melee hit and boosting undead summons, but requires an evil playthrough or deliberate oath-breaking. Both are excellent.

Warlock Patron

The Fiend (Recommended)

Dark One's Blessing: Gain temp HP equal to CHA + Warlock level every time you kill something. With your burst damage, you kill a LOT. This keeps you topped off in every fight.

Spells: Burning Hands, Command, Scorching Ray, Fireball.

The Great Old One (Utility)

Mortal Reminder: When you crit, nearby enemies are Frightened for 2 turns. With Vow of Enmity doubling crit chance, this triggers often.

Spells: Tasha's Hideous Laughter, Dissonant Whispers, Phantasmal Force.

Best Combo

Vengeance + Fiend is the most popular and consistent. Vow of Enmity gives you advantage (more crits, more smites land), and Dark One's Blessing keeps you alive. Oathbreaker + Fiend is highest raw damage if you want to go full dark knight.

Warlock Mechanics Explained

Short Rest Slot Recovery

Paladin spell slots recharge on long rest only. Warlock spell slots recharge on short rest. BG3 gives you 2 short rests per long rest (3 total rest cycles).

At Warlock 5 you have 2 Warlock slots. That means: 2 slots × 3 rest cycles = 6 Warlock-powered smites per long rest, on top of your 4 Paladin slots. Total: 10 smites per adventuring day.

A pure Paladin 12 has 9 slots total. You have 10 smites AND Eldritch Blast AND Invocations AND Pact of the Blade. The math doesn't even close.

Pact of the Blade

Your pact weapon uses CHA for attack and damage rolls. This means CHA is your ONLY important stat — it powers your melee, your spells, your Aura of Protection, and your Eldritch Blast. True single-attribute dependency (SAD build).

You can bind ANY weapon as your pact weapon. Found an amazing greatsword? Bind it. Want a glaive for Polearm Master? Bind it. The weapon doesn't need to be summoned — binding an existing weapon works.

Eldritch Blast Scaling

Eldritch Blast scales with character level, not class level. At character level 5 it fires 2 beams. At level 11, 3 beams (though you cap at 12 in BG3). Each beam can trigger Hex (+1d6) and Agonizing Blast (+CHA mod).

At level 11 with Agonizing Blast + Hex: 2 beams × (1d10 + 5 + 1d6) = 2d10 + 2d6 + 10 per turn at range, no slot cost. This is better than most martial characters' ranged damage.

Divine Smite + Warlock Slots

BG3 lets you smite with any spell slot, regardless of source. Warlock level 3 slots deal 3d8 radiant (4d8 vs undead/fiends) per smite. On a crit, all smite dice are doubled: 6d8 radiant (8d8 vs undead). Average crit smite: 27-36 bonus radiant damage. PER HIT.

Combat Guide

Standard Rotation

  1. Pre-buff Before Combat
    Cast Hex (bonus action, lasts until long rest). Activate Bless on yourself and 2 allies if expecting a tough fight. Vow of Enmity on the priority target (Vengeance).
  2. Engage — Darkness (optional) Turn 1
    If you have Devil's Sight, drop Darkness on yourself. You now have advantage on all attacks and enemies have disadvantage on you. Skip this vs scattered enemies.
  3. Attack + Smite Every Turn
    Attack twice (Extra Attack). Smite on both hits using Warlock slots first. With Hex, each hit deals: weapon damage + CHA + 1d6 (Hex) + 2-3d8 (Smite). On a crit, double all dice.
  4. Ranged Fallback When Needed
    If you can't reach melee, Eldritch Blast: 2 beams at 1d10+5+1d6 each. Better than most martials' ranged options. Push enemies off cliffs with Repelling Blast.
  5. Short Rest Between Fights
    After every 2-3 fights, short rest. Warlock slots refill. You're back to full smite capacity while the rest of the party is running low. This is the build's core advantage.

Burst Rotation (Boss Kills)

  1. Vow of Enmity Turn 0
    Advantage on all attacks vs target for 10 turns. Doubles your crit chance. No concentration.
  2. Hex + Bonus Action Smite Spell Bonus Action
    Cast Hex if not already active. Or use Thunderous Smite for knockback + extra damage.
  3. Attack 1 + Divine Smite (Level 3 slot) Action
    First attack. If it hits, smite with a level 3 Warlock slot: weapon + CHA + 3d8 radiant + 1d6 necrotic. On a crit: weapon + CHA + 6d8 + 2d6.
  4. Attack 2 + Divine Smite (Level 3 slot) Action
    Second attack. Smite again. Both Warlock slots are now spent, but the target is probably dead or close to it.
Damage Example (Single Turn, Level 11)

Greatsword (2d6+5) + Smite (3d8) + Hex (1d6) = ~28 damage per hit. Two attacks = ~56 damage. On a crit (doubled dice): ~80+ damage in a single turn. With Oathbreaker's Aura of Hate (+5 per hit): ~66 normal, ~90+ crit. No other build in BG3 matches this single-target burst.

Honour Mode Tips

Honour Mode Changes

Honour Mode gives bosses Legendary Actions (extra turns/reactions), higher HP, and sometimes new mechanics. Your burst damage and Counterspell are critical here.

General Strategy

  • Priority Always keep Counterspell ready — Honour bosses love to cast Hold Person, Banishment, and Power Word Kill. One Counterspell can save a run.
  • Priority Short rest religiously — In Honour Mode, attrition kills you. Short rest after every 2 fights to refill Warlock slots.
  • Priority Aura positioning — Your +5 save bonus is 3m radius. Keep allies clustered. This is the difference between surviving boss AoEs and wiping.
  • Tip Save smites for crits — In Honour Mode, resources matter more. Don't auto-smite every hit. Wait for crits (especially with Vow of Enmity active) to maximize value.
  • Tip Darkness is your panic button — Drop Darkness when things go wrong. Enemies can't target you, can't cast spells on you, and have disadvantage on melee. You can heal/reposition safely inside.

Key Honour Mode Boss Tips

  • Act 1 Grym (Adamantine Forge) — Immune to most damage. Lure onto the forge plate, superheat with lava, then smash with the hammer. Save your smites for when he's vulnerable.
  • Act 2 Ketheric Thorm — Phase 2 has massive HP. Vow of Enmity + double smite + Hex burns him down. Save Counterspell for his self-heals.
  • Act 3 Raphael — One of the hardest fights. His Legendary Actions are brutal. Darkness + Devil's Sight to avoid his targeting. Counterspell his Power Word Kill. Smite the pillars first to remove his immortality.
  • Act 3 Final Boss — Multiple phases. Conserve Warlock slots for the final phase. Aura of Protection keeps the party alive through AoE. This build is one of the safest for the final fight.
Honour Mode Insurance

The Lockadin is one of the safest Honour Mode builds because it combines high burst (end fights fast), Aura of Protection (survive save-or-die effects), Counterspell (negate threats), Lay on Hands (free emergency healing), and heavy armor + shield (high AC). You have answers to everything.

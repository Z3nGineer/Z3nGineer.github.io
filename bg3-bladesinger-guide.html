Bladesinger Wizard — The Arcane Dancer

High Elf · Bladesinger Wizard 10 / Fighter 2 · Patch 8 Meta
Bladesinger Melee + Full Caster 25+ AC Extra Attack + Cantrip Patch 8

Overview

The Bladesinger Wizard is the new meta Wizard build in Baldur's Gate 3 as of Patch 8, replacing Abjuration as the top-tier arcane option. Bladesong adds your Intelligence modifier to AC and Concentration saving throws, making you absurdly tanky for a Wizard. At level 6, Extra Attack lets you swing a weapon AND cast a cantrip in the same Attack action. You are a full 6th-level spell caster who fights in melee, wears no heavy armor, and still hits 25+ AC while maintaining concentration on Haste or Shadow Blade.

Why It Replaced Abjuration

Abjuration Ward gave you temporary HP. Bladesong gives you +INT to AC (up to +5), +INT to Concentration saves, +10 movement speed, and advantage on Acrobatics. That is better defense, better mobility, and better concentration all in one feature. Add Extra Attack at Wizard 6 — which Abjuration never gets — and you have a Wizard who can fight in melee AND cast a full cantrip each turn. Abjuration has no answer to that combination.

The Fantasy

You are an elven warrior-mage who dances through combat with a rapier in one hand and arcane power in the other. Bladesong makes you untouchable (25+ AC with Shield), Extra Attack lets you stab someone and Booming Blade them in the same action, and you still have full access to Fireball, Haste, Counterspell, and Wall of Force. No other class in BG3 combines this level of melee competence with the full Wizard spell list.

Core Synergies

  • AC Bladesong + DEX + INT — Unarmored base of 10 + DEX + INT (Bladesong) + Shield reaction = 25-30 AC. Untouchable.
  • Attack Extra Attack (Bladesinger) — One weapon attack + one cantrip (Booming Blade) in the same Attack action. Unique to Bladesinger.
  • Conc INT to Concentration — Bladesong adds INT to CON saves for concentration. With War Caster, you almost never drop Haste/Shadow Blade.
  • Spells Full Wizard Spell List — You are still a full Wizard. Fireball, Counterspell, Wall of Force, Haste — every tool in the game.

Race & Background

Best Race: High Elf

  • Racial Longsword/Shortsword/Longbow Proficiency — Gives you martial weapon proficiency for rapier-tier weapons without a multiclass dip. Essential for early melee.
  • Racial Extra Cantrip — One free cantrip from any class. Take a utility option since you already have plenty of damage cantrips.
  • Racial Darkvision + Fey Ancestry — 12m Darkvision for Act 2 darkness. Fey Ancestry prevents sleep and grants advantage vs charm.

Alternative: Githyanki

  • Racial Medium Armor + Sword Proficiency — Greatsword and medium armor proficiency. You will not use heavy armor (breaks Bladesong), but medium armor gives an early-game AC option before your INT is maxed.
  • Racial Misty Step (free) — Free once per long rest. Saves a prepared spell slot for the best repositioning tool in the game.
  • Racial Githyanki Psionics — Mage Hand for free at level 1, Jump at level 3. Extra utility without spell slots.

Alternative: Wood Elf

  • Racial Extra Movement Speed — 1.5m extra base speed stacks with Bladesong's +10 feet. You become the fastest character in the game.
  • Racial Elf Weapon Training — Same weapon proficiencies as High Elf. Rapier access from level 1.
  • Racial Mask of the Wild — Stealth proficiency for scouting. Useful for a character who can Misty Step into position.
Other Viable Races

Half-Elf (High) — Extra cantrip + Darkvision + Fey Ancestry + two extra skill proficiencies. More versatile but slightly less thematic. Human — Carrying capacity and Civil Militia (shield proficiency), but you cannot use shields during Bladesong anyway.

Background

Pick Sage for Arcana and History proficiency — fits the scholar-warrior archetype and you use INT for both. Noble is an alternative for History and Persuasion if you want social skills, though your CHA will be low.

Why INT is Everything

Intelligence fuels your spell save DC, spell attack bonus, AND your AC during Bladesong. Every +1 INT is +1 AC, +1 to concentration saves, and better spells. Going from INT 16 to 20 is literally +2 AC, +2 to all concentration saves, and +2 spell DC. No other class benefits this much from a single ability score. Max INT first, always.

Feat Priority for Ability Scores

  • Level 4 War Caster — Advantage on concentration saves. Combined with Bladesong's +INT to concentration, you basically never drop Haste or Shadow Blade. This is the #1 feat for Bladesinger, no contest.
  • Level 8 +2 INT → 20 — Or take Resilient (CON) if you started with odd CON. Maxing INT = +5 AC from Bladesong, highest possible spell DC.
  • Level 12 Alert — +5 initiative. Going first means Bladesong is up before anyone attacks you. Combined with DEX 16, you act first almost every fight.

This 2-level Fighter dip costs you 6th-level Wizard spells (like Chain Lightning and Globe of Invulnerability). If you prefer the full spell list, go pure Wizard 12 instead. Action Surge is incredible, but 6th-level spells are also incredible. Both paths are viable.

12 Fighter 2 Action Surge ★ Power Spike ▸
Action Surge
  • Feature Action Surge — One additional action per short rest. Use it to cast TWO full spells in one turn (e.g., Fireball + Fireball, or Hold Monster + full Attack action with Extra Attack). The most broken feature in BG3 for spellcasters.
  • Combo Hold Monster → Action Surge → Attack x2 — Hold Monster (action 1) → Action Surge → Attack + Booming Blade on paralyzed target (action 2) = guaranteed crits. Delete bosses.

Action Surge once per short rest is worth more than 6th-level spells for burst potential. Two full actions means double Fireball, or spell + full melee combo. This is why Fighter 2 is the go-to multiclass for Wizards.

Bladesong Mechanics

Bladesong is the heart of this build. Understanding exactly what it does and how to maintain it is critical to playing a Bladesinger correctly.

Activating Bladesong

Action cost: Bonus action to activate.
Duration: 1 minute (10 rounds in combat).
Uses: 2 per short rest. You will have it up for every fight as long as you short rest between encounters.
Free hand: You must have one hand free or be wielding a one-handed weapon. No shields, no two-handed weapons.

What Bladesong Gives You

+INT modifier to AC — At INT 20, this is +5 AC. This stacks with Mage Armor, DEX, and Shield. It is the single largest AC boost available to any Wizard.

+INT modifier to Concentration saves — At INT 20, +5 to CON saves for concentration. Combined with War Caster (advantage) and proficiency bonus (+4 at level 10), your concentration save is effectively +9 with advantage. You pass DC 10 checks with 99.75% reliability.

+10 feet movement speed — Extra mobility for positioning in and out of melee. Combined with Wood Elf racial, you can reach any target on the battlefield.

Advantage on Acrobatics checks — Prevents enemies from shoving you. A Bladesinger in Bladesong is extremely hard to knock prone.

What Ends Bladesong

Heavy armor: Equipping heavy armor immediately ends Bladesong. Use Mage Armor, light armor, or no armor.
Shield: Equipping a shield ends Bladesong. One-handed weapon + free hand only.
Two-handed weapon: Using a two-handed weapon (greatsword, longbow) ends Bladesong. Stick to rapiers, shortswords, or Shadow Blade.
Incapacitated: Being stunned, paralyzed, or unconscious ends Bladesong.

The Shield Spell is NOT a Shield

The Shield reaction spell (+5 AC until your next turn) is perfectly compatible with Bladesong. It is not the same as equipping a physical shield. You should cast Shield on almost every attack that would hit you. With Bladesong + Shield, you hit 25+ AC at level 8.

Extra Attack + Cantrip Combo

Bladesinger's Extra Attack at Wizard 6 is unique among all subclasses in BG3. When you take the Attack action, you attack twice, but one of those attacks can be replaced with casting a cantrip. This is what makes Bladesinger a true melee/caster hybrid.

How It Works

Attack action: Make two attacks. ONE can be replaced by a cantrip.
Best combo: Attack with Shadow Blade (2d8 psychic) + Booming Blade with rapier (weapon damage + 1d8 thunder + 2d8 if target moves).
Why Booming Blade: It includes a melee weapon attack as part of the cantrip, so you effectively make two weapon swings and get bonus thunder damage. If the target tries to chase you or reposition, the extra 2d8 thunder triggers.

Damage Breakdown (Level 6, INT 20)

  • Hit 1 Shadow Blade Attack — 2d8 + 3 (DEX) psychic = 12 avg. Advantage in dim light.
  • Hit 2 Booming Blade (rapier) — 1d8 + 3 (DEX) piercing + 1d8 thunder = 12 avg. If target moves: +2d8 thunder (9 avg).
  • Total 24 avg per round — 33 if Booming Blade rider triggers. Without any spell slot expenditure beyond Shadow Blade upkeep.
With Haste

If you cast Haste on yourself, you get an extra action that allows one weapon attack. That's three attacks per turn: Shadow Blade + Booming Blade + Haste attack. Add the +2 AC from Haste on top of Bladesong AC. The tradeoff is Haste requires concentration instead of Shadow Blade — use a real rapier instead.

Feat Picks

Priority Order

  • 1st War Caster — Advantage on concentration saves. Spell with occupied hands. This is mandatory — your entire build depends on maintaining concentration on Shadow Blade or Haste while fighting in melee.
  • 2nd +2 INT → 20 — Max your primary stat. +5 AC from Bladesong, highest spell DC, best concentration bonus. Everything scales off INT.
  • 3rd Alert — +5 initiative, can't be surprised. Going first means Bladesong is always active before enemies attack. With DEX 16 (+3) and Alert (+5), you have +8 initiative. You go first in almost every fight.
Alternative Feat: Resilient (CON)

If you feel your concentration is already strong enough with War Caster + Bladesong, Resilient (CON) adds proficiency bonus (+4) to all CON saves. This helps against poison, exhaustion, and other CON-based effects beyond just concentration. Take this instead of Alert if you find yourself failing important CON saves.

Feats to Avoid

Great Weapon Master: Bladesong ends if you use two-handed weapons. Incompatible.
Shield Master: You cannot use a shield during Bladesong. Incompatible.
Heavy Armor Master: Heavy armor ends Bladesong. Incompatible.
Dual Wielder: You need a free hand for Bladesong and spellcasting. Incompatible.

31 AC Explained

With all buffs active and Shield cast as a reaction, you reach 31 AC. Most enemies in BG3 have +8 to +12 attack bonus. At 31 AC, an enemy with +12 needs to roll a 19 or 20 to hit you — that's a 10% chance. Against most enemies with +8, only a natural 20 hits. You are effectively immune to weapon attacks. Even without Shield, 26 AC is unhittable by most Act 1 and Act 2 enemies.

AC Limitations

AC does not protect against saving throw effects (Fireball, Hold Person, etc.). This is why Counterspell and high saves matter. AC also does not work against attacks that auto-hit (Magic Missile). Keep Shield ready for most attacks, and Counterspell ready for spells.

Combat Rotation

Standard Combat Flow

  1. Activate Bladesong Bonus Action
    Always activate Bladesong at the start of every combat. +5 AC, +5 concentration, +10 speed. 2 uses per short rest, lasts the whole fight.
  2. Cast Shadow Blade or Haste Turn 1 Action
    If no Shadow Blade is active, cast it (concentration). If you prefer the +2 AC and extra action, cast Haste on yourself and use a physical rapier for melee. Choose one concentration spell and commit.
  3. Extra Attack: Weapon + Booming Blade Every Turn
    Attack with Shadow Blade or rapier, then cast Booming Blade with your second attack. Two melee hits per action. If Hasted, make a third weapon attack with the Haste action.
  4. Shield (reaction) when attacked Every Turn
    If an attack roll would hit you, cast Shield as a reaction for +5 AC until your next turn. At high levels, this makes you effectively untouchable.
  5. Counterspell when enemy casts As Needed
    Watch for enemy spellcasters. Counterspell negates their spell entirely. If you already used Shield this round, prioritize based on threat level.

Burst Combo (Action Surge)

  1. Hold Monster (action 1) Action
    Target the biggest threat. Paralyzed = auto-crit within 3m. WIS save, but your DC is 17+ so most enemies fail.
  2. Action Surge Free
    Use your Fighter 2 Action Surge for a second full action this turn.
  3. Extra Attack on paralyzed target Action 2
    Attack + Booming Blade, both auto-crit on paralyzed target. All damage dice doubled. Shadow Blade crits for 4d8 psychic. Booming Blade crits for extra thunder dice. Devastating single-target burst.

AoE Combo

  1. Wall of Force (action 1) Action
    Dome half the enemies. No save, no escape. Now you fight 3 enemies instead of 6.
  2. Action Surge → Fireball (action 2) Action
    Fireball the group that is NOT inside the Wall of Force dome. 8d6 fire damage to the remaining enemies.
  3. Melee cleanup next turn Following Turns
    With half the enemies dead from Fireball and the other half trapped, walk up and Extra Attack the survivors.
When to Cast vs When to Attack

Cast when: AoE situations (Fireball), control is needed (Hold Monster, Wall of Force), or buffing (Haste on an ally). Attack when: single target, Shadow Blade is active, target is already debuffed/paralyzed, or you need consistent damage. The flexibility to do both is what makes Bladesinger the best Wizard subclass.

Honour Mode Tips

Honour Mode has one save, legendary actions on bosses, and harder enemy AI. The Bladesinger thrives here because high AC prevents the random deaths that end Honour runs, and the full Wizard spell list gives you answers to every encounter.

Key Rules

  • Rule Always have Shield prepared — Your #1 survival tool. Never unprepare it, never forget to cast it.
  • Rule Bladesong before combat starts — If you know a fight is coming, activate Bladesong out of combat. It lasts 1 minute — plenty of time to initiate.
  • Rule Counterspell every dangerous spell — In Honour Mode, one enemy Fireball or Hold Person can end your run. Always save your reaction for Counterspell against casters.
  • Rule Wall of Force splits every hard fight — Fight 6 enemies as two groups of 3. This is the single most impactful strategy in Honour Mode.
  • Rule Mirror Image is free defense — Not concentration. Cast it before Bladesong and Shadow Blade. Three free misses before enemies even touch your AC.
  • Rule Short rest after every fight — Bladesong has 2 uses per short rest. Short resting resets it. Never enter a fight without Bladesong available.
Recommended Party for Honour Mode

You (Bladesinger): Front line / AC tank, control, Counterspell, off-damage, flex caster.
Shadowheart (Life/Tempest Cleric): Primary healer, Spirit Guardians, buff spells, Warding Bond on you.
Astarion (Thief Rogue): Two bonus actions, massive sneak attack burst, lockpicking, utility.
Karlach (Berserker Barbarian): Rage tank, Reckless Attack for damage. She draws aggro, you control the battlefield.

Honour Mode Boss Tip

Legendary actions let bosses break Hold Person and similar effects after one round. Against bosses, use Wall of Force to isolate adds, then focus the boss with melee + Action Surge burst. Do not rely on paralysis lasting multiple rounds — get your crits in the first round and have a backup plan.

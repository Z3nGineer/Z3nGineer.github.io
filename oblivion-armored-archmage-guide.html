Armored Archmage

Breton · The Apprentice · Destruction + Blade + Heavy Armor
Battlemage Heavy Armor Caster Destruction Focus Remastered 2025

Overview

The Armored Archmage is the definitive battlemage fantasy in Oblivion Remastered: full Daedric plate armor, devastating destruction magic from one hand, a glowing enchanted blade in the other. Where the Spellsword is a melee fighter who casts, the Armored Archmage is a mage who refuses to be squishy. You lead with Destruction at range, close with a blade when they survive, and your heavy armor means you never need to run.

The build's genius is in its racial synergy. The Apprentice birthsign gives you +100 Magicka (the largest flat Magicka boost in the game) but inflicts a brutal 100% Weakness to Magic. On any other race, this is suicide. On a Breton, your innate 50% Magic Resistance cancels exactly half of that weakness, leaving you with only a manageable 50% net vulnerability. Then you stack Resist Magic through enchanting and gear until that weakness is completely erased, and you're left with +100 free Magicka and zero drawback.

Why Battlemage, Not Spellsword?

Both are melee-magic hybrids, but the Armored Archmage leans magic-primary. Your Strength and Intelligence are both favored attributes. You carry Alchemy as a Major skill for potion sustain. Conjuration gives you summoned tanks. The Apprentice birthsign's +100 Magicka (vs. The Mage's +50) means you can cast more spells, more often. This is a mage in heavy armor, not a warrior who dabbles in magic.

Race — Breton

  • Passive 50% Magic Resistance — best defensive racial in the game, permanent. Critical for Apprentice synergy.
  • Passive +50 Magicka — permanent bonus to your mana pool. Stacks with Apprentice for +150 total.
  • Daily Dragon Skin — 50% physical damage absorption for 60 seconds. Save for bosses.
  • Skills +10 Conjuration, Mysticism, Restoration · +5 Alchemy, Alteration, Illusion

Class — Custom Battlemage

  • Spec Magic
  • Favored Strength, Intelligence
  • Major Alchemy, Alteration, Blade, Conjuration, Destruction, Heavy Armor, Mysticism
Why These 7 Major Skills?

Alchemy — potion crafting for sustain (Restore Magicka, Resist Magic potions, Fortify Intelligence). Breton gets +5 racial.

Alteration — Shield spells stack with heavy armor for absurd defense. Open Lock saves you from Security. Breton gets +5 racial.

Blade — your melee weapon skill. Power attacks + enchanted sword for when enemies close the gap.

Conjuration — summons tank for you while you cast. Breton gets +10 racial, so you start at Apprentice rank.

Destruction — your primary damage school. Fire, frost, shock at range. The reason you're a mage.

Heavy Armor — full plate protection. Rush to Journeyman (50) for near-zero spell penalty.

Mysticism — Soul Trap for enchanting, Detect Life for awareness, Dispel for enemy buffs. Breton gets +10 racial.

First Steps After the Tutorial

You leave the sewers with +150 Magicka, 50% Magic Resistance, and a 100% Weakness to Magic that you need to manage. Here is the optimal opening sequence:

  1. Buy spells at the Imperial City Market District
    Visit Edgar's Discount Spells for Snowball and basic Destruction spells. Hit Mystic Emporium for Protect (Shield spell) and Soul Trap. Buy a cheap blade and any heavy armor from "A Fighting Chance" if you exited without gear.
  2. Grab a Mortar and Pestle from any alchemist
    Alchemy is a Major skill. Start collecting every ingredient you see. Your first priority potions are Restore Magicka (Steel-Blue Entoloma + Flax Seeds) and Resist Magic (Clouded Funnel Cap + Wisp Stalk Caps). Resist Magic potions are your early-game defense against The Apprentice weakness.
  3. Join the Mages Guild
    Talk to any Mages Guild hall to start the recommendation quest chain. You need all 7 city recommendations to access the Arcane University for spellmaking and enchanting. Start early and do them between other quests. This is your most important questline.
  4. Travel to Skingrad for Destruction spells
    Skingrad Mages Guild is the best spell shop. Buy Flare and Snowball from Druja, Frost Touch from Sulinus Vassinus, and Fireball from Vigge the Cautious. Do Skingrad's recommendation quest while here.
  5. Get Chillrend in Chorrol
    Complete "The Killing Field" quest (talk to Valus Odiil at the Grey Mare inn). Keep both his sons alive. Reward: Chillrend — frost damage blade that scales with your level. Best early weapon for this build.
  6. Raid Vindasel for Umbra (optional, risky)
    Southwest of Imperial City. The NPC Umbra drops a 28-damage sword with built-in Soul Trap and a full Ebony Armor set. You can lure her to Imperial City guards if you cannot fight her. Umbra trivializes soul gem farming for enchanting later.
Manage Your Weakness Early

Until you stack enough Resist Magic to negate The Apprentice weakness, enemy mages are extremely dangerous. Carry Resist Magic potions (Alchemy), use Dragon Skin (daily power) for tough magic-heavy fights, and let your summoned creatures tank caster enemies. Do not facetank mage bosses at low level.

Grind While You Travel

Cast Flare at nothing to level Destruction. Cast Protect on yourself repeatedly to level Alteration. Collect every ingredient along the road and brew potions to level Alchemy. Summon a Skeleton before fights for Conjuration XP. Every step should level something.

The Math Behind Breton + Apprentice

The Apprentice applies 100% Weakness to Magic. This is a separate modifier from Magic Resistance. In Oblivion's formula, Weakness and Resistance are additive: 50% Resist + 100% Weakness = net 50% Weakness to Magic. That means you effectively take 1.5x magic damage early on.

However, every point of additional Resist Magic you stack further reduces this. At just 50 additional Resist Magic (from enchantments, gear, or Alchemy), you reach: 100% Resist - 100% Weakness = 0% net. You take normal magic damage with +100 free Magicka. Stack beyond 50% additional and you become magic-resistant again.

Resist Magic Milestones

0 extra Resist: 50% net weakness (early game — be careful with mages)

+25 Resist: 25% net weakness (mid game — manageable)

+50 Resist: 0% net (neutral — the Apprentice weakness is fully negated)

+85+ Resist: 35%+ net resistance (endgame — you are now magic-resistant with +100 free Magicka)

Leveling — Virtue System

The remaster replaced the old attribute multiplier system. At each level-up you get 12 Virtues to distribute freely. Max 5 per attribute, max 3 attributes per level. Luck costs 4 per point. For the Armored Archmage, you want to push both Strength and Intelligence hard, with Willpower close behind.

IntelligenceMAX FIRSTMax Magicka pool. Every point of INT = 2 Magicka (with Magic spec). You already have +150 from race/birthsign — push this higher.
WillpowerSECONDMagicka regen (quadratic scaling in Remastered). The single most impactful stat for sustain. Push to 80+ as soon as STR and INT are comfortable.
EnduranceSECONDHP per level + HP regen. Retroactive in Remastered, so no rush, but you want this at 60+ for melee durability.
AgilityLATERFatigue pool for power attacks. At 30, you are Fatigue-starved early. Bump to 50 when you can.
SpeedLATERMovement speed. At 30, you are slow in heavy plate. Not urgent but quality of life.
LuckSKIPCosts 4 Virtues per point. Not worth investing.
Suggested Distribution

Levels 2-8: Strength 5, Intelligence 5, Willpower 2

Levels 9-15: Willpower 5, Intelligence 5, Endurance 2

Levels 16-20: Endurance 5, Agility 5, Speed 2

Levels 21+: Fill remaining attributes. Speed, then whatever needs topping off.

Enchanting — Erasing the Apprentice Weakness

Enchanting is the endgame strategy that transforms this build from strong to broken. Your primary goal is stacking Resist Magic on every piece of gear until The Apprentice's 100% Weakness to Magic is completely negated — and then surpassed.

Where to Enchant

Enchanting Altars

Arcane University — Chironasium building. Requires all 7 Mages Guild recommendations.

Frostcrag Spire — personal tower (DLC, included in Remastered). Buy Magetallow Candles from Aurelinwae at Mystic Emporium to activate.

Soul Gem Priority

Use Grand Souls for Resist Magic

Resist Magic enchantments scale dramatically with soul quality. A Petty soul gives ~5% Resist. A Grand soul gives 15-20%. Always use Grand Soul Gems for your Resist Magic enchants on cuirass, helmet, ring, and amulet. Use Umbra's Soul Trap to passively farm Grand Souls from Liches, Xivilai, and Gloom Wraiths.

Combat Guide

The Armored Archmage Loop

  1. Summon your creature before engaging
    Summon a Skeleton/Scamp/Dremora Lord before combat starts. They draw aggro and tank while you cast freely. This is especially important early game when your magic weakness is still active.
  2. Open with Destruction at range
    Fire Lightning Bolt or Fireball as enemies approach. Against mages, always lead with Shock to drain their Magicka. Against melee enemies, use Frost to slow their approach. Get 2-3 casts off before they reach you.
  3. Cast Shield before they close
    Alteration Shield spell (Protect, Aegis, Greater Shield) stacks with your heavy armor. Cast it as enemies close the gap. This makes you nearly unkillable in melee with Daedric armor + Shield spell.
  4. Switch to blade and melee
    Draw Goldbrand or Chillrend and finish weakened enemies with melee. Power attacks deal massive damage to enemies you have already softened with Destruction. Enchanted blade adds fire or frost on top.
  5. Paralyze Touch for tough enemies
    Custom Paralyze Touch (1-2 sec) knocks them flat. Follow immediately with a power attack while they are on the ground. This is your kill combo for anything that survives the initial barrage.
  6. Heal between swings with Alchemy
    Use Restore Health and Restore Magicka potions between melee exchanges. You are an alchemist — you should always have potions. You can also cast Restoration spells if you have them, but potions are faster.
Against Mages (Your Weakness)

Until you stack Resist Magic, enemy mages are dangerous. The counter strategy: 1) Lead with Shock to drain their Magicka. 2) Let your summon tank their spells. 3) Close to melee as fast as possible — mages are squishy in melee. 4) Carry Resist Magic potions (Alchemy) for emergencies. 5) Use Dragon Skin (daily) for the hardest mage fights.

Fatigue Management

Power attacks and sprinting cost Fatigue. In heavy plate with 30 Agility, your Fatigue pool is small. Do not spam power attacks. Use them strategically after Paralyze or when the enemy is staggered. Carry Restore Fatigue potions or cast Minor Respite.

The Endgame Fantasy

At level 25+ with full Daedric plate, 100%+ Resist Magic, +100 Magicka from Apprentice, Goldbrand in one hand and master Destruction in the other: you are an unstoppable arcane juggernaut. Summon a Dremora Lord, cast a custom Shock nuke, close with Goldbrand, and nothing in Cyrodiil can touch you. The "weakness" that defines this build in early game becomes your greatest strength.

Tips & Remastered Mechanics

Willpower Quadratic Regen

In the Remastered version, Magicka regen scales quadratically with Willpower, not linearly. This means the difference between 60 and 80 Willpower is much larger than the difference between 40 and 60. Push Willpower past 80 as soon as your core stats (STR/INT) are comfortable. The regen increase is dramatic and makes sustained casting in heavy armor feel effortless.

Heavy Armor + Conjuration Duration Warning

Wearing heavy armor with low Heavy Armor skill reduces spell effectiveness, which includes Conjuration duration. If your summons disappear too quickly, it is because your spell effectiveness is being penalized by your armor. This is why rushing Heavy Armor to 50 (Journeyman) is non-negotiable. At 50+, the penalty is only 5% — your Dremora Lord stays out nearly as long as it would without armor.

Alchemy is Your Lifeline

As a Major skill, Alchemy levels naturally as you brew. Your three priority potions: Resist Magic (covers your weakness), Restore Magicka (sustain for long dungeon crawls), and Restore Health (combat healing). Pick up every ingredient you find. Brew at every alchemy station you pass. By level 15 your Alchemy should be high enough to make powerful potions.

The Apprentice Weakness is Temporary

The biggest mistake players make with this build is thinking The Apprentice is permanently crippling. It is not. With Breton racial (50% MR), just one enchanted ring or amulet with +15-20% Resist Magic reduces your net weakness to only 30%. By mid-game, the weakness is gone. By endgame, you are magic-resistant. The early game caution is a small price to pay for +100 permanent Magicka.

Sleep to Level Up

You do not level up automatically. When the game says you should rest and meditate, find any bed (inns, your house, dungeon bedrolls) and sleep to trigger the level-up screen and spend your 12 Virtues.

Repair Hammers Are Essential

Weapons and armor degrade with use, and a broken weapon does almost no damage. As a heavy armor user, your repair costs are high. Carry Repair Hammers (buy from any blacksmith) and repair between fights. With Armorer skill at 50+, repairs become very efficient.

Efficient Enchanting Route

1) Use Umbra to passively fill soul gems on every kill. 2) Buy empty Grand Soul Gems from Mages Guild vendors. 3) Hunt Grand-soul creatures (Liches, Xivilai, Gloom Wraiths) to fill them. 4) Enchant Resist Magic on helmet, cuirass, ring, and amulet first. 5) Then Fortify Magicka/Willpower on remaining slots. Each piece that adds Resist Magic makes you noticeably tankier against magic.

Do Not Steal from the Mages Guild

Getting expelled from the Mages Guild locks you out of spell vendors, the Spellmaking Altar, and the Enchanting Altar until you complete a penance quest. For this build, losing enchanting access is devastating. Steal from everyone else — just not the Mages Guild.

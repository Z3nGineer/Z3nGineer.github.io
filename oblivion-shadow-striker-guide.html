Shadow Striker

Wood Elf · The Thief · Marksman + Sneak + Alchemy
Stealth Archer Poison Crafter Assassin Remastered 2025

Overview

The Shadow Striker is a pure stealth predator. One poisoned arrow from the shadows deals 3x sneak attack damage, and with Alchemy-crafted poisons stacking Weakness to Poison + Damage Health, a single shot can exceed 700 damage by mid-game. Switch to a dagger for 6x melee sneak attacks when enemies close the gap. Illusion magic keeps you invisible and resets combat when things go wrong. Nothing survives what it cannot see.

Remastered Changes That Matter

Arrows are always recoverable. In the remaster, arrows can be picked up after combat — no more running out of ammo. Agility governs blade damage for daggers, not Strength, making AGI your primary stat. Fatigue no longer affects damage output, so you can sprint freely without losing DPS. Poison damage is unaffected by difficulty slider.

Race — Wood Elf (Bosmer)

  • Skill +10 Marksman — start with Apprentice-tier archery immediately
  • Skill +10 Sneak — harder to detect from the first dungeon
  • Skill +5 Acrobatics, Alchemy, Alteration, Light Armor — all useful for this build
  • Daily Beast Tongue — Command Creature for 60 seconds. Turns bears, wolves, trolls into allies. Situational but strong in wilderness.
  • Passive Resist Disease 75% — nearly immune to diseases from traps and undead

Class — Custom: Shadow Striker

  • Spec Stealth
  • Favored Agility, Endurance
  • Major Marksman, Sneak, Alchemy, Blade, Light Armor, Illusion, Acrobatics
Why Custom Over Pre-Made?

The pre-made "Assassin" class takes Security and Speechcraft instead of Alchemy and Acrobatics. Security is irrelevant with Open Lock spells, and Speechcraft is niche. Alchemy is the core of this build — your poison damage is your real weapon. Acrobatics lets you reach vantage points and escape melee.

First Steps After the Tutorial

Once you leave the sewers, stealth builds need specific things immediately. Here's the optimal opening route:

  1. Collect every ingredient you see — immediately
    Before going anywhere, start picking up every ingredient on the ground: Flax Seeds, Cairn Bolete, Motherwort, Steel Blue Entoloma. You need Alchemy leveled as fast as possible. Buy a Mortar and Pestle from the first alchemist you find (The Main Ingredient in Imperial City Market District). You can start crafting poisons at Alchemy 15.
  2. Buy a bow and Illusion spells in Imperial City
    Visit A Fighting Chance for a decent bow (Iron or Steel — upgrade when you find better). Then go to Edgar's Discount Spells and buy Starlight (Night-Eye) and any Novice Illusion spells available. Visit Mystic Emporium for an Open Lock spell. Buy arrows in bulk — they're cheap.
  3. Trigger the Dark Brotherhood
    You need to murder an innocent NPC — any non-hostile, non-essential character. Go to a remote house at night, save first, and kill someone while undetected. Sleep in any bed afterward. Lucien Lachance visits you with the Dark Brotherhood invitation. This questline gives you the Blade of Woe and Shrouded Armor — critical gear for this build.
  4. Join the Thieves Guild
    Go to the Imperial City Waterfront at midnight and find the beggars. Talk to Armand Christophe who appears at the garden of Dareloth at midnight. Read one of the wanted posters around the Waterfront to start the quest. The Thieves Guild gives fences to sell stolen goods and eventually the Gray Cowl of Nocturnal.
  5. Find your first real bow — Ruins of Vilverin
    The ruins right outside the sewer exit. Clear it at level 1 for practice and loot. You'll find a decent bow, arrows, and alchemical ingredients inside. Sneak through the entire dungeon — the XP from undetected kills is massive.
Level Sneak While Walking

Toggle sneak on whenever you're near NPCs or enemies. You gain Sneak XP just by moving undetected near living things. Walking through cities in sneak mode at night levels Sneak passively. Cast any Illusion spell you can afford to level that school simultaneously.

Avoid — The Warrior / The Mage

Combat and Magic birthsigns do nothing for a stealth build. The Warrior's +10 STR/END is wasted when Agility is your damage stat. The Mage's +50 Magicka is overkill — you only need enough for Invisibility and Chameleon, and Illusion spells are cheap.

Alchemy & Poison Crafting

This is the heart of the Shadow Striker. Your bow damage is decent. Your poison damage is obscene. Understanding how poison stacking works is the difference between a good build and a broken one.

The Weakness to Poison + Damage Health Stack

How It Works — The Core Mechanic

Oblivion processes poison effects in the order they're listed. If a poison contains both "Weakness to Poison 100%" and "Damage Health 5pts for 10 seconds," the game applies the Weakness first, then the Damage Health is amplified by that weakness. A 100% Weakness doubles the Damage Health. Stack multiple hits and the numbers get absurd.

The Two-Poison Method (Advanced)

  1. Apply Poison #1 — Weakness to Poison
    Craft a poison that contains Weakness to Poison 100% for 10+ seconds. Apply it to your arrow. Hit the enemy. Now they take double damage from the next poison.
  2. Apply Poison #2 — Damage Health
    Immediately switch arrows and apply your strongest Damage Health poison. This second poison now deals double damage because of the active Weakness. A base 30 Damage Health becomes 60. With sneak attack 3x multiplier, that's 180 per tick.
  3. Stack more Weakness for exponential scaling
    Apply Weakness to Poison again before the first wears off. Now the enemy has 200% Weakness (or more). The next Damage Health poison does triple damage. This is how you hit 700+ damage per poisoned arrow at high Alchemy skill.

Poison Damage is NOT Affected by Difficulty

Oblivion's difficulty slider only affects direct weapon and spell damage. Poison damage always deals full damage regardless of difficulty setting. This means on maximum difficulty, where your bow does 1/6th normal damage, your poisons still deal 100%. The Shadow Striker is arguably the strongest build on the hardest difficulty because of this.

Combat Rotation

The Shadow Striker Loop

  1. Approach in stealth — always crouched
    Toggle sneak before entering any room. Use Detect Life to check for enemies through walls. Position yourself at maximum bow range with a clear line of sight. Elevation advantage (balconies, ledges) is ideal.
  2. Apply Weakness to Poison, draw, and fire
    Open your inventory, apply the Weakness to Poison poison to your equipped arrow. Draw the bow fully for maximum damage. Release. The sneak attack multiplier (3x for Marksman) plus the Weakness effect lands simultaneously.
  3. Cast Invisibility immediately after the shot
    The moment your arrow flies, cast Invisibility. The arrow hit alerts enemies, but you vanish before they can locate you. They enter search mode ("must have been the wind") and eventually return to idle.
  4. Reposition while invisible
    Move to a different angle. Enemies search where you were, not where you are. Apply your Damage Health poison to the next arrow while invisible (opening inventory doesn't break Invisibility).
  5. Fire the killing shot — Damage Health poison
    With Weakness to Poison still active on the target, this arrow's poison deals amplified damage. Most enemies die here. If not, repeat the Invisibility cycle.
  6. Emergency melee — dagger sneak attack (6x)
    If an enemy closes to melee range, switch to the Blade of Woe. If you're still hidden (Chameleon stacking), your dagger sneak attack deals 6x damage. One dagger hit with poison often one-shots enemies that survived your arrows.
  7. If detected — Calm spell, then re-stealth
    Cast Calm on the enemy. They stop fighting immediately. Crouch. Within 2-3 seconds you re-enter stealth (Calm resets their AI). Your next attack is another sneak attack. This loop has no counter.
Boss Fight Strategy

Against tough enemies (Daedra, liches, boss NPCs): open with 3 rapid Weakness to Poison arrows to stack the debuff, then switch to your strongest Damage Health poison. The stacked weakness means even boss-tier HP pools melt. On Xivilai and Spider Daedra, this kills them in 2-3 hits total.

Why Sneak Archery is A-Tier in Remastered

In the original Oblivion, arrows could miss and were consumed. In the remaster, arrows always land if aimed correctly and can be recovered from bodies/walls. Combined with the Invisibility reset loop, you get infinite sneak attacks with zero resource loss. The only cost is Magicka for Invisibility, and at 60+ Willpower that regenerates in seconds.

Tips & Remastered Changes

Arrows Are Infinite Now

In Oblivion Remastered, arrows can always be recovered from bodies and surfaces. You never need to buy arrows in bulk. Pick up every arrow after combat and you'll always have enough. This single change makes stealth archery dramatically more viable than in the original game.

Agility Governs Dagger Damage

In the remaster, Agility is the governing attribute for both Marksman (bows) and Blade (daggers). This means your primary stat powers both your ranged and melee weapons simultaneously. Strength is only needed for carry weight. Pump Agility to 100 as fast as possible.

Fatigue Doesn't Affect Damage

Unlike the original game where low Fatigue reduced damage, the remaster decoupled Fatigue from damage output. You can sprint, jump, and power attack without worrying about your Fatigue bar affecting bow or poison damage. Sprint freely to reposition.

Sneak Has a Sound Radius

Moving while sneaking makes noise based on your armor weight and speed. Light Armor is quieter than Heavy Armor. Walking is quieter than running. If enemies keep detecting you, slow down and check your armor. The Shrouded Armor has built-in Fortify Sneak for this reason.

Poison Damage Ignores Difficulty Slider

The difficulty slider scales direct weapon and spell damage only. Poison damage, which is classified as a magical effect from Alchemy, always deals full damage. On max difficulty where your bow does 1/6th damage, your poisons are unaffected. This makes the Shadow Striker the strongest build on the hardest difficulty setting.

Dark Brotherhood = Best Questline for This Build

Every Dark Brotherhood contract is an assassination mission — literally designed for stealth builds. The quests reward you with Blade of Woe, Shrouded Armor, Shadowmere (horse), bonuses to Sneak/Blade, and Gold. Do the entire questline. The "Whodunit?" quest (a murder mystery party where you kill all the guests) is one of the best quests in any Elder Scrolls game.

Enchant Your Bow with Weakness to Poison

At the Arcane University or Frostcrag Spire, enchant your endgame bow with Weakness to Poison on Strike. This means every arrow you fire automatically applies Weakness — no need to manually apply the first poison. Follow up with a Damage Health poison for instant devastating damage.

Steal Everything — Fences Buy It All

The Thieves Guild gives you access to fences who buy stolen goods. Stealth naturally leads to a lot of theft. Every dungeon clear is a payday. Higher-rank fences (Luciana Galena in Bravil, Fathis Ules in IC Elven Gardens) buy items worth up to 1500+ gold. You'll never be poor.

Save Before Every Major Contract

Dark Brotherhood and Thieves Guild quests often have bonus objectives (kill only the target, don't get detected, etc.) that reward extra gold or gear. Save before each quest and reload if you get spotted. Perfectionism is rewarded.

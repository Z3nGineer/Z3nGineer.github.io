Light Cleric — Radiating Juggernaut

Gold Dwarf / Half-Elf · Light Domain · Cleric 12 · #10 Ranked
Healer AoE Damage Full Caster Heavy Armor Patch 8

Overview

Light Cleric is the best beginner class in Baldur's Gate 3, and one of the strongest builds at every stage of the game. You get heavy armor, a shield, full spellcasting, the entire healing kit, AND the best blaster spells in the game via domain spells — Fireball, Scorching Ray, and Wall of Fire on a Cleric chassis. No other class does everything this well.

Spirit Guardians is the single strongest sustained-damage spell in BG3. Cast it, walk into a group of enemies, and watch them melt. Add Spiritual Weapon for free bonus-action damage every turn, Warding Flare to make enemies miss, and Radiance of the Dawn for burst AoE — and you have a frontline juggernaut that also happens to be the best healer in the party.

Why Light Domain?

Light Domain gives Clerics something no other subclass can match: Fireball and Wall of Fire as domain spells (always prepared, free). Combined with the Cleric's base kit of Spirit Guardians + Spiritual Weapon + healing, you become a full damage dealer AND full healer simultaneously. Life Domain heals more but deals no damage. Tempest Domain has a nuke but no sustained AoE. Light does both.

Best Solo Class in BG3

If you could only pick one class for every encounter in the game — boss fights, trash mobs, traps, dialogues, honour mode — it would be Light Cleric. Heavy armor + shield means you don't die. Healing Word picks up allies. Spirit Guardians + Fireball kills everything. Guidance dominates dialogues. It has no bad levels and no bad matchups.

Race & Ability Scores

Primary Pick — Gold Dwarf

  • Racial Dwarven Toughness — +1 HP per level. 12 extra HP by endgame. You're a frontliner, this matters.
  • Racial Dwarven Resilience — Advantage on saving throws against poison, resistance to poison damage.
  • Racial Darkvision — 12m range. Operate in darkness without torches.
  • Racial No STR Requirement — Dwarves ignore heavy armor STR requirements. Dump STR freely.

Alternative — Half-Elf (High)

  • Racial Fey Ancestry — Advantage on saves vs Charm. Common in Act 2-3.
  • Racial Darkvision — 12m range.
  • Racial Extra Cantrip — High Half-Elf gets a Wizard cantrip. Take Fire Bolt for ranged damage.
  • Note You'll need 14 STR for heavy armor (or settle for medium). Gold Dwarf is strictly better mechanically.

Warding Flare (Level 1)

As a reaction, when a creature you can see within 9m attacks you or an ally, impose disadvantage on the attack roll. Uses equal to your WIS modifier per long rest (3-5 uses). This is essentially free Shield-like protection for the party.

Improved Warding Flare (Level 6)

Warding Flare can now protect any ally within 9m, not just yourself. This turns you into a reactive defender for the whole party.

Radiance of the Dawn (Channel Divinity)

9m radius AoE burst: 2d10 + Cleric level radiant damage (CON save for half). Also dispels magical darkness. Recharges on short rest. Use this as your burst AoE when Fireball would be overkill or enemies resist fire.

Radiant vs Fire

Many enemies in BG3 resist fire. Almost nothing resists radiant. Radiance of the Dawn and Spirit Guardians deal radiant damage, making them reliable in every encounter. Fireball is for when you need raw damage and fire resistance isn't an issue.

Channel Divinity Uses

  • Level 2 1 use per short rest — Radiance of the Dawn OR Turn Undead
  • Level 6 2 uses per short rest — use both in a big fight, short rest to recharge

Combat Guide

Standard Rotation

  1. Pre-buff Before Combat
    Heroes' Feast before boss fights. Death Ward on your squishiest ally. Aid with leftover slots from previous rest. Longstrider on everyone if available.
  2. Turn 1: Spirit Guardians + Spiritual Weapon Turn 1
    Action: cast Spirit Guardians. Bonus action: cast Spiritual Weapon. You now have persistent AoE damage and a free bonus-action attack every turn. This is your setup — every fight starts this way.
  3. Turn 2: Move into enemies Turn 2
    Walk directly into the largest group of enemies. Spirit Guardians procs when they start their turn in the radius OR when they first enter it. The more enemies in your aura, the more damage you do. Bonus action: Spiritual Weapon attacks.
  4. Maintain & damage Every Turn
    Stay in the middle of enemies. Spirit Guardians does the work. Use your action for: Toll the Dead (free damage cantrip), Healing Word (pick up downed ally as bonus action), or Fireball (if a distant group needs nuking). Spiritual Weapon attacks every turn as bonus action.
  5. Radiance of the Dawn for burst When Needed
    When surrounded by many enemies, use Radiance of the Dawn for an AoE burst on top of Spirit Guardians. This stacks — enemies take Guardians damage at their turn start AND Radiance damage on yours.
  6. Emergency healing Reactive
    Healing Word (bonus action, 18m range) to pick up downed allies without breaking your action economy. Mass Cure Wounds or Heal for emergencies. Never drop Spirit Guardians to heal — Healing Word is a bonus action for a reason.
The Spirit Guardians Rule

Spirit Guardians is your #1 damage source. Never drop concentration voluntarily. Don't cast Bless, don't cast Hold Person, don't cast anything with concentration while Spirit Guardians is up. Your action should be cantrips, non-concentration spells (Fireball, Healing Word), or the Dodge action to protect your concentration. The damage from maintaining Guardians almost always outweighs any other spell you could cast.

Warding Flare Timing

Save Warding Flare for attacks against YOU specifically — you're the one maintaining Spirit Guardians, so you're the one who can't afford to take big hits. After level 6 (Improved Warding Flare), start using it to protect squishy allies who are about to take lethal damage.

When to Cast Fireball Instead

  • Scenario Distant cluster — enemies grouped 18m+ away that you can't reach with Guardians this turn
  • Scenario Opening surprise — enemies haven't spotted you yet — Fireball opener then Spirit Guardians next turn
  • Scenario Explosive barrels — fire + barrels = environmental nuke. Fireball detonates everything in range
  • Scenario Cleanup — 3-4 low-HP enemies grouped together. Fireball ends the fight, Guardians would take 2+ rounds

Honour Mode Tips

Honour Mode Warning

One save. No reloads. If your party wipes, the run is over. Light Cleric is one of the safest Honour Mode picks because you can heal, tank, and deal damage simultaneously. Here's how to maximize survivability.

Key Strategies

  1. Always have Death Ward active
    Before any boss fight or dangerous encounter, cast Death Ward on yourself. You're the healer — if you die, the party follows. Death Ward prevents one lethal hit.
  2. Heroes' Feast every boss
    The fear immunity alone is worth the 6th-level slot. Many Honour Mode bosses have fear effects that disable your entire party. Heroes' Feast prevents it.
  3. War Caster + Resilient CON are mandatory
    In Honour Mode, enemies hit harder. Every concentration check is life or death. Take both feats. No exceptions. WIS cap is nice but surviving is better.
  4. Warding Flare on the killing blow
    Save Warding Flare for attacks that would down or kill a party member. Don't waste it on chip damage. One clutch Flare can save a run.
  5. Radiance of the Dawn dispels darkness
    Many Honour Mode encounters add magical darkness. Your Channel Divinity removes it AND deals damage. Don't forget this — it can turn a losing fight around.
  6. Keep a Revivify scroll as backup
    If you go down, no one else can cast Revivify. Buy scrolls of Revivify and give them to Gale or another party member. Redundancy saves runs.
Honour Mode Boss Order

Act 1: Spirit Guardians trivializes Goblin leaders. Use Turn Undead on Withers' crypt skeletons. Fireball the Grymforge encounters.
Act 2: Turn Undead + Radiance of the Dawn makes the Shadow Cursed Lands easy. Spirit Guardians for Ketheric.
Act 3: Heroes' Feast before every boss. Death Ward on yourself. Viconia's Walking Fortress shield makes you nearly untouchable. Spirit Guardians + Fireball clears the Brain fight.

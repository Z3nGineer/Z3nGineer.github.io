Holy Crusader

Breton · The Lady · Blunt + Blade + Restoration + Heavy Armor
Paladin Crusader Tank Undead Slayer Remastered 2025

Overview

The Holy Crusader is Oblivion's ultimate warrior-priest. A Breton paladin clad in gleaming heavy armor, wielding divine Restoration magic and bone-crushing blunt weapons to purge the undead from Cyrodiil. Turn Undead trivializes roughly 40% of the game's dungeon content — every vampire lair, zombie-infested ruin, skeleton crypt, lich fortress, and ghost haunt becomes a cakewalk when your enemies flee in terror at the sight of your outstretched hand.

This isn't a glass cannon. With 50% innate magic resistance, heavy armor, Shield spells, and on-demand healing, the Holy Crusader stands in the thick of combat where other builds crumble. Blunt Expert's Silence power attack shuts down enemy mages. Block Expert's Shield Bash staggers melee fighters. And when the Knights of the Nine DLC gear comes online, you become an unstoppable divine engine of destruction.

Why This Build Dominates

Turn Undead is the most underrated spell school in the game. Vampires, zombies, liches, skeleton champions, ghosts, wraiths — all of them flee from a single spell. While they run, you heal, reposition, and pick them off one by one. In a game where roughly 40% of enemies are undead, this alone makes the build elite.

Race — Breton

  • Passive 50% Magic Resistance — best defensive racial in the game, permanent. Enemy spells do half damage.
  • Passive +50 Magicka — permanent bonus to mana pool. Lets you cast more Restoration spells per fight.
  • Daily Dragon Skin — 50% physical damage shield for 60 seconds. Emergency button for tough fights.
  • Skills +10 Conjuration, Mysticism, Restoration · +5 Alchemy, Alteration, Illusion
Why Breton Over Imperial or Redguard?

Imperial gives +10 Blade and a Charm power, but no magic resistance. Redguard has Adrenaline Rush but negative Magicka — terrible for a caster hybrid. The Breton's 50% MR + 50 bonus Magicka is unmatched for a paladin who needs to cast Restoration spells constantly while tanking hits in heavy armor.

Class — Custom Crusader

  • Spec Combat — favors melee skill leveling speed
  • Favored Strength, Willpower
  • Major Blunt, Blade, Block, Heavy Armor, Restoration, Alteration, Athletics
Why Both Blade AND Blunt?

Blunt Expert (skill 75) gives Silence on power attacks — shuts down mages completely. Blade gives you access to silver weapons for ghosts/wraiths (which resist blunt) and the Crusader's Sword. Having both as major skills means total weapon flexibility — pull out whatever the situation demands.

First Steps After the Tutorial

The moment you leave the sewers, you have one mission: become a walking chapel. Here's exactly what to do:

  1. Buy Turn Undead immediately
    Go to the Imperial City Temple District. Visit any Great Chapel (Chapel of Akatosh, Talos, etc.). Chapel healers sell Turn Undead (Restoration, Novice-rank). This single spell transforms 40% of the game's dungeons from dangerous to trivial. Also buy Heal Minor Wounds if you don't already have it.
  2. Get starting Heavy Armor and a Blunt weapon
    Visit "A Fighting Chance" in the Imperial City Market District. Buy an iron or steel mace and any heavy armor you can afford. A mace + shield is your bread and butter — block, bash, smash. Also grab a silver shortsword for ghosts.
  3. Clear undead dungeons for easy XP and loot
    With Turn Undead, you can safely clear undead-heavy dungeons that would be suicide for other builds at level 1. Vilverin (right outside the sewers exit) has undead. Ayleid ruins along the road are full of zombies and skeletons. Cast Turn Undead, watch them flee, pick them off one by one. Free loot, free skill XP.
  4. Visit Chorrol for the Escutcheon
    Travel to Chorrol and do the quest "Sins of the Father" (talk to Countess Arriana Valga in Castle Chorrol). Reward: Escutcheon of Chorrol — a heavy shield with Reflect Damage. Your shield until Crusader's Relics.
  5. Start Knights of the Nine questline (level 1+)
    Visit any Chapel and pray at the altar, or look for the "Prophet" NPC wandering the Imperial City. This starts the Knights of the Nine DLC questline, which rewards the Crusader's Relics — a full armor set + mace + sword + shield, all with holy enchantments. You can start this at any level. The sooner, the better.
Spam Restoration While Traveling

While walking between cities, constantly cast Heal Minor Wounds on yourself to level Restoration. Even at full health, the spell still gives skill XP. In the remaster, higher-cost spells give more XP, so upgrade to Heal Major Wounds as soon as you can afford it. Every step you take should be accompanied by a cast.

Leveling — Virtue System

The remaster replaced the old attribute multiplier system. At each level-up you get 12 Virtues to distribute freely. Max 5 per attribute, max 3 attributes per level. Luck costs 4 per point. The Holy Crusader needs BOTH physical stats (Strength for melee damage) AND Willpower (for magicka regen to fuel Restoration). This dual dependency makes Virtue allocation critical.

Suggested Distribution

Levels 2–8: Strength 5, Willpower 5, Endurance 2

Levels 9–15: Strength 5, Willpower 5, Intelligence 2

Levels 16+: Fill Endurance, then Speed, then Agility. Top off whatever needs it.

Willpower Quadratic Regen — Why It Matters

In the remaster, magicka regen scales quadratically with Willpower — meaning the jump from 70 to 80 WIL gives FAR more regen than 40 to 50. For a paladin who casts Turn Undead, Shield, and heals every single fight, this is the difference between running dry mid-dungeon and being self-sustaining forever. Push Willpower aggressively.

Combat Mechanics

The Holy Crusader has a specific answer for every enemy type. Understanding the Expert-rank perks for Blunt and Block is what elevates this build from "good" to "broken."

Blunt Expert — Silence on Power Attacks

Mage Killer

At Blunt skill 75 (Expert), your power attacks Silence the target for several seconds. Silenced enemies cannot cast any spells — no healing, no damage, no summons. Combined with your innate 50% Magic Resistance, enemy mages go from being the most dangerous threat in the game to being helpless sacks of HP. One power attack and they're muted. Follow up with normal swings while they flail uselessly.

Block Expert — Shield Bash

Melee Dominance

At Block skill 75 (Expert), you can Shield Bash by attacking while blocking. This staggers the enemy, interrupting their attack and leaving them open for a counter-attack. Against melee enemies, the loop is: block their swing, Shield Bash to stagger, power attack while staggered, repeat. They never get a clean hit on you. In the remaster, Shield Bash is significantly stronger than in the original game.

Weapon Swap Strategy

Keep a blunt weapon as your main hand for the Silence perk. Swap to a silver blade when you encounter ghosts or wraiths (they resist blunt). The hotkey system lets you bind weapons to number keys — set Mace to 1 and Silver Sword to 2 for instant swaps mid-combat.

Gear by Level

Spell Effectiveness in Armor

Wearing armor reduces spell power. At Heavy Armor Novice (0-24), you lose ~18%. At Journeyman (50+), you only lose 5% — totally worth it. Rush Heavy Armor to 50 so your heals and Turn Undead fire at near full strength.

Crusader's Relics — Knights of the Nine DLC

The Knights of the Nine questline is the defining quest for this build. You must complete the Pilgrimage of the Nine Divines (visit all 9 wayshrines scattered across Cyrodiil), then recover each Relic from a separate dungeon. Combat Loop

Against Undead (~40% of encounters)

  1. Cast Turn Undead
    Open with Turn Undead. All affected undead flee in terror for 30 seconds. If there's a pack, this scatters them — you fight one at a time instead of being swarmed.
  2. Switch weapon if needed
    Ghosts/wraiths? Silver sword. Skeletons/zombies? Mace or Crusader's Mace. The enemy is running — you have time to hotkey swap.
  3. Chase and power attack
    Run after the fleeing undead and deliver power attacks while their backs are turned. They can't fight back. Free damage.
  4. Heal and repeat
    Cast Heal Major Wounds or Absorb Health between kills. Re-cast Turn Undead if the duration expires. Move to the next target.

Against Mages

  1. Close distance quickly
    Your 50% MR absorbs their spells as you approach. Cast an Alteration Shield buff while running in.
  2. Blunt power attack — Silence
    One power attack with any blunt weapon at Expert rank Silences the mage. They can't cast for several seconds. This is your kill window.
  3. Follow up with normal attacks
    Mages have low HP and no armor. Two or three normal mace swings while they're Silenced finishes the fight.

Against Melee Fighters

  1. Block their opening attack
    Raise your shield as they swing. Your heavy armor + Shield spell absorbs the hit.
  2. Shield Bash to stagger
    At Block Expert (75), Shield Bash interrupts and staggers the enemy. They stumble back, unable to act for a moment.
  3. Power attack during stagger
    Immediately follow the Bash with a blunt power attack. The Silence effect doesn't matter against non-casters, but the raw damage is enormous.
  4. Heal — Cast Restoration between exchanges
    Between their swing attempts, quickly cast Heal Major Wounds or Absorb Health Touch. You out-sustain any melee enemy.
  5. Alteration Shield buff if HP drops low
    If you're taking too much damage, cast an Alteration Shield spell for flat damage reduction that stacks with your armor. With both active, you're nearly invincible.
Dragon Skin for Emergencies

Your Breton daily power — 50% physical damage absorption for 60 seconds. Save this for boss fights, Oblivion Gate sigil stone runs, or when you're swarmed by 4+ enemies. Combined with heavy armor + Shield spell + Dragon Skin, you take almost zero physical damage for a full minute.

Fatigue Management

Power attacks and sprinting cost Fatigue. If Fatigue is depleted, you can be staggered through your block. Keep Minor Respite (Restore Fatigue) on a hotkey. Don't spam power attacks — use them after Shield Bash or Turn Undead when the enemy can't retaliate.

Remastered Tips

Willpower Quadratic Regen is Essential

This cannot be overstated. In the original Oblivion, magicka regen was linear. In the remaster, it's quadratic. A paladin with 100 Willpower regenerates magicka DRAMATICALLY faster than one with 60. This is the difference between "I need to rest after every fight" and "I can clear an entire dungeon without stopping." Prioritize Willpower in your Virtue allocation.

Block Expert Shield Bash is Stronger in the Remaster

Shield Bash was mediocre in the original game. In the remaster, it's been buffed significantly — longer stagger duration, more reliable interrupts, and it works against power attacks. This makes Block one of the best combat skills in the game, and it's a major skill for this build. Use it constantly.

Endurance HP is Retroactive

In the original game, HP gained per level was based on your Endurance AT THAT LEVEL — if you didn't max END early, you permanently lost HP. In the remaster, Endurance HP is fully retroactive. You get the HP you "should have" no matter when you raise END. This means you can safely prioritize Strength and Willpower early without losing max HP.

Blade + Blunt Both as Major = Total Flexibility

Most builds lock into one weapon type. By having both Blade and Blunt as major skills, you can pull out whatever the situation demands. Mace for Silence on mages. Silver sword for ghosts. Crusader's Sword for fire damage. Crusader's Mace for Turn Undead on hit. You're never stuck with the wrong weapon.

Sleep to Level Up

Unlike most RPGs, you don't level up automatically. When the game says "you should rest and meditate on what you've learned," you need to sleep in any bed to trigger the level-up screen and spend your Virtues. Any bed works — inns, your house, bedrolls in dungeons.

Knights of the Nine — Infamy Requirement

The Knights of the Nine questline requires 0 infamy to start. If you've committed crimes (theft, murder, assault), your infamy may be above 0. The Pilgrimage of the Nine Divines (visiting all 9 wayshrines) resets your infamy to 0. But if you gain infamy AFTER completing the questline, the Crusader's Relics will be unequippable until you do another Pilgrimage. Play as a righteous paladin — it's not just roleplay, it's mechanically enforced.

Repair Your Gear

Weapons and armor degrade with use. Carry Repair Hammers (buy from any blacksmith) and repair between fights. A broken mace does almost no damage, and broken armor provides almost no AR. Stock up — they're cheap and weightless.

Alchemy for Free Potions

Even without investing skill points, Alchemy lets you make Restore Health and Restore Magicka potions from common ingredients. Pick up every ingredient you find. Buy a Mortar and Pestle from any alchemist. Free potions supplement your Restoration magic for long dungeon crawls.

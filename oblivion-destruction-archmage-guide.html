Destruction Archmage

Breton · The Mage · Pure Mage · All 7 Magic Schools
Pure Caster Spellcrafting Archmage Remastered 2025

Overview

The Destruction Archmage is the most powerful late-game build in Oblivion Remastered. No weapons. No armor. Just raw magical supremacy. A Breton with 50% innate magic resistance and +50 Magicka, stacking The Mage birthsign for another +50, starts the game with 250+ Magicka and access to every school of magic. Your weapon is the Spellmaking Altar at the Arcane University, where you craft custom spells that exploit Weakness stacking to deal exponentially scaling damage that no enemy in the game can survive.

Why Pure Mage Works in the Remaster

Willpower-based regen is quadratic. In the original game, mages ran dry constantly. The remaster ties magicka regeneration to Willpower with exponential scaling — at 100 Willpower, your regen is fast enough to sustain continuous casting. Combined with no spell effectiveness penalty (you wear robes, not armor), every spell hits at 100% power from level 1.

Race — Breton

  • Passive 50% Magic Resistance — best defensive racial in the game, permanent. Enemy mages barely scratch you.
  • Passive +50 Magicka — permanent bonus to your mana pool, stacks with birthsign and Intelligence
  • Daily Dragon Skin — 50% physical damage absorption for 60 seconds. Your emergency button when enemies close the gap.
  • Skills +10 Conjuration, Mysticism, Restoration · +5 Alchemy, Alteration, Illusion
Why Not High Elf?

High Elves get +100 Magicka (vs Breton's +50) but have Weakness to Fire, Frost, and Shock — the three main damage types enemies use. As a pure mage with zero armor, that weakness is lethal. Breton's 50% Magic Resistance is far more valuable for survival, and the Magicka difference is easily closed with Intelligence investment and enchanted gear.

Class — Custom "Archmage"

  • Spec Magic
  • Favored Intelligence, Willpower
  • Major 1 Destruction — your primary damage school
  • Major 2 Conjuration — summoned creatures tank for you
  • Major 3 Restoration — healing + Fortify attribute buffs
  • Major 4 Alteration — Shield spells + Paralyze + Open Lock
  • Major 5 Mysticism — Soul Trap + Absorb + Reflect + Detect Life
  • Major 6 Illusion — Invisibility + Chameleon + Charm + Silence
  • Major 7 Alchemy — Restore Magicka potions for infinite sustain
Level Scaling Warning

With all 7 major skills being magic schools that you cast constantly, you will level up fast. Enemies scale with your level. The upside: the Virtue system in the remaster makes this far less punishing. The downside: you need to keep your Destruction and combat spells upgraded to match enemy HP scaling. Don't neglect Spellcrafting once you unlock it.

First Steps After the Tutorial

As a pure mage, your first priority is unlocking the Spellmaking Altar at the Arcane University. Everything before that is preparation. Here is the exact path:

  1. Join the Mages Guild IMMEDIATELY
    Leave the sewers and go directly to any Mages Guild hall. You need recommendations from all 7 city chapters to access the Arcane University. This is your single most important questline. Start it before anything else. Talk to the chapter head in any city to begin.
  2. Buy starter spells from every city you visit
    Each Mages Guild chapter has spell vendors with unique inventories. Buy every spell you can afford — the more spells you know, the more options you have at the Spellmaking Altar later. Priority: Flare (Skingrad, Druja), Snowball (Skingrad, Druja), Heal Minor Wounds (free), Protect (IC, Mystic Emporium), Summon Skeleton (IC, Mystic Emporium).
  3. Skingrad — your main spell shop
    Skingrad Mages Guild has the best spell selection in the game. Druja sells novice/apprentice Destruction and Restoration. Vigge the Cautious sells journeyman+ Destruction. Sulinus Vassinus sells Frost Touch and Alteration spells. Buy everything. Do the Skingrad recommendation quest while here.
  4. Bravil — Illusion and Mysticism spells
    Ita Rienus sells Chameleon and Invisibility. Delphine Jend sells Charm and Mysticism spells. Both essential schools for a pure mage. Do the Bravil recommendation quest.
  5. Cheydinhal — Conjuration and Alteration
    Trayvond the Redguard sells Conjuration and Alteration spells. Get Summon Scamp and Open Easy Lock here. You need a summoned creature between you and enemies at all times early on.
  6. Complete all 7 recommendations — unlock Arcane University
    Anvil, Bravil, Bruma, Cheydinhal, Chorrol, Leyawiin, Skingrad. Each recommendation quest is short (30-60 min). Once all 7 are done, you gain access to the Arcane University grounds in the Imperial City — specifically the Praxographical Center where the Spellmaking Altar lives. This is where your build goes from "competent mage" to "unstoppable archmage."
  7. Continue the Mages Guild questline toward Arch-Mage
    After gaining University access, the Mages Guild questline continues. Complete it to eventually become Arch-Mage and receive the Arch-Mage's Robe — the best mage armor in the game. The questline also deepens your connection to the University resources.
Level Destruction While Traveling

Between cities, spam Flare at nothing — trees, rocks, the ground. In the remaster, higher-cost spells give more XP per cast, so use your most expensive Destruction spell when grinding. Also cast Heal Minor Wounds constantly (Restoration XP) and Protect before every encounter (Alteration XP). Your skills level just by using them.

Frostcrag Spire Shortcut

If you have the Frostcrag Spire DLC (included in the Remastered edition), buy Magetallow Candles from Aurelinwae at the Mystic Emporium in the Imperial City Market District. Bring them to Frostcrag Spire to activate its Spellmaking Altar — no Mages Guild recommendations needed. You still want the Guild questline for the Arch-Mage's Robe, but this gives you early Spellcrafting access.

Leveling — Virtue System

The remaster replaced the old attribute multiplier system with Virtues. At each level-up you get 12 Virtues to distribute freely. Max 5 per attribute, max 3 attributes per level. Luck costs 4 per point. As a pure mage, your priority is clear: Intelligence and Willpower above everything.

AttributePriorityWhy
IntelligenceMAX FIRSTEvery point of INT = 2 more Magicka. At 100 INT, you have a massive spell pool. This is your primary resource — max it ASAP.
WillpowerMAX FIRSTMagicka regen scales quadratically with Willpower. At 50 WIL, regen is mediocre. At 100 WIL, it's 4x faster. This is the difference between running dry in every fight and never stopping.
EnduranceSECONDHealth pool. You have zero armor — you WILL get hit. Endurance HP is retroactive in the remaster, so you don't lose anything by raising it after INT/WIL.
SpeedLATERMovement speed = kiting ability. As a pure mage, distance is your armor. At 30 starting SPD, you're painfully slow — invest after your core attributes are solid.
AgilityLATERFatigue pool. Affects spell cost slightly. Low priority for a pure caster but eventually worth raising.
StrengthSKIPCarry weight only. You don't use melee weapons. Carry potions and soul gems, nothing heavy. Skip unless you're constantly over-encumbered.
LuckSKIPCosts 4 Virtues per point. Marginal benefit. Not worth the investment for a focused mage build.
Suggested Distribution

Levels 2-8: Intelligence 5, Willpower 5, Endurance 2

Levels 9-15: Intelligence 5 (if not maxed), Willpower 5 (if not maxed), Endurance 2

Levels 16+: Endurance 5, Speed 5, Agility 2 — fill remaining gaps.

Willpower Quadratic Regen Explained

Magicka regen in the remaster uses a quadratic formula tied to Willpower. Roughly: regen per second = (Willpower/100)^2 * base_rate. At 50 Willpower, you regenerate at 25% efficiency. At 75, you're at 56%. At 100, you hit full 100% regen speed. The difference between 60 and 100 Willpower is nearly triple your regeneration rate. This is why Willpower is equally important as Intelligence — a bigger pool means nothing if it takes forever to refill.

Spellcrafting — Your Real Weapon

Spellcrafting at the Spellmaking Altar is the single most powerful mechanic in Oblivion, and this build is designed to exploit it completely. Every spell you know unlocks its effects as components for custom spells. The more spells you buy from vendors, the more building blocks you have.

How Spellcrafting Works

  1. Go to a Spellmaking Altar
    Arcane University (Praxographical Center) or Frostcrag Spire. Activate the altar to open the spell creation interface.
  2. Choose effects from spells you already know
    Every spell effect you've learned from vendor spells appears as an option. If you want to make a Weakness to Fire spell, you need to already know a spell with that effect. This is why buying many spells from vendors matters — each one unlocks new crafting options.
  3. Set magnitude, duration, and range
    Higher magnitude = stronger effect but costs more Magicka. Longer duration = cheaper per second. Touch range is cheaper than Target (projectile). You can combine multiple effects into one spell — up to 8 effects per spell.
  4. Name it and pay the gold cost
    Custom spells cost gold to create (based on complexity). Name your spell something memorable — you'll be casting it hundreds of times.

The Weakness Stack — Exponential Damage

This is the core mechanic that makes the Destruction Archmage the most powerful build in the game. Here is how it works:

The Weakness to Element Mechanic

Weakness to Fire 100% means the target takes double fire damage. But here is the trick: Weakness also amplifies OTHER Weakness effects of the same type. So casting Weakness to Fire twice doesn't just add — it multiplies.

Example: Single Weakness Stack

StepSpell CastResult
1Weakness to Fire 100% (10 sec)Target now takes 2x fire damage
2Fire Damage 50 ptsHits for 50 x 2.0 = 100 damage

Example: Double Weakness Stack (Exponential)

StepSpell CastResult
1Weakness to Fire 100% (10 sec)Target takes 2x fire damage (including future Weakness spells)
2Weakness to Fire 100% (10 sec)Applied at 2x strength = 200% Weakness. Total: target takes 4x fire damage
3Fire Damage 50 ptsHits for 50 x 4.0 = 200 damage

Example: Triple Weakness Stack (Overkill)

StepSpell CastResult
1Weakness to Fire 100% (10 sec)2x fire damage
2Weakness to Fire 100% (10 sec)Applied at 2x = 200%. Total: 4x fire damage
3Weakness to Fire 100% (10 sec)Applied at 4x = 400%. Total: 8x fire damage
4Fire Damage 80 ptsHits for 80 x 8.0 = 640 damage
Pro Tip — Combine into Fewer Spells

Instead of casting Weakness and Damage as separate spells, craft a single spell with both effects: "Weakness to Fire 100% for 3 sec + Fire Damage 50 pts" in one cast. The Weakness applies first within the same spell, so the Fire Damage is already amplified. Cast this spell twice on the same target and the second cast benefits from the Weakness already on them — exponential damage in just two button presses.

Gear

As a pure mage, you wear robes and enchanted jewelry. No armor — it reduces spell effectiveness. Your gear is about maximizing Magicka, Willpower, and magical defense.

No Armor Penalty = 100% Spell Power

Wearing any armor piece reduces your spell effectiveness. Even one piece of light armor causes a penalty. As a pure mage, you wear only robes, hoods, and enchanted clothing — every spell hits at full power. This is your tradeoff: zero physical defense, maximum magical offense.

Robe: Arch-Mage's Robe (quest reward, can't be beaten)

Use Azura's Star + Soul Trap to fill and refill endlessly. Enchant your best unenchanted clothing and jewelry with Grand Souls for maximum effect magnitudes.

Combat Guide

The Weakness Stack Rotation

  1. Summon your tank
    Before engaging, summon a Clannfear (or Dremora Lord late game). It charges into melee and absorbs all enemy aggro. You stay at range. Always have a summon active — this is your "armor."
  2. Pre-cast Shield and buffs
    Cast your custom Mage Armor spell (Shield 25% + Resist Magic 20%). If fighting a boss, cast Fortify Intelligence/Willpower buff for a larger Magicka pool. These are cheap and last 30+ seconds.
  3. Cast Weakness to Fire 100% (first application)
    Your combo spell (Weakness to Fire 100% for 10 sec + Fire Damage 30). The Weakness debuff applies, the Fire Damage hits for base value. Target is now taking 2x fire damage from all sources.
  4. Cast the same spell again (exponential damage)
    Second cast: the Weakness effect is amplified by the existing debuff (now 200% Weakness total = 4x damage). The Fire Damage component hits for 4x base value. Two casts of a 45-Magicka spell deal more damage than any vendor nuke in the game.
  5. Finish with your big nuke if still alive
    If the target survived two Weakness stacks, hit them with Archmage's Wrath (Fire Damage 80). At 4x amplification, this deals 320 damage. Nothing in the game survives this rotation except Mehrunes Dagon himself.

AoE for Groups

Crowd Control Strategy

Step 1: Summon your creature into the center of the enemy group — it draws all aggro.

Step 2: Cast your AoE fire spell (Crowd Burner: Fire Damage 30 in 15ft). Hits all enemies.

Step 3: If enemies peel toward you, cast Paralyze Touch on the closest one, then Invisibility to reset.

Step 4: Re-summon if your creature died. Repeat AoE. Two rounds typically clears any group.

Fighting Enemy Mages

You Counter Mages

As a Breton with 50% Magic Resistance + Arch-Mage's Robe Spell Absorption + enchanted Resist Magic gear, enemy mages barely scratch you. Hit them with Shock damage (drains their Magicka) or Silence (prevents casting entirely). Once their Magicka is empty, they switch to weak melee attacks that your summon handles. You are the ultimate anti-mage.

Emergency Survival

When Things Go Wrong

Overwhelmed by melee enemies: Cast Invisibility → run → re-summon → re-engage from range.

Magicka empty: Pop a Restore Magicka potion (always carry 10+) or use a staff (staves have their own charges).

About to die: Activate Dragon Skin (daily Breton power, 50% physical absorption for 60 sec) → cast Emergency Heal → Invisibility → escape and recover.

Multiple strong enemies: Cast Paralyze Touch on the strongest, kill the weaker ones first, then focus the paralyzed target when it wears off.

Tips and Tricks

Magic XP Scales with Spell Cost

In the remaster, the XP you gain for each skill is proportional to the Magicka cost of the spell you cast. Spamming Flare (11 Magicka) gives much less XP per cast than using Lightning Bolt (61 Magicka). Once you can afford it, always use your most expensive spell to grind skill XP. This means your custom spells (which tend to be expensive multi-effect combos) level your skills faster than cheap vendor spells.

Willpower Regen is Everything

Unlike the original game where mages relied on potions and waiting, the remaster's Willpower-based regen means you regenerate Magicka continuously in and out of combat. At 100 Willpower, you regenerate fast enough to cast a Flare-tier spell every 2-3 seconds even during combat. This fundamentally changes mage gameplay — you are not a "burst and wait" caster, you are a sustained damage dealer. Get Willpower to 100 as fast as possible.

All 7 Schools = An Answer for Everything

This build's greatest strength is versatility. Locked door? Alteration. Enemy group? Destruction AoE + Conjuration tank. Enemy mage? Shock drain + Silence. Need to escape? Illusion Invisibility. Need health? Restoration. Need souls for enchanting? Mysticism Soul Trap. Need potions? Alchemy. No situation in the game is unwinnable for a fully leveled Archmage.

Alchemy is Your Magicka Battery

Even with excellent Willpower regen, long dungeon crawls will drain you. Alchemy solves this completely. Collect ingredients everywhere you go. Combine any two ingredients that share "Restore Magicka" to create potions. Common sources: Flax Seeds + Dragon's Tongue, Bergamot Seeds + Steel-Blue Entoloma. Buy a Mortar and Pestle from any alchemist shop. 10 potions = 10 full Magicka refills = an entire dungeon cleared without running dry.

Level Scaling — Stay Ahead

With all 7 major skills being magic schools, you level up fast. Enemies scale with your level. If your Destruction damage doesn't keep up with enemy HP, you'll feel it. Upgrade your damage spells at the Spellmaking Altar every 5-10 levels. A Fireball that one-shot bandits at level 5 tickles Daedra at level 20. Craft stronger Weakness combos to stay ahead of the curve. The exponential Weakness stack is your answer to scaling — it grows faster than enemy HP.

Don't Steal from the Mages Guild

If you're caught stealing or attacking Mages Guild members, you get expelled and lose access to spell vendors and the Spellmaking Altar. For a pure mage, this is catastrophic. Steal from everyone else — just never the Mages Guild.

Sleep to Level Up

You don't level up automatically. When the game says "you should rest and meditate," sleep in any bed to trigger the level-up screen and spend your Virtues. Any bed works — inns, your house, dungeon bedrolls.

Frostcrag Spire is Your Home Base

The Frostcrag Spire DLC (included in Remastered) gives you a personal tower with a Spellmaking Altar, Enchanting Altar, and Alchemy equipment — all in one location. Activate it with Magetallow Candles from Aurelinwae in the Imperial City. This is the perfect mage home base. No need to travel to the Arcane University every time you want to craft a spell.

Welkynd Stones — Free Full Magicka

Welkynd Stones are glowing blue crystals found in Ayleid Ruins. Activating one fully restores your Magicka. Collect them — they're single-use but incredibly valuable during long dungeons. There are hundreds of them across the game. If you're using The Atronach birthsign, these are your primary sustain mechanic. Even with The Mage, they're a welcome emergency refill.
